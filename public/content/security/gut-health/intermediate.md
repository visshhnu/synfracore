# Gut Health Intermediate

## Gut-Brain Axis

### The Enteric Nervous System
500 million neurons in the gut wall — more than the spinal cord. Can function independently of the central brain. Known as the "second brain" or enteric nervous system (ENS).

Bidirectional communication via:
- Vagus nerve (80% of signals travel GUT → BRAIN, not the reverse)
- Hormones: GLP-1, ghrelin, CCK, PYY
- Immune signals: cytokines, interleukins
- Microbial metabolites: SCFAs, tryptophan metabolites, GABA precursors

### Gut-Mental Health Link
- 90% of serotonin is produced and stored in the gut
- Gut bacteria produce GABA, dopamine precursors, and other neuroactive compounds
- Chronic gut inflammation linked to depression and anxiety through inflammatory cytokines
- Stress (HPA axis activation) alters gut motility, increases permeability, and shifts microbiome composition
- Bidirectional: anxiety causes gut symptoms (IBS); gut dysbiosis worsens mood

## Prebiotics and Probiotics

### Probiotics — Evidence Summary
Live beneficial bacteria administered orally. Most studied genera: Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Saccharomyces.
| Condition | Evidence Level |
|---------|---------------|
| Antibiotic-associated diarrhoea | Strong (Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG) |
| IBS symptom relief | Moderate |
| Prevention of C. difficile infection | Moderate |
| Vaginal health | Moderate |
| General immune support | Limited but positive |

Choose strain-specific products for specific conditions — generic "probiotic" claims mean little.

### Prebiotics — Key Sources
FOS (fructooligosaccharides): garlic, onion, asparagus, banana
Inulin: chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, leeks, dandelion root
GOS (galactooligosaccharides): legumes, lentils, chickpeas
Resistant starch: cooked and cooled rice/potato, green banana, oats

### Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs)
Produced when gut bacteria ferment soluble fibre:
- Butyrate: primary fuel for colonocytes; anti-inflammatory; reduces colon cancer risk; strengthens gut barrier
- Propionate: transported to liver; modulates glucose/fat metabolism; reduces appetite
- Acetate: energy for muscle and brain; appetite regulation; most abundant SCFA

## FODMAP Diet for IBS

### What Are FODMAPs?
Fermentable Oligo-, Di-, Mono-saccharides And Polyols — short-chain poorly absorbed carbohydrates. Rapidly fermented by gut bacteria producing gas and osmotic shift, causing: bloating, gas, cramping, diarrhoea or constipation in susceptible individuals.

### High FODMAP Foods (Reduce in IBS)
Fructans: wheat, rye, garlic, onion, leek
Lactose: milk, soft cheese, ice cream, yogurt (in large amounts)
Excess fructose: apple, pear, mango, honey, high-fructose corn syrup
Polyols: stone fruits (peach, plum, cherry), mushrooms, sorbitol/mannitol sweeteners
GOS: chickpeas, lentils, kidney beans (large amounts)

### Low FODMAP Alternatives
Grains: rice, oats (moderate), quinoa, gluten-free products
Vegetables: carrot, cucumber, zucchini, spinach, tomato, bell pepper
Fruits: strawberry, orange, kiwi, grape, blueberry, cantaloupe
Dairy: lactose-free milk, hard cheese (cheddar, parmesan), butter
Protein: eggs, firm tofu, meat, fish
