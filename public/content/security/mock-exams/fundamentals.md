# Mock Exams — Overview and Strategy

## Purpose of Mock Tests
- Simulate actual exam conditions
- Identify weak areas
- Build time management skills
- Reduce exam anxiety
- Track progress over time

## Types of Mock Tests
- **Full-length mocks**: Complete simulation (same duration, question count, marking)
- **Subject-specific tests**: Deep dive into one subject area
- **Topic-wise tests**: Focus on specific chapters or topics
- **Previous year papers**: Actual exam questions from past years
- **Sectional tests**: Practice individual sections of the exam

## Exam Taking Strategy

### Before the Exam
- Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
- Eat a good meal; stay hydrated
- Carry all necessary documents (admit card, ID)
- Arrive early to avoid last-minute stress
- Brief review of important formulas/facts

### During the Exam

**Time Management:**
- Calculate time per question: total time / number of questions
- Don't spend more than 2x average time on any single question
- Mark difficult questions, skip and return
- Keep 5-10 minutes at end for review

**Question Approach:**
- Read the entire question before attempting
- Eliminate obviously wrong options (eliminate and choose)
- Watch for qualifiers: "always", "never", "only", "except"
- Negative questions: "which of the following is NOT..."

**Mental Approach:**
- If stuck, move on — don't let one question ruin your time
- Trust your first instinct unless you have strong reason to change
- Stay calm; anxiety hurts performance

### After Each Mock Test
- Review every question (right AND wrong)
- Understand WHY you got wrong answers
- Identify: careless mistake, conceptual gap, or time pressure
- Track accuracy by topic
