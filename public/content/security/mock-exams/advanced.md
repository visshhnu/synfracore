# Mock Exams — Advanced Strategies

## High-Performance Test Taking

### Psychological Preparation
- Normalize test anxiety: moderate arousal improves performance (Yerkes-Dodson curve)
- Pre-exam routine: same breakfast, travel, warm-up creates consistency
- Visualization: mentally rehearse entering exam, reading questions calmly
- Growth mindset: mistakes in mocks are learning, not failure
- Self-efficacy: build confidence through preparation, not just positive thinking

### Advanced Time Management — Triage Method
**Pass 1** (first 40% of time): Mark all easy questions first — build confidence, secure easy marks
**Pass 2** (next 40% of time): Attempt medium-difficulty questions
**Pass 3** (final 20%): Hard questions; if stuck after 2 min, eliminate worst options and guess
**Reserve**: 5-10 min at end for review and filling unmarked answers

### Decision Making Under Uncertainty
When genuinely unsure between two options:
- Eliminate obviously wrong options first (usually 2 of 4)
- Look for qualifier words: "always/never" tend to be wrong, "usually/often" tend to be right
- Longer, more specific answer is often correct (more hedging)
- Your first instinct after elimination is usually right — change only with clear reason
- In negative marking: attempt only if you can eliminate at least 2 options

## Competitive Analysis

### Percentile vs Raw Score
Most exams rank on percentile — rank matters, not absolute score.
- GATE 2024: score 50/100 → approximately 65th percentile
- JEE Main: 150/300 → percentile varies by shift difficulty
- SSC CGL: final selection based on merit list (percentile)
Research previous year cutoffs and scores-to-percentile conversion for your target.

### Shift Normalization (GATE, JEE Main)
Multi-shift exams normalize scores across shifts.
Actual score → normalized percentile score.
Don't panic if your paper felt harder — normalization accounts for this.

## Exam Day Execution

### Checklist — Day Before
- Pack admit card + original ID (government-issued)
- Confirm exam centre address and travel route
- Set 2 alarm clocks
- Light dinner; avoid heavy or new foods
- Review 1-page formula sheet only (no new material)
- Sleep by 10 PM for a morning exam

### Checklist — Exam Morning
- Wake up with 90+ minutes before departure
- Light familiar breakfast; avoid heavy/greasy food
- Quick 10-min review of formulas/mnemonics
- Arrive at centre 30+ minutes before reporting time
- Phone off; deposit valuables as required
- Deep breath on entering hall — own the space

### Psychological Recovery Mid-Exam
If you realize you've spent too much time on a section:
1. Stop. Mark remaining and move on immediately.
2. Take 3 deep breaths (activates parasympathetic system)
3. Recalculate: remaining time ÷ remaining questions = time per question
4. Execute with the new pace — don't try to "catch up" by rushing everything
