# Home Health Coding Intermediate

## PDGM (Patient-Driven Groupings Model)

### Overview
CMS payment model for home health effective January 1, 2020. Replaced OASIS-based HIPPS with clinical groupings.

### 30-Day Payment Periods
- Replaced 60-day episodes with two 30-day payment periods
- Each period classified independently
- Admission source: community vs institutional

### Clinical Groupings
12 broad clinical groups based on primary diagnosis:
1. Musculoskeletal rehab
2. Neuro rehab
3. Wound care
4. Complex nursing interventions
5. Behavioral health
6. Medication management/teaching/assessment
7. Cardiac/circulatory
8. Endocrine
9. GI/GU
10. Respiratory
11. Infectious disease/neoplasms/blood disorders
12. Immobility

### Comorbidity Adjustment
PDGM looks at secondary diagnoses for comorbidity adjustments:
- None: base payment
- Low: moderate increase
- High: higher increase

## Coding for Common Home Health Conditions

### CHF (Congestive Heart Failure)
- I50.x (heart failure type)
- Visit teaching: medication management, sodium restriction, fluid monitoring
- Signs triggering notification: weight gain, edema, dyspnea

### Diabetes with Wound
- E11.621: Type 2 DM with foot ulcer
- L97.x: Non-pressure chronic ulcer (add site)
- Z89 if amputation history
- Skills: wound care, glucose monitoring teaching, insulin administration

### Orthopedic (Post-hip or knee replacement)
- Z96.641 or similar for presence of prosthetic joint
- S72.xx1D for fracture follow-up (subsequent encounter)
- PT for gait training, functional mobility

### Wound Care
- L89.x: Pressure ulcers (stage 1-4, unstageable)
- L97.x: Non-pressure chronic ulcers
- T81.x: Post-procedural wound complications
- Document: location, size, depth, drainage, surrounding tissue

## Documentation Requirements

### Skilled Care Documentation
Every visit note must support:
- Skilled need for this visit
- Services provided
- Patient response to treatment
- Progress toward goals
- Ongoing skilled need

### Homebound Status Documentation
- Distance patient can travel
- Effort required
- Reason leaving home is significant burden
- Recent or planned absence from home (shopping, doctor visits — brief absences allowed)
