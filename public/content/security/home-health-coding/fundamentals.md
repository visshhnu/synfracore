# Home Health Coding Fundamentals

## Medicare Home Health Coverage
Medicare covers skilled home health services provided in a patient's place of residence. Covered services: skilled nursing (SN), physical therapy (PT), occupational therapy (OT), speech-language pathology (SLP), medical social work, and home health aide services (only when skilled service is also being provided simultaneously).

## The Four Medicare Coverage Requirements
ALL four criteria must be met simultaneously for Medicare to cover home health:
1. **Homebound**: Leaving home requires considerable effort due to illness or injury. Brief absences are permitted (medical appointments, religious services, adult day care).
2. **Skilled service needed**: Must require SN, PT, OT, or SLP services. SN is most common trigger.
3. **Under physician's care**: Physician establishes and certifies plan of care (CMS Form 485) every 60 days.
4. **Reasonable and necessary**: Services appropriate for the patient's clinical condition and therapeutic goals.

## Who Can Perform OASIS?
| Clinician | OASIS Authorized |
|-----------|-----------------|
| SN — RN/LPN (Skilled Nurse) | YES |
| PT (Physical Therapist) | YES |
| SLP / ST (Speech-Language Pathologist) | YES |
| OT (Occupational Therapist) | NO — cannot fill OASIS independently |

## OASIS Assessment Types and Timing
| Assessment | Trigger | Required Timing |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| SOC (Start of Care) | First skilled visit | Within 5 calendar days |
| ROC (Resumption of Care) | Returns from inpatient stay | Within 24 hours of return |
| REC (Recertification) | Extending episode at 60 days | Near end of episode |
| SCIC (Significant Change in Condition) | Acute change, no hospitalisation | Within the episode |
| DC (Discharge) | Last visit | At time of discharge visit |

## Primary Diagnosis Selection Rules
- Must DIRECTLY relate to the reason for skilled services
- Must SUPPORT the homebound status
- Must be the condition being ACTIVELY TREATED by the skilled service
- NOT necessarily the patient's most severe diagnosis
- WRONG primary diagnosis → wrong PDGM clinical group → wrong payment

## PDGM — Patient-Driven Groupings Model
Replaced PPS in January 2020. Payment per 30-day period based on five factors:
1. Timing: Early (1st 30-day period) or Late (subsequent periods)
2. Admission source: Community (higher payment) or Institutional (post-hospital)
3. Clinical grouping: 12 groups based on primary ICD-10-CM diagnosis
4. Functional level: Low/Medium/High based on OASIS functional items
5. Comorbidity adjustment: None/Low/High based on secondary diagnoses

## Plan of Care — CMS Form 485
- Physician-certified plan required every 60 days
- Contents: all diagnoses, medications, functional limitations, safety measures, goals, visit frequency and duration
- Physician must sign; cannot be backdated
- Without valid, current 485: services are not billable to Medicare

**Q: What is the Low Utilization Payment Adjustment (LUPA) in PDGM?**
LUPA: when the number of home health visits in a 30-day payment period falls below the minimum threshold for that clinical group, payment switches from the standard per-period rate to a per-visit rate. LUPA thresholds range from 2-6 visits depending on the clinical group. LUPA payments are significantly lower than standard period payments. To avoid LUPA: ensure minimum visit thresholds are met; schedule visits appropriately; document medical necessity for all visits. Some clinical groups have very low thresholds (2 visits) while others (wound care) have higher thresholds (6 visits).
