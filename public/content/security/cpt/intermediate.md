# CPT Intermediate

## Surgical Section

### Integumentary System (10004-19499)
- Excisions: measured by excised diameter + margins
- Repair: simple, intermediate, complex — by total length
- Skin grafts: type (split-thickness, full-thickness), size (sq cm)
- Wound care: debridement coded by depth and surface area

### Musculoskeletal (20005-29999)
- Fracture care: open vs closed treatment, with/without manipulation
- Arthroscopy: diagnostic vs surgical
- Joint replacement: primary vs revision
- Tendon/ligament repairs coded by specific structure and approach

### Cardiovascular (33010-37799)
- Cardiac catheterization: diagnostic vs interventional
- Coronary artery bypass: number of vessels, arterial vs venous
- Pacemaker implantation: single chamber, dual chamber, leadless

## Radiology Section

### Imaging Types
- X-ray: views (AP, lateral, oblique)
- CT: with/without contrast, body area
- MRI: with/without contrast, body area, sequences
- Ultrasound: real-time vs limited, with/without Doppler
- Nuclear medicine: scan type, radiopharmaceutical

### Professional vs Technical Component
- Modifier -26: Professional component (physician interpretation)
- Modifier -TC: Technical component (equipment and staff)
- Global code: both components together

## E/M Coding — Advanced

### Hospital E/M Codes
- Initial hospital care: 99221-99223
- Subsequent hospital care: 99231-99233
- Hospital discharge: 99238-99239

### Critical Care
- 99291: First 30-74 minutes of critical care
- 99292: Each additional 30 minutes
- Patient must be critically ill; physician providing high complexity MDM

### Consultations (Inpatient)
- 99251-99255 (some payers no longer accept; use regular E/M)
- Request, reason, opinion, plan documented
