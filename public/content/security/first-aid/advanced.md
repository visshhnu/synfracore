# First Aid Advanced Topics

## Cardiac Emergencies

### Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
Warning signs: Chest pain/pressure/tightness, radiating to arm/jaw/back, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, lightheadedness. Women may have atypical symptoms (fatigue, indigestion, jaw pain).

First aid:
1. Call 112 immediately
2. Have patient rest in comfortable position (semi-reclined if breathing difficulty)
3. Chew aspirin 300mg (if not allergic, no contraindications)
4. Loosen tight clothing
5. Be prepared for CPR
6. Do NOT let patient drive themselves

### Using an AED (Automated External Defibrillator)
1. Power on (most talk you through)
2. Attach pads as shown (upper right chest, lower left)
3. Clear patient while AED analyzes
4. Deliver shock if advised (ensure everyone clear)
5. Resume CPR immediately after shock; reanalyze every 2 minutes
AEDs are safe — will not shock unless shockable rhythm detected.

## Environmental Emergencies

### Heat Stroke vs Heat Exhaustion
| Feature | Heat Exhaustion | Heat Stroke |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Sweating | Heavy | May stop |
| Skin | Pale, cool | Red, hot |
| Temperature | Normal or slightly elevated | >40°C |
| Mental status | Alert, dizzy | Confused, unconscious |
| Danger | Moderate | LIFE-THREATENING |

Heat stroke first aid: cool rapidly (immersion in cool water, ice packs to neck/armpits/groin, wet sheet + fanning); call 112; this is an emergency.

### Hypothermia
- Core temperature <35°C
- Mild: shivering, poor coordination; Severe: no shivering, confusion, loss of consciousness
- Remove wet clothing, insulate from ground, add layers
- Warm gradually — never immerse in hot water
- Warm drinks if conscious (not alcohol)
- Severe: hospital warming protocols needed

### Drowning
- Remove from water safely (if possible without endangering yourself)
- Begin CPR immediately if not breathing (chest compressions + rescue breaths)
- Call 112; all drowning victims need medical evaluation even if conscious

## Documentation and Handover

### SBAR Communication (Handing Over to EMS)
- **S**ituation: What is happening?
- **B**ackground: Relevant history, allergies, medications
- **A**ssessment: What do you think is wrong?
- **R**ecommendation: What does the patient need?

### First Aid Documentation
Record: time of incident, observations, treatments given, vital signs, response to treatment. Valuable for EMS and hospital care.

## Psychological First Aid
- Safety: ensure person is safe, meets immediate needs
- Calm: help reduce overwhelming stress reactions
- Connect: connect to social supports
- Self-efficacy: help person help themselves
- Hope: reinforce realistic positive expectations
