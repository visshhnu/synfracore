# First Aid Fundamentals

## What is First Aid?
Immediate care given to an injured or ill person before professional medical help arrives. Proper first aid can save lives, prevent conditions from worsening, and reduce recovery time.

## Primary Survey (ABCDE)

### A — Airway
- Ensure airway is open and clear
- Tilt head back, lift chin (head-tilt chin-lift) if no suspected spinal injury
- Look, listen, feel for breathing
- Remove visible obstructions

### B — Breathing
- Check for normal breathing (not gasping)
- If not breathing normally → start CPR
- Rescue breaths if trained and willing

### C — Circulation
- Check for severe bleeding → control with direct pressure
- Signs of circulation: color, warmth, capillary refill
- CPR if no pulse and not breathing

### D — Disability (Neurological)
- AVPU scale: Alert, responds to Voice, responds to Pain, Unresponsive
- Check pupils for equality and reaction to light
- Monitor level of consciousness

### E — Exposure/Environment
- Expose injury for assessment
- Prevent hypothermia (cover patient)
- Look for additional injuries

## Emergency Action Steps
1. **Ensure scene safety**: Don't become another victim
2. **Call for help**: 112 (India emergency), 102 (ambulance), 101 (fire)
3. **Provide first aid**: What you know, until help arrives
4. **Stay with patient**: Monitor and reassure until handover

## CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

### Adult CPR
1. Call 112; get AED if available
2. Position: flat on back, hard surface
3. Chest compressions: center of chest; heel of hand; 2 inches deep; 100-120/min
4. Rescue breaths (if trained): 30 compressions : 2 breaths
5. Continue until: AED available, person breathes normally, EMS arrives, too exhausted

### Hands-Only CPR
- If untrained or uncomfortable with rescue breaths
- Continuous chest compressions at 100-120/min
- More people will do it; evidence shows comparable outcomes for witnessed cardiac arrest

## Choking

### Adult/Child (over 1 year)
- Mild choking (can cough): encourage coughing
- Severe choking (cannot breathe/speak/cough):
  1. 5 back blows (between shoulder blades with heel of hand)
  2. 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver): stand behind, fist above navel, thrust upward
  3. Alternate until object expelled or unconscious
  4. If unconscious: CPR (each time you open airway, look for object)

### Infant (under 1 year)
- NEVER do Heimlich on infant — rupture internal organs
- 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts (not abdominal)
