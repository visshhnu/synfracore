# First Aid Intermediate

## Bleeding Control

### Direct Pressure
- Apply firm, continuous pressure with clean cloth/bandage
- Don't remove cloth (add more on top if soaked)
- Elevate limb above heart level if possible
- 10-15 minutes sustained pressure for most wounds

### Tourniquets
- Life-threatening limb bleeding not controlled by pressure
- Place 2-3 inches above wound (not on joint)
- Tighten until bleeding stops
- Note time applied — critical info for medical team
- Do NOT remove once applied — hospital procedure

### Wound Care (Minor)
1. Clean hands before touching wound
2. Rinse with clean running water 5-10 minutes
3. Remove visible debris (not embedded objects)
4. Apply antiseptic (optional; wound irrigation is more important)
5. Cover with sterile dressing
6. Seek care if: deep, won't stop bleeding, animal bite, dirty wound, tetanus status unknown

## Burns

### Classification
- 1st degree: Superficial; redness, pain; no blisters (sunburn)
- 2nd degree: Partial thickness; blisters, severe pain, wet appearance
- 3rd degree: Full thickness; dry, leathery, may be painless (nerve damage)

### Burn First Aid
1. Cool burn: cool (not cold) running water 10-20 minutes
2. Do NOT use: ice, butter, toothpaste, oil
3. Cover with non-adherent dressing
4. Do NOT burst blisters
5. Seek medical care: >2.5cm blisters, face/hands/genitals, deep burns, electrical/chemical burns, smoke inhalation

## Fractures and Sprains

### Signs of Fracture
- Pain (worsens with movement), swelling, deformity, crepitus (grinding), bruising, inability to bear weight

### Fracture First Aid
- RICE: Rest, Ice (20 min on, 20 min off), Compression, Elevation
- Immobilize (splint in position found — don't straighten deformed limb)
- Seek X-ray for suspected fractures
- Open fracture: cover with sterile dressing; do not push bone back; urgent medical care

### Sprain vs Strain
- Sprain: ligament injury (joint) — ankle, knee, wrist
- Strain: muscle/tendon injury — hamstring, calf, back
- Both: RICE initially; if severe, seek medical evaluation

## Loss of Consciousness

### Fainting (Vasovagal Syncope)
- Lay person flat; elevate legs 30cm
- Ensure airway open; do not give water until fully conscious
- Recovery position if vomiting
- Seek care if: first episode, prolonged, injury from fall, no clear cause

### Seizure First Aid
- Protect from injury (cushion head, clear area)
- DO NOT restrain, hold down, or put anything in mouth
- Time the seizure
- Recovery position when movements stop
- Call 112: seizure >5 min, no return to consciousness, injury, first seizure, pregnant

## Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction)

### Recognition
- Sudden onset after allergen exposure
- Skin: urticaria (hives), angioedema, flushing
- Respiratory: throat tightening, wheeze, shortness of breath
- Cardiovascular: rapid pulse, low blood pressure, dizziness
- GI: nausea, vomiting

### Treatment
1. Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen): thigh, outer aspect; first-line treatment
2. Call 112 immediately
3. Lie flat with legs raised (or sitting if breathing difficulty)
4. Second dose epinephrine after 5-15 min if no improvement
5. Hospital observation minimum 4-6 hours (biphasic reaction risk)
