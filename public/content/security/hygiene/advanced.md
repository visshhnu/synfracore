# Personal Hygiene Advanced Topics

## Environmental Hygiene

### Home Cleaning Priority Guide
| Surface/Item | Frequency |
|-------------|----------|
| Kitchen counter, sink, stovetop | Daily |
| Toilet handle, light switches, door handles | Daily |
| Phone/tablet screen | Daily to weekly |
| Bathroom sink | Every 2-3 days |
| Toilet bowl | Every 2-3 days |
| Shower/bathtub | Weekly |
| Floors | Weekly |
| Bed sheets | Weekly |
| Refrigerator handles | Weekly |
| Refrigerator interior | Monthly |

### Kitchen Food Safety
- Two-board rule: separate cutting boards for raw meat vs ready-to-eat foods
- Refrigerator ≤4°C; Freezer ≤-18°C
- 2-hour rule: don't leave perishables at room temperature >2 hours (1 hour if >32°C)
- Thaw safely: in refrigerator, cold water, or microwave — never on countertop
- Hand wash: before and after handling raw meat, before eating, after toilet

### Laundry Hygiene
- Underwear, socks, gym clothes: wash at 60°C+ to kill bacteria and fungi
- Bed sheets: weekly; wash at 60°C to kill dust mites
- Dry promptly: damp clothes grow mould and bacteria within hours
- Clean washing machine drum monthly: hot cycle + vinegar or machine cleaner

## Digital Device Hygiene

### Smartphone Cleaning
Smartphones carry more bacteria than most toilet seats (studies show 10× more bacteria than a toilet handle).
Weekly cleaning routine:
- Power off; remove case
- Wipe screen and back with 70% isopropyl alcohol wipe (safe for most glass)
- Clean case separately with soap and water (silicone) or alcohol wipe (hard plastic)
- Let dry completely before reassembly
- Clean between case and phone body with cotton swab

### Other Devices
- Keyboard: compressed air for debris weekly; alcohol wipe for surfaces
- Earphone tips: wipe with alcohol after use; replace foam tips monthly
- Glasses: clean lenses and nose pads daily with lens cloth or soap and water (most touched facial accessory)

## Travel Hygiene

### High-Risk Travel Precautions
- Hand sanitiser (≥60% alcohol): carry accessible at all times in transit
- "Boil it, cook it, peel it, or forget it" — food safety mantra in developing countries
- Bottled or boiled water where tap water safety is uncertain; avoid ice in drinks
- Pre-travel vaccinations: hepatitis A, typhoid, yellow fever, Japanese encephalitis per destination
- Traveller's diarrhoea: most common travel illness; oral rehydration salts + probiotics

### Airline and Public Transport
High-touch surfaces rarely sanitised between flights: tray table, armrests, headrest, seatbelt buckle, window shade.
Actions: sanitise hands after touching these; use tissue for lavatory flush and door handles; wear face mask if immunocompromised or during peak illness seasons.

## Workplace Hygiene

### Office Best Practices
- Personal desk: wipe surfaces and keyboard weekly; don't eat at desk or clean up immediately
- Shared equipment: wipe before and after use (printer touchscreen, photocopier)
- Kitchen area: label personal food; refrigerator audit weekly; clean up immediately after use
- Sick leave: stay home — presenteeism spreads illness and reduces total team productivity
- One personal mug and utensils: avoid using shared ones without washing

### Sick Day Decision Making
Stay home if: fever (any temperature above 37.8°C), productive cough, active diarrhoea or vomiting, symptoms within first 2-3 days (peak contagiousness for most respiratory infections). Return when: fever-free for 24 hours without fever-reducing medication, symptoms improving.
