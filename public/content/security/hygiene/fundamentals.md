# Personal Hygiene Fundamentals

## What is Personal Hygiene?
Practices that maintain cleanliness and health of the body, preventing infection, disease, and social discomfort. Good hygiene protects both yourself and others.

## Hand Hygiene — The Most Important

### Why Hand Hygiene Matters
Hands are the primary route of disease transmission. Proper handwashing prevents:
- Respiratory infections (flu, COVID-19, RSV)
- Gastrointestinal infections (norovirus, salmonella)
- Eye infections (conjunctivitis)
- Skin infections

### WHO 5 Moments for Hand Hygiene
1. Before touching a patient/food
2. Before clean/aseptic procedures
3. After body fluid exposure risk
4. After touching a patient
5. After touching patient surroundings

### Proper Handwashing Technique
1. Wet hands with clean water
2. Apply soap
3. Lather and scrub for 20 seconds (sing Happy Birthday twice)
4. Clean all surfaces: palms, backs, between fingers, thumbs, fingernails, wrists
5. Rinse thoroughly under clean water
6. Dry with clean towel or air dry

### Hand Sanitizer
60%+ alcohol kills most germs. Use when soap and water unavailable. Not effective for all pathogens (C. diff, norovirus). Cannot replace handwashing when visibly dirty.

## Oral Hygiene

### Tooth Brushing
- Brush twice daily (morning + before bed) for 2 minutes
- Soft-bristled brush at 45° angle to gums
- Use fluoride toothpaste
- Replace toothbrush every 3 months

### Flossing
- Once daily; removes plaque between teeth where brush can't reach
- Proper technique: curve floss around each tooth, gentle sawing motion
- Alternatives: interdental brushes, water flosser

### Additional Oral Care
- Mouthwash: antimicrobial (chlorhexidine), fluoride, or cosmetic
- Tongue cleaning: reduces bad breath
- Dentist visit: every 6 months for cleaning and checkup

## Body Hygiene

### Bathing
- Daily bathing removes sweat, bacteria, dead skin cells, pollutants
- Focus on: armpits, groin, feet (between toes), face, neck
- Soap type: mild, pH-balanced for skin
- Water temperature: warm (not hot — hot water strips skin oils)

### Hair Hygiene
- Wash frequency depends on hair type: oily (daily), normal (every 2-3 days), dry/curly (less frequent)
- Scalp health: dandruff (antifungal shampoo), seborrheic dermatitis
- Clean hairbrushes weekly

## Clothing and Linens
- Change underwear daily; socks daily
- Wash clothes after each wear (especially workout clothes, underwear)
- Wash bed sheets weekly; pillowcases twice weekly
- Towels: wash after 3-4 uses; avoid sharing
