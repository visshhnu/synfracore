# Personal Hygiene Intermediate

## Skin Health and Care

### Understanding Skin Structure
Three layers:
- Epidermis: outer barrier; dead skin cells shed constantly (skin cell turnover ~28 days)
- Dermis: collagen/elastin fibres, hair follicles, sweat glands, sebaceous glands, nerve endings
- Hypodermis: fat layer; insulation, cushioning, anchors skin to underlying tissue

### Skin Care Routine
Morning: Gentle cleanser → Vitamin C serum (optional antioxidant) → Moisturiser → SPF 30+ (non-negotiable)
Evening: Double cleanse if wearing sunscreen/makeup → Active treatment (retinol, AHA, BHA) → Moisturiser

### Acne Prevention
- Wash face twice daily max — more dries out skin, triggers excess oil production
- Non-comedogenic products only (labelled "won't clog pores")
- Change pillowcase twice weekly; avoid resting hands on face
- Wash makeup brushes and sponges weekly
- Never pop pimples — introduces bacteria, causes scarring, prolongs healing

## Hair and Scalp Health

### Washing Frequency by Hair Type
| Hair Type | Frequency |
|-----------|----------|
| Oily / Fine | Daily or every other day |
| Normal / Medium | Every 2-3 days |
| Dry / Thick / Curly | 1-2 times per week |
| Chemically treated / Coloured | 2 times per week maximum |

### Common Scalp Conditions
Dandruff: Caused by Malassezia fungus or dry skin. Treatment: zinc pyrithione (Head & Shoulders) or ketoconazole (Nizoral) shampoo; leave on 3 minutes before rinsing.
Seborrhoeic dermatitis: More severe; greasy yellowish flakes, redness, itching. Treatment: antifungal + medicated shampoos; may require prescription treatment.
Scalp psoriasis: Thick silvery scales; often extends to hairline; requires dermatologist treatment (topical corticosteroids, tar shampoos).

## Intimate Hygiene

### Evidence-Based Approach
- External cleansing only: mild, unscented soap and lukewarm water for outer area
- Internal douching: NOT recommended — disrupts natural pH (3.8-4.5) and Lactobacillus-dominant flora; INCREASES infection risk (bacterial vaginosis, yeast infection)
- Clothing: cotton underwear; change promptly after exercise; avoid prolonged tight synthetic clothing

### UTI Prevention
- Stay well hydrated (dilutes bacteria; frequent urination flushes urethra)
- Urinate promptly after sexual activity
- Wipe front to back always
- Avoid harsh soaps/products near the urethra
- Cranberry: weak evidence; staying hydrated is more effective

## Respiratory Hygiene

### Cough Etiquette
1. Turn away from people
2. Cover with bent elbow (not hand — avoids contaminating hands)
3. Use tissue: one-use only → dispose immediately → wash/sanitise hands
4. Wear surgical mask if unwell but must be in public
5. Stay home during acute infectious respiratory illness

### Nasal Hygiene
- Blow nose gently, one nostril at a time (prevents pressure building in sinuses and eustachian tubes)
- Saline nasal spray/rinse: hydrates mucosa, flushes allergens and pathogens; effective for rhinitis and sinusitis
- Neti pot: effective sinus rinse; use distilled or boiled water only (NOT tap water — risk of Naegleria fowleri infection)
