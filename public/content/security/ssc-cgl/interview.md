# SSC CGL Interview Q&A

## Quantitative Aptitude

**Q: Shopkeeper marks goods 40% above CP and gives 20% discount. Find profit%?**
SP = CP × 1.4 × 0.8 = 1.12 × CP. Profit% = 12%.

**Q: If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, find A:B:C.**
A:B = 2:3 = 8:12. B:C = 4:5 = 12:15. A:B:C = 8:12:15.

**Q: Train 200m long crosses 400m platform in 30 seconds. Speed in km/h?**
Total distance = 600m. Speed = 600/30 = 20 m/s. In km/h: 20 × 18/5 = 72 km/h.

**Q: A and B together complete work in 12 days. A alone takes 20 days. B alone?**
Rate: 1/B = 1/12 − 1/20 = (5−3)/60 = 2/60. B = 30 days.

**Q: If x² + 1/x² = 14, find x + 1/x.**
(x + 1/x)² = x² + 2 + 1/x² = 14 + 2 = 16. So x + 1/x = 4 (taking positive root).

**Q: Compound interest on ₹10,000 at 10% p.a. for 2 years?**
A = P(1 + r/100)ⁿ = 10,000 × (1.1)² = 10,000 × 1.21 = ₹12,100. CI = ₹2,100.

## Reasoning

**Q: Series: 3, 8, 15, 24, 35, ?**
Differences: 5, 7, 9, 11, 13. Next: 35 + 13 = 48.

**Q: In a class of 40, A ranks 8th from top. What is A's rank from bottom?**
Rank from bottom = 40 − 8 + 1 = 33.

## General Awareness

**Q: Which Article abolishes untouchability?**
Article 17 of the Indian Constitution abolishes untouchability and forbids its practice in any form. Any violation is made punishable under law.

**Q: What is the Preamble of the Indian Constitution?**
Declares India to be a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic. Secures: Justice (social, economic, political), Liberty (thought, expression, belief, faith, worship), Equality (status and opportunity), Fraternity (assuring dignity of the individual and unity/integrity of the nation). Adopted November 26, 1949.

**Q: Who appoints the Chief Election Commissioner?**
The President of India appoints the CEC on the advice of the Prime Minister. The CEC can only be removed through a process similar to that of a Supreme Court judge — by a resolution passed by both Houses of Parliament with a special majority. This ensures independence from the executive.

**Q: Minimum age to contest Lok Sabha elections?**
25 years for Lok Sabha and state Vidhan Sabha. 30 years for Rajya Sabha. 35 years for President. The candidate must also be a citizen of India and enrolled as a voter in any parliamentary constituency.

**Q: What is the PM-KISAN scheme?**
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi — direct income support of ₹6,000 per year (₹2,000 every 4 months) transferred directly to eligible small and marginal farmer families. Launched February 2019. Administered by Ministry of Agriculture. Aims to supplement income for procurement of agricultural inputs and household needs.

**Q: What is the Preamble of the Indian Constitution and its significance?**
The Preamble introduces the Constitution, declares India a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic. Secures Justice (social, economic, political), Liberty (thought, expression, belief, faith, worship), Equality (status and opportunity), and Fraternity (dignity of individual + national unity). Adopted November 26, 1949. Key legal status: Kesavananda Bharati case (1973) — Preamble is part of the Constitution and can be amended but its basic structure cannot be altered. "Socialist" and "Secular" added by 42nd Amendment (1976) during Emergency.

**Q: How many members does Parliament have and what are the two Houses?**
Lok Sabha (House of the People): 545 seats (543 elected from constituencies + 2 nominated by President representing Anglo-Indian community — though nomination provision was ended by 104th Amendment 2020). Maximum term: 5 years; can be dissolved earlier.
Rajya Sabha (Council of States): 250 members maximum (238 elected by state and UT legislative assemblies using single transferable vote + 12 nominated by President for expertise in art, science, literature, social service). Permanent body — cannot be dissolved; members serve 6-year terms with 1/3rd retiring every 2 years.

**Q: What is the eligibility for SSC CGL and what are the main posts?**
Eligibility: Indian citizen aged 18-32 years (age relaxation for SC/ST/OBC/PwD/Ex-servicemen). Educational qualification: bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognised university. Some posts require specific degrees or knowledge (accounts officer needs commerce background for preference).
Main posts: Assistant Audit Officer (Group B Gazetted), Assistant Accounts Officer, Income Tax Inspector, Central Excise Inspector, Preventive Officer (Customs), Assistant Section Officer (CSS, MEA, AFHQ), Sub-Inspector (CBI, NIA), Junior Statistical Officer, Statistical Investigator. Posts are classified into Groups B and C across various central government ministries and departments.

**Q: What is the selection process for SSC CGL in detail?**
Tier 1 (CBT): 100 questions, 60 minutes, 0.5 negative marking — qualifying but marks used for shortlisting to Tier 2. Sections: General Intelligence & Reasoning (25Q), General Awareness (25Q), Quantitative Aptitude (25Q), English Comprehension (25Q). Tier 2 (CBT): Paper I (Maths + Reasoning + English + GK) for all posts; Paper II (Finance/Economics) for AAO/AO; Paper III (Statistics) for JSO/Statistical Investigator. Tier 2 Paper I is the primary merit-determining exam. Computer Proficiency Test (CPT): for MEA and CSS posts requiring typing/data entry. Document Verification: verification of all certificates. No interview for most SSC CGL posts (interviews discontinued for Group C and D posts from 2016). Final selection based on Tier 2 performance + preference form for post and zone.

**Q: What is the Indian Evidence Act and why was it replaced?**
The Indian Evidence Act, 1872 governed the rules of evidence in civil and criminal proceedings — what can be proved, how, and by whom. It was replaced by the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (effective July 1, 2024) as part of the major reform of India's criminal law system (along with Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita replacing IPC, and Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita replacing CrPC). Key changes: explicit recognition of electronic records as primary evidence (not just secondary as before), updated provisions for admissibility of electronic evidence, clearer standards for documentary evidence, and language modernisation. The change was made to update colonial-era laws and align with the digital age.
