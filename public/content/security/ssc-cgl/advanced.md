# SSC CGL Advanced Preparation

## Tier 1 Section Strategy

### Optimal Section Order (Most Candidates)
1. Reasoning (12-14 min): most mechanical, highest accuracy potential, fast completion
2. GK (6-8 min): know it immediately or skip — never waste more than 30 seconds per question
3. English (10-12 min): RC requires careful reading but rewards it
4. Quant (18-20 min): most calculation-heavy, use full remaining time

### Accuracy Targets Per Section
- Reasoning: >90% (deterministic, rule-based)
- GK: >80% (you know it or you don't)
- English: >85% (strong grammar rules + vocabulary)
- Quant: >70% (highest careless error rate — slow down and verify)

## Advanced Quantitative Techniques

### Algebra Identities for Speed
```
If a + b + c = 0, then a³ + b³ + c³ = 3abc (very common in SSC)
(a+b)² − (a−b)² = 4ab → ab = [(a+b)² − (a−b)²] / 4
a² − b² = (a+b)(a−b)
a³ + b³ = (a+b)(a²−ab+b²)
If x + 1/x = k, then x² + 1/x² = k² − 2; x³ + 1/x³ = k³ − 3k
```

### Percentage Mental Math
- 1% of any number: move decimal 2 places left
- 5% = half of 10%
- 12.5% = 1/8 of the number
- 33.33% = 1/3; 66.67% = 2/3
- 1/6 ≈ 16.67%; 1/7 ≈ 14.29%; 1/9 ≈ 11.11%

### Geometry — High-Yield Theorems
- Angle in semicircle = 90° (Thales' theorem)
- Tangent from external point: both tangents equal length
- Tangent-radius angle = 90°
- Exterior angle of triangle = sum of two non-adjacent interior angles
- Centroid divides median 2:1 from vertex
- In right triangle: median to hypotenuse = half the hypotenuse

## High-Yield GK for SSC CGL

### Polity (5-8 questions typically)
Constitutional articles to memorise: Art 14 (equality), Art 19 (freedoms), Art 21 (life and liberty), Art 32 (constitutional remedies — Dr Ambedkar: heart and soul), Art 368 (amendment), Art 352/356/360 (emergencies).
Bodies: Election Commission (Art 324), CAG (Art 148), UPSC (Art 315), Finance Commission (Art 280).
Parliament: Lok Sabha (545 elected + 2 nominated), Rajya Sabha (250 total, 12 nominated). PM must be member of Parliament.

### Economy (4-6 questions)
Key RBI rates: Repo (6.5%), Reverse Repo (3.35%), CRR (4%), SLR (18%) — memorise current values.
Budget terms: fiscal deficit = total expenditure − total receipts excluding borrowings. Revenue deficit = revenue expenditure − revenue receipts. Primary deficit = fiscal deficit − interest payments.
GDP components: C (consumption) + I (investment) + G (government) + NX (net exports).

### Science (4-6 questions)
Newton's laws, Archimedes' principle, laws of thermodynamics, electromagnetic spectrum (radio → micro → IR → visible → UV → X → gamma).
Chemistry: pH scale, acids/bases, allotropes of carbon (diamond, graphite, fullerene).
Biology: cell organelles and functions, blood groups (ABO system), vitamins and deficiency diseases, classification of organisms.

**Q: What are the main posts filled through SSC CGL and which tier tests for them?**
All posts require clearing Tier 1 (CBT). Select posts also require Tier 2:
- AAO (Assistant Audit Officer), AO (Assistant Accounts Officer): Tier 1 + Tier 2 Paper I + Paper II (Finance/Accounts)
- Income Tax Inspector, Central Excise Inspector, Preventive Officer: Tier 1 + Tier 2 Paper I
- Sub-Inspector CBI: Tier 1 + Tier 2 + Physical + Medical
- Junior Statistical Officer: Tier 1 + Tier 2 Paper I + Paper III (Statistics)
- Statistical Investigator Gr.II: Tier 1 + Tier 2 Paper I + Paper III
All posts: Tier 1 is qualifying for final merit in most cases. Document verification follows merit-based allocation.
