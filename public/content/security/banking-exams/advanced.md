# Banking Exams — Advanced

## RBI Grade B Preparation

### Phase I (Objective)
- General Awareness: 80Q / 80 marks
- English Language: 30Q / 30 marks
- Quantitative Aptitude: 30Q / 30 marks
- Reasoning: 60Q / 60 marks

### Phase II (Descriptive)
**Paper 1 — Economic & Social Issues:**
- Indian economy fundamentals
- Monetary and fiscal policy
- Social sector schemes
- International trade and organizations

**Paper 2 — English (Writing Skills):**
- Essay (600 words)
- Précis writing
- Comprehension
- Business/official letter

**Paper 3 — Finance & Management:**
- Basics of Finance
- Financial institutions regulation
- Management principles
- Ethics in corporate governance

## High-Scoring Strategy for Mains

### Data Analysis & Interpretation
- Caselet problems: practice 5+ per day
- Line graphs + Bar charts combined: most common combo
- Target: 25/35 in this section for a good score

### Reasoning & Computer Aptitude
- Computer awareness: OS basics, MS Office, Internet, cybersecurity basics
- Puzzles: 4-5 puzzle sets likely; aim for 35+ out of 45
- Input-Output: learn word-shifting and digit-shifting patterns

### Essay Topics — Recent Trends
- Digital India initiatives
- Cryptocurrency and CBDC
- Green Banking and ESG
- Financial Inclusion
- Privatization of banks

## Time Management in Exam
```
Main Exam (3 hours):
- Spend first 5 min scanning all sections
- Start with your strongest section
- Don't spend > 3 min on any single question
- Keep 10 min buffer for review
- Mark doubtful questions and revisit

Target Attempt Rates:
Quant: 28–32/35
Reasoning: 35–40/45
English: 28–32/35
GA: 30–35/40
```

## Interview Preparation (for PO/SO)
- Know your region's banking infrastructure
- Read annual reports of the bank you're applying to
- Current economic situation (GDP, inflation, employment)
- RBI policies: latest monetary policy review
- Technology in banking: UPI, IMPS, NEFT, digital wallets
- Be ready to discuss your graduation subject in context
