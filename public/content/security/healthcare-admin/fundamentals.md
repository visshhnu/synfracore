# Healthcare Administration Fundamentals

## Healthcare System Overview
Healthcare administration involves managing healthcare organizations — hospitals, clinics, insurance companies, and government agencies.

## Key Organizations

### Providers
- Hospitals: inpatient and outpatient services
- Physician practices: primary care, specialists
- Long-term care: nursing homes, assisted living
- Home health agencies
- Behavioral health facilities

### Payers
- **Medicare**: Federal program for 65+ and disabled (CMS administers)
- **Medicaid**: Federal-state program for low-income
- **Commercial insurance**: Private payers (Aetna, UnitedHealth, BCBS)
- **Self-pay**: Patient pays directly

### Government Agencies
- **CMS** (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services): Medicare, Medicaid
- **HHS** (Department of Health and Human Services)
- **CDC**: Public health, disease prevention
- **FDA**: Drug and device approval
- **OIG**: Oversight of HHS programs, fraud and abuse

## Revenue Cycle Management (RCM)

### RCM Process
1. **Patient registration**: Demographics, insurance verification
2. **Charge capture**: Document services provided
3. **Coding**: ICD-10-CM, CPT, HCPCS code assignment
4. **Claims submission**: Electronic (ANSI 837) or paper (CMS-1500, UB-04)
5. **Payment posting**: Apply payments to accounts
6. **Denial management**: Appeal and resubmit denied claims
7. **Collections**: Follow up on outstanding balances

### Clean Claim
A claim that is complete, accurate, and payable without needing additional information.
Key elements: provider NPI, patient demographics, diagnosis codes, procedure codes, dates of service.

## HIPAA Overview

### Privacy Rule
- Protects individually identifiable health information (PHI)
- Patients have rights to access and amend their records
- Covered entities: healthcare providers, health plans, clearinghouses

### Security Rule
- Protects electronic PHI (ePHI)
- Administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
- Risk analysis and management required

### Transaction and Code Set Standards
- EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) standards
- 837P: Professional claim | 837I: Institutional claim | 835: Remittance

## Healthcare Quality

### Quality Measures
- **Structure**: Resources available (staffing ratios, facilities)
- **Process**: Care delivered (immunization rates, appropriate prescribing)
- **Outcome**: Patient results (mortality rates, readmissions)

### Accreditation Bodies
- **The Joint Commission (TJC)**: Hospital accreditation
- **NCQA**: Health plan accreditation
- **URAC**: Utilization management accreditation
