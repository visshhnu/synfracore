# Healthcare Administration Intermediate

## Medicare Billing

### Medicare Parts
- **Part A**: Hospital, SNF, hospice (inpatient focus)
- **Part B**: Physician services, outpatient, DME
- **Part C**: Medicare Advantage (private plans)
- **Part D**: Prescription drug coverage

### Medicare Claim Forms
- **CMS-1500**: Professional (physician/outpatient) claims
- **UB-04 (CMS-1450)**: Institutional (hospital/facility) claims

### Medicare Reimbursement Methods
- **IPPS**: Inpatient Prospective Payment System (DRGs)
- **OPPS**: Outpatient PPS (APCs)
- **RBRVS**: Resource-Based Relative Value Scale (physician fee schedule)
- **SNF PPS**: Skilled nursing facility payment

## DRG System

### How DRGs Work
- Diagnosis Related Groups: classifies inpatient stays into similar resource-use groups
- CMS uses MS-DRGs (Medicare Severity)
- Based on: principal diagnosis, secondary diagnoses, procedures, patient age, discharge status
- Flat rate payment per DRG (hospital gets same regardless of actual days/services)

### CC and MCC
- **CC**: Complication or Comorbidity (increases DRG weight moderately)
- **MCC**: Major Complication or Comorbidity (increases DRG weight significantly)
- Accurate coding of CCs/MCCs increases appropriate reimbursement

## Denial Management

### Common Claim Denials
| Reason | Solution |
|--------|---------|
| Missing/invalid NPI | Verify provider enrollment |
| Incorrect ICD-10/CPT codes | Correct and resubmit |
| No prior authorization | Obtain auth or submit appeal |
| Timely filing | Submit within payer timelines |
| Coordination of benefits (COB) | Update primary/secondary insurance |
| Medical necessity | Provide clinical documentation |

### Appeals Process
1. Internal appeal to payer
2. External appeal (independent review)
3. Administrative law judge (Medicare)
4. Federal court

## Healthcare Compliance

### Office of Inspector General (OIG)
- Publishes OIG Work Plan (audit priorities)
- Issues Advisory Opinions
- Compliance guidance for various healthcare settings

### False Claims Act (FCA)
- Prohibits knowingly submitting false claims to government
- Qui tam provisions: whistleblowers can file on behalf of government
- Penalties: 3× damages + fines per false claim

### Anti-Kickback Statute
- Prohibits offering/receiving remuneration to induce referrals
- Safe harbors: employment, group purchasing, personal services

## Healthcare Technology

### EHR (Electronic Health Records)
- Meaningful Use (now Promoting Interoperability)
- HL7 FHIR standard for data exchange
- HIPAA security requirements for ePHI

### Telehealth
- Expanded during COVID-19; permanent changes under consideration
- Medicare reimbursement for telehealth under Part B
- GT modifier (real-time interactive) or 95 (synchronous)
