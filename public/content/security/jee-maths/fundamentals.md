# JEE Mathematics Fundamentals

## JEE Exam Overview
JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) for IIT/NIT admissions. Mathematics is one of three subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Math). 25 questions, 100 marks in JEE Main.

## Algebra

### Sets, Relations and Functions
- Set operations: union, intersection, complement, Cartesian product
- Types of functions: injective, surjective, bijective
- Composition of functions, inverse functions
- Domain and range determination

### Complex Numbers
- z = a + ib, modulus |z| = √(a²+b²), argument θ = arctan(b/a)
- Polar form: z = r(cosθ + i sinθ) = re^(iθ)
- De Moivre's theorem: z^n = r^n (cos nθ + i sin nθ)
- nth roots of unity: e^(2πik/n), k = 0,1,...,n-1

### Quadratic Equations
- ax² + bx + c = 0: roots α,β where α+β = -b/a, αβ = c/a
- Discriminant D = b²-4ac: D>0 (real distinct), D=0 (equal), D<0 (complex)
- Nature of roots and graph of quadratic

### Sequences and Series
- AP: nth term = a+(n-1)d, Sum = n/2[2a+(n-1)d]
- GP: nth term = ar^(n-1), Sum = a(r^n-1)/(r-1)
- Special sums: Σn = n(n+1)/2, Σn² = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6, Σn³ = [n(n+1)/2]²

## Coordinate Geometry

### Straight Lines
- Slope: m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)
- Forms: slope-intercept, point-slope, two-point, intercept form
- Distance from point to line: |ax₁+by₁+c|/√(a²+b²)
- Angle between lines: tan θ = |(m₁-m₂)/(1+m₁m₂)|

### Circles
- Standard form: (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r²
- General form: x² + y² + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0; center (-g,-f), radius √(g²+f²-c)
- Tangent from external point, power of a point

### Conic Sections
- Parabola: y² = 4ax (focus (a,0), directrix x=-a)
- Ellipse: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1 (a>b, foci at (±ae,0))
- Hyperbola: x²/a² - y²/b² = 1

## Calculus

### Limits and Continuity
- L'Hôpital's rule for 0/0 or ∞/∞ forms
- Standard limits: lim(sin x/x)=1, lim((1+1/n)^n)=e
- Continuity: f is continuous at c if lim_{x→c} f(x) = f(c)

### Differentiation
- Product rule, quotient rule, chain rule
- Derivatives of standard functions
- Implicit differentiation
- Applications: maxima/minima, rate of change, tangent/normal

### Integration
- Standard integrals, substitution, parts (ILATE rule)
- Definite integrals: area under curve
- Properties of definite integrals

## Probability
- Classical probability, addition theorem, multiplication theorem
- Conditional probability, Bayes' theorem
- Binomial distribution, mean and variance
