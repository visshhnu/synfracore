# JEE Chemistry Fundamentals

## Physical Chemistry

### Mole Concept
- 1 mole = 6.022×10²³ particles (Avogadro's number)
- Molar mass (g/mol) = molecular mass (amu)
- n = mass/molar mass = V(STP)/22.4 L
- Molarity M = moles/litre; Molality m = moles/kg solvent
- Normality N = equivalents/litre

### Stoichiometry
- Balance equations first; mole ratio from balanced equation
- Limiting reagent: determines max product
- % yield = (actual/theoretical) × 100
- % purity = (pure mass/total mass) × 100

### Atomic Structure
- Bohr model: En = -13.6/n² eV; rn = 0.529n² Å
- Quantum numbers: n (shell), l (subshell 0 to n-1), ml (-l to +l), ms (±½)
- Orbitals: s (l=0, 1 orbital), p (l=1, 3), d (l=2, 5), f (l=3, 7)
- Aufbau principle: fill lowest energy first
- Hund's rule: maximize unpaired electrons in degenerate orbitals
- Pauli exclusion: max 2 electrons per orbital, opposite spins

### Chemical Bonding
- Ionic: complete electron transfer; high mp, bp; conducts when molten/dissolved
- Covalent: electron sharing; VSEPR for geometry; electronegativity for polarity
- Metallic: sea of electrons; conductivity, ductility, luster
- Hydrogen bond: N-H, O-H, F-H with electronegative atom; explains H₂O properties

## Inorganic Chemistry

### Periodic Table Trends
| Property | Across Period | Down Group |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Atomic radius | Decreases | Increases |
| Ionization energy | Increases | Decreases |
| Electronegativity | Increases | Decreases |
| Electron affinity | Generally increases | Decreases |
| Metallic character | Decreases | Increases |

### s-Block Elements
- Group 1 (alkali): Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs; soft metals; +1 ions; react vigorously with water
- Group 2 (alkaline earth): Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba; harder; +2 ions; less reactive
- Diagonal relationship: Li-Mg and Be-Al similarities

### p-Block Overview
- Group 13: B (semimetal), Al (metal); trivalent
- Group 14: C (nonmetal), Si, Ge (semimetals), Sn, Pb (metals)
- Group 15: N, P (nonmetals), As, Sb (semimetals), Bi (metal)
- Group 16: O, S, Se, Te; mostly nonmetals
- Group 17 (Halogens): F, Cl, Br, I; most electronegative
- Group 18 (Noble gases): He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe; inert

## Organic Chemistry Basics

### Nomenclature
- IUPAC: find longest chain (parent), number from end nearest substituent
- Functional group priority (for numbering): COOH > CHO > OH > NH₂ > C=C > C≡C
- Prefixes: meth(1), eth(2), prop(3), but(4), pent(5), hex(6), hept(7), oct(8)
- Suffixes: -ane (alkane), -ene (alkene), -yne (alkyne), -ol (alcohol), -al (aldehyde), -one (ketone), -oic acid (carboxylic)

### Isomerism
- Structural: chain, position, functional group, ring-chain
- Stereoisomerism: geometric (cis/trans; E/Z) and optical (enantiomers, diastereomers)
- Chiral center: sp³ carbon with 4 different groups; R/S configuration
- Optical activity: enantiomers rotate plane-polarized light equally, opposite directions

### Reaction Mechanisms
- Nucleophile: electron-rich, attacks electrophile (NH₃, OH⁻, CN⁻, H₂O)
- Electrophile: electron-poor, attacks nucleophile (H⁺, BF₃, Br⁺, NO₂⁺)
- Free radical: unpaired electron; chain reaction (initiation, propagation, termination)
