# JEE Chemistry Advanced Topics

## Coordination Chemistry
- Central metal + ligands → coordination compound
- Ligands: monodentate (NH₃, Cl⁻, H₂O), bidentate (en, ox²⁻), hexadentate (EDTA)
- IUPAC naming: ligands alphabetically, metal name, [ox state]; anion ends -ate
- Crystal Field Theory: d-orbital splitting by ligand field
- Octahedral: t₂g (lower) and eₘ (higher); Δ_oct splitting
- Strong field ligands: CN⁻>NO₂⁻>en>NH₃>H₂O>OH⁻>F⁻>Cl⁻>Br⁻>I⁻ (spectrochemical series)
- High spin (weak field) vs low spin (strong field)
- Color: d-d transitions absorb visible light; complementary color observed
- Magnetic properties: unpaired electrons → paramagnetic

## Advanced Organic — Named Reactions

### Carbonyl Chemistry
- **Aldol condensation**: α-carbon attacks carbonyl; β-hydroxy carbonyl → α,β-unsaturated (elimination)
- **Cannizzaro**: no α-H aldehydes + strong base → disproportionation (one oxidized, one reduced)
- **Claisen condensation**: ester + base → β-ketoester
- **Reformatsky**: zinc-mediated reaction of α-haloester with aldehyde/ketone

### Rearrangements
- **Beckmann**: oxime → amide (acid catalyst; N migrates antiperiplanar to OH)
- **Hoffmann**: amide + Br₂ + NaOH → primary amine (one carbon shorter)
- **Pinacol**: 1,2-diol + H⁺ → carbonyl compound (1,2-shift)
- **Baeyer-Villiger**: ketone + peracid → ester (O insertion; migrates more substituted group)

## Biomolecules

### Carbohydrates
- Monosaccharides: glucose (aldohexose), fructose (ketohexose), galactose
- Fischer projection: CHO at top, CH₂OH at bottom; D/L configuration by bottom chiral C
- Haworth projection: cyclic form; OH below = D-sugar
- Anomers: α (OH axial/down) vs β (OH equatorial/up) at anomeric C
- Disaccharides: maltose (α-1,4), cellobiose (β-1,4), sucrose (α-1,β-2), lactose (β-1,4)
- Polysaccharides: starch (amylose α-1,4 + amylopectin α-1,6 branches), cellulose (β-1,4)

### Proteins
- Amino acids: L-configuration; 20 standard; essential = not synthesized by body
- Peptide bond: -CO-NH- (amide, resonance, partial double bond character, planar)
- Primary: sequence; Secondary: α-helix (H-bonds intrastrand), β-sheet (interstrand)
- Tertiary: 3D folding (disulfide, hydrophobic, ionic interactions)
- Quaternary: multiple polypeptide subunits

### Nucleic Acids
- DNA: deoxyribose, double helix, A-T (2 H-bonds), G-C (3 H-bonds), antiparallel
- RNA: ribose, single-stranded, A-U pairing; mRNA, tRNA, rRNA
- Central dogma: DNA → RNA → Protein

## Polymers

### Types of Polymerization
- **Addition**: monomers add without byproduct; alkenes, radical/ionic/coordination mechanism
- **Condensation**: bifunctional monomers; byproduct (H₂O, HCl); nylon, polyester, Bakelite

### Important Polymers

| Polyethylene | Ethylene | Addition | Plastic bags |

## Environmental Chemistry
- Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, CFCs, H₂O vapor
- Ozone depletion: CFCs → Cl radicals; Cl + O₃ → ClO + O₂ (catalytic cycle)
- Acid rain: SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃; NOₓ + H₂O → HNO₃; damages buildings, ecosystems
- BOD: Biochemical Oxygen Demand — measure of organic pollution in water
- Heavy metal pollution: Pb, Hg, Cd — bioaccumulation in food chain
