# General Science Fundamentals

## Physics Basics

### Mechanics
- Newton's laws: inertia, F=ma, action-reaction
- Work = Force × displacement × cos θ; W = Fd (if parallel)
- Energy conservation: KE + PE = constant (no friction)
- Power = Work/Time = Force × velocity

### Heat and Temperature
- Temperature scales: Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin
- K = °C + 273; °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
- Heat transfer: conduction, convection, radiation
- Specific heat capacity: Q = mcΔT

### Light
- Speed of light: 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum
- Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection
- Refraction: light bends when changing medium
- Dispersion: prism splits white light into spectrum
- Total internal reflection: basis of optical fiber

### Electricity
- Current I = Q/t (amperes); Voltage V; Resistance R
- Ohm's law V = IR; Power P = VI = I²R = V²/R
- Series circuit: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + ...; same current
- Parallel circuit: 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂; same voltage

## Chemistry Basics

### Atomic Structure
- Atom: protons (+) and neutrons in nucleus; electrons (-) orbit
- Atomic number Z = protons; Mass number A = protons + neutrons
- Isotopes: same Z, different A (same element, different mass)
- Electron configuration: shells K(2), L(8), M(18)

### Chemical Reactions
- Types: combination, decomposition, displacement, double displacement, redox
- Balancing equations: conserve atoms and charge
- Acids and bases: pH scale 0-14; acid (pH<7), neutral (pH=7), base (pH>7)
- Neutralization: acid + base → salt + water

### Periodic Table
- Groups (vertical): similar chemical properties
- Periods (horizontal): increasing atomic number
- Metals, non-metals, metalloids
- Important groups: Noble gases (18), Halogens (17), Alkali metals (1)

## Biology Basics

### Cell Biology
- Cell: basic unit of life
- Prokaryotic (no nucleus): bacteria, archaea
- Eukaryotic (has nucleus): plants, animals, fungi, protists
- Organelles: nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, ER, Golgi, chloroplasts (plants)
- Cell division: mitosis (body cells), meiosis (reproductive cells)

### Human Body Systems
- Digestive: mouth → esophagus → stomach → small intestine → large intestine
- Circulatory: heart pumps blood; arteries (away), veins (toward), capillaries (exchange)
- Respiratory: breathing; alveoli for gas exchange; O₂ in, CO₂ out
- Nervous: brain + spinal cord (CNS) + peripheral nerves
- Immune: WBCs, antibodies, lymph nodes

### Genetics
- DNA: double helix, base pairs A-T and G-C
- Gene: sequence of DNA coding for a protein
- Inheritance: Mendel's laws, dominant and recessive traits
- Mutations: changes in DNA sequence; can be harmful, neutral, or beneficial
