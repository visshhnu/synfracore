# General Science Advanced Topics

## Modern Physics

### Quantum Mechanics
- Wave-particle duality: light and matter exhibit both wave and particle properties
- Double-slit experiment: demonstrates wave nature of electrons
- Photoelectric effect: E = hf - φ (Einstein, Nobel 1921) — proves particle nature of light
- Heisenberg uncertainty: Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2; ΔE·Δt ≥ ℏ/2
- Bohr model → quantum mechanical model: electrons in probability clouds (orbitals)
- Schrödinger equation: ψ(x,t) describes quantum state; |ψ|² = probability density

### Nuclear Physics
- Binding energy: E_b = (Zm_p + Nm_n - M_nucleus)c²
- Binding energy per nucleon: peaks at Fe-56 (most stable)
- Alpha decay: ₂He⁴ emitted; Z→Z-2, A→A-4
- Beta-minus: n → p + e⁻ + antineutrino; Z→Z+1
- Gamma: nuclear energy level transition; no Z or A change
- Fission: U-235 or Pu-239 + neutron → two fragments + 2-3 neutrons + energy; chain reaction
- Fusion: H + H → He + energy; requires extreme temperature; powers the sun

### Special Relativity
- Time dilation: Δt = Δt₀/√(1-v²/c²) — moving clocks run slower
- Length contraction: L = L₀√(1-v²/c²) — objects contract in direction of motion
- Mass-energy equivalence: E = mc²
- Relativistic momentum: p = γmv
- GPS satellites require both special and general relativity corrections

## Advanced Chemistry

### Biochemistry
- Enzymes: protein catalysts; lower activation energy; lock-and-key/induced fit models
- Cofactors: inorganic ions that assist enzymes; Coenzymes: organic molecules (NAD⁺, FAD)
- Allosteric regulation: effector binds away from active site, changes enzyme conformation
- Metabolism: catabolism (breakdown) + anabolism (synthesis)
- ATP: universal energy currency; hydrolysis releases ~30.5 kJ/mol

### Polymer Chemistry
- Natural polymers: proteins, DNA, starch, cellulose, natural rubber
- Synthetic addition polymers: polyethylene, PVC, polystyrene, Teflon
- Synthetic condensation polymers: nylon (polyamide), polyester, Bakelite (phenol-formaldehyde)
- Vulcanization of rubber: cross-linking with sulfur → improved elasticity and strength

## Advanced Biology

### Immunology
- Innate immunity: non-specific; skin, mucus membranes, phagocytes, complement, NK cells
- Adaptive immunity: specific; B cells (humoral — antibodies), T cells (cellular)
- Antibody structure: Y-shaped; 2 heavy + 2 light chains; variable region = antigen-binding site
- Memory cells: remain after infection; enable rapid secondary immune response
- Vaccines: prime immune system without causing disease; herd immunity when >threshold % vaccinated
- Autoimmune disease: immune system attacks self (Type 1 DM, rheumatoid arthritis, SLE, MS)

### Biotechnology
- PCR: polymerase chain reaction; amplifies specific DNA sequences; forensics, diagnosis, research
- Gel electrophoresis: separates DNA/proteins by size in electric field
- Recombinant DNA: insert gene of interest into vector (plasmid) → express in host organism
- CRISPR-Cas9: precise gene editing; guide RNA directs Cas9 nuclease to target sequence
- GMO crops: Bt cotton (insecticidal protein), Golden Rice (beta-carotene), herbicide-resistant soy

### Neuroscience
- Neuron: dendrites (input) → cell body (integration) → axon (output) → synaptic terminal
- Action potential: Na⁺ influx (depolarization), K⁺ efflux (repolarization), refractory period
- Synapse: chemical (neurotransmitter) or electrical (gap junction)
- Key neurotransmitters: ACh (muscle), dopamine (reward), serotonin (mood), GABA (inhibitory), glutamate (excitatory)
- Neuroplasticity: synaptic strengthening (LTP) and weakening (LTD); basis of learning
