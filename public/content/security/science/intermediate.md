# General Science Intermediate

## Physics — Waves and Sound

### Wave Properties
- Wavelength (λ): distance between crests; Frequency (f): cycles per second; Period T = 1/f
- Wave speed: v = fλ; Sound in air ≈ 340 m/s; Light in vacuum = 3×10⁸ m/s
- Amplitude: height of wave; determines loudness (sound) or brightness (light)
- Transverse waves: oscillation perpendicular to propagation (light, water waves)
- Longitudinal waves: oscillation parallel to propagation (sound)

### Sound
- Produced by vibrations; requires medium (cannot travel in vacuum)
- Pitch ∝ frequency; Loudness ∝ amplitude (decibels = dB)
- Doppler effect: moving source → frequency shift (higher approaching, lower receding)
- Resonance: object vibrates at natural frequency when driven at that frequency

### Optics
- Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection (plane mirrors)
- Refraction: light bends when changing medium; n = c/v (refractive index)
- Snell's law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂
- Total internal reflection: when angle > critical angle (fiber optics, diamonds)
- Dispersion: different wavelengths (colors) refract differently → prism splits white light

## Chemistry — Solutions and Reactions

### Solution Chemistry
- Solubility: amount that dissolves; solids ↑ with temp; gases ↓ with temp
- Molarity: M = moles/liter; useful for reactions
- Colligative properties depend on number of dissolved particles (not identity):
  - Boiling point elevation: ΔTb = Kb × m
  - Freezing point depression: ΔTf = -Kf × m
  - Osmotic pressure: π = MRT

### Types of Chemical Reactions
- Combination: A + B → AB (synthesis)
- Decomposition: AB → A + B
- Single displacement: A + BC → AC + B (more reactive metal displaces less reactive)
- Double displacement: AB + CD → AD + CB (includes precipitation, neutralization)
- Combustion: fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (+ energy)
- Redox: oxidation (electron loss) + reduction (electron gain) occur together

### Electrochemistry
- Electrolysis: electrical energy → chemical change (electroplating, Hall process for Al)
- Galvanic cell: chemical energy → electrical energy (batteries)
- Oxidation at anode (An Ox), Reduction at cathode (Red Cat)
- Standard reduction potentials: more positive = stronger oxidizing agent

## Biology — Systems Physiology

### Nervous System
- CNS: brain + spinal cord; integrates and processes information
- PNS: somatic (voluntary muscles) + autonomic (sympathetic/parasympathetic)
- Sympathetic: fight-or-flight; increases HR, BP, diverts blood to muscles
- Parasympathetic: rest-and-digest; decreases HR, increases digestion

### Endocrine System
- Hormones: chemical messengers in blood; target specific receptors
- Pituitary: "master gland"; controls other glands (thyroid, adrenal, gonads)
- Thyroid: metabolism (T3, T4); Pancreas: blood glucose (insulin, glucagon)
- Adrenal: cortex (cortisol, aldosterone); medulla (adrenaline/epinephrine)
- Negative feedback: hormone level controls its own production

### Reproductive System
- Meiosis: produces gametes (sperm, eggs) with n chromosomes (haploid)
- Fertilization: sperm + egg → zygote (2n, diploid)
- Embryonic development: zygote → morula → blastocyst → implantation
- DNA inheritance: half from each parent; 23 pairs of chromosomes
