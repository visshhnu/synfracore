# RRB NTPC Interview Q&A

## Mathematics Problems

**Q: Pipe A fills tank in 12 hours, Pipe B empties it in 16 hours. Both open together — time to fill?**
Filling rate A = 1/12. Emptying rate B = −1/16.
Net rate = 1/12 − 1/16 = 4/48 − 3/48 = 1/48 per hour.
Time to fill = 48 hours.

**Q: Train 150m long passes a pole in 15 sec. Time to cross a 300m bridge?**
Speed = 150/15 = 10 m/s. Distance = 150 + 300 = 450m. Time = 450/10 = 45 seconds.

**Q: Average age of 5 persons is 25. A person aged 35 joins. New average?**
Old total = 5 × 25 = 125. New total = 125 + 35 = 160. New average = 160/6 ≈ 26.67 years.

**Q: Simple interest on ₹5000 at 8% p.a. for 3 years?**
SI = P × R × T / 100 = 5000 × 8 × 3 / 100 = ₹1,200. Amount = ₹6,200.

## Reasoning Problems

**Q: Series: 2, 5, 10, 17, 26, ?**
Differences: 3, 5, 7, 9, 11. Next difference = 11. Answer: 26 + 11 = 37.

**Q: Series — squares of primes: 4, 9, 25, ?, 121, 169**
2²=4, 3²=9, 5²=25, 7²=49, 11²=121, 13²=169. Answer: 49.

**Q: Blood relations — A is B's father. C is B's mother. How is A related to C?**
A is father of B, C is mother of B. A and C are both parents of B → A is C's husband.

## General Awareness — Railways

**Q: What is the function of RDSO?**
Research Designs and Standards Organisation — technical arm of Indian Railways, headquartered in Lucknow. Develops and maintains technical standards for railway equipment, rolling stock, track components. Conducts R&D for new technologies. Certifies all new equipment before it can be used on Indian Railways. Critical for safety and standardisation across the network.

**Q: What is the Vande Bharat Express?**
India's indigenously designed and manufactured semi-high-speed train. Designed by RDSO and manufactured at Integral Coach Factory (ICF), Chennai. Top speed 160 km/h; typical operating speed 110-130 km/h. Features: automatic doors, on-board infotainment, GPS-based passenger information system, faster acceleration than conventional trains. First service inaugurated February 2019 (New Delhi–Varanasi).

**Q: What is the Kavach system?**
Kavach (Shield) = Train Collision Avoidance System. Developed indigenously by RDSO. Automatically applies brakes when two trains approach each other on the same track within an unsafe distance. Prevents Signal Passed At Danger (SPAD) incidents and rear-end collisions. Being progressively deployed across Indian Railways network. Demonstrated capability to prevent high-speed collisions in field trials.

**Q: What are the 18 railway zones?**
Indian Railways has 18 zones. Notable ones: Northern (New Delhi), Southern (Chennai), Eastern (Kolkata), Western (Mumbai), Central (Mumbai), South Central (Secunderabad), South Eastern (Kolkata), North Eastern (Gorakhpur), Northeast Frontier (Maligaon/Guwahati), East Central (Hajipur), East Coast (Bhubaneswar), West Central (Jabalpur), North Central (Prayagraj), North Western (Jaipur), South Western (Hubballi), South East Central (Bilaspur), Metro Railway Kolkata, and South Coast Railway (Visakhapatnam — newest).

**Q: What is the Indian Railway's freight pricing model?**
Indian Railways classifies freight into commodity groups (A, B, C...) with rates varying by commodity, distance, wagon type, and loading factors. Key components: basic freight rate + surcharges (busy season, Development surcharge) + terminal charges. Concessions: empty flow direction concession (encourage return loads), minirak concession (full train of one commodity by one party). Pricing is set by Railway Board. Freight historically cross-subsidised passenger fares — a structural issue that has impacted competitiveness vs road transport.

**Q: What is the significance of dedicated freight corridors (DFCs)?**
Dedicated Freight Corridors (DFCs): separate rail lines exclusively for freight trains, eliminating conflict with passenger trains. Two main corridors: Eastern DFC (Ludhiana to Dankuni, 1856 km) and Western DFC (Jawaharlal Nehru Port to Dadri, 1504 km). Significance: allow heavier, longer trains (double-stack containers), higher speeds for freight (from 25 km/h to 75+ km/h), frees existing tracks for more passenger trains, reduces logistics costs and dwell times, critical for PM GatiShakti and logistics efficiency goals. Operated by Dedicated Freight Corridor Corporation of India (DFCCI).

**Q: What is the difference between metre gauge, broad gauge, and narrow gauge?**
Indian Railways uses multiple track gauges:
Broad Gauge (BG): 1676mm (5 ft 6 in) — the widest; used on major trunk routes; allows heavier, faster trains; most of Indian Railway network. Project Unigauge aimed to convert all gauge to BG.
Metre Gauge (MG): 1000mm (3 ft 3⅜ in) — significant network in Rajasthan, interior areas; being converted to BG.
Narrow Gauge (NG): 762mm or 610mm — mountain railways (Darjeeling, Ooty/Nilgiri, Matheran); heritage and tourist value; impractical to convert due to terrain. Konkan Railway and most new lines use broad gauge.

**Q: What are the main categories of posts in RRB NTPC?**
RRB NTPC covers Non-Technical Popular Category posts in Indian Railways. Graduate-level posts (require degree): Junior Clerk cum Typist, Accounts Clerk cum Typist, Junior Time Keeper, Trains Clerk, Commercial cum Ticket Clerk, Station Master, Goods Guard, Senior Commercial cum Ticket Clerk, Senior Clerk cum Typist, Junior Account Assistant cum Typist, Senior Time Keeper, Commercial Apprentice, Traffic Assistant. 12th-pass level posts: Junior Clerk cum Typist, Accounts Clerk cum Typist, Junior Time Keeper, Trains Clerk, Commercial cum Ticket Clerk. All posts are gazetted or non-gazetted Group C posts. Pay levels range from Level 2 to Level 6 of the 7th Pay Commission pay matrix.

**Q: What is the selection process for RRB NTPC?**
Multi-stage process: CBT 1 (Computer Based Test): 100 questions, 90 minutes — qualifying test (marks not counted in final merit). CBT 2: 120 questions, 90 minutes — marks COUNT toward final merit; standard is higher than CBT 1. Typing Skill Test (TST): for clerical posts requiring typing (30 wpm English or 25 wpm Hindi). Computer Based Aptitude Test (CBAT): for Station Master and Traffic Assistant posts. Document Verification (DV): verification of all educational and category certificates. Medical examination: fitness standards specific to each post category. Final merit based on CBT 2 marks with normalisation across shifts.
