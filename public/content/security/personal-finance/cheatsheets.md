# Personal Finance Cheatsheet

## 50/30/20 Budget Rule
```
Take-home income = 100%

50% — NEEDS (non-negotiable):
  Rent/EMIs, groceries, utilities, transport, insurance

30% — WANTS (lifestyle):
  Dining out, entertainment, hobbies, shopping, travel

20% — SAVINGS + DEBT REPAYMENT:
  Emergency fund first → investments → extra debt payments
```

## Rule of 72 — Time to Double Money
| Annual Return | Years to Double |
|--------------|----------------|
| 6% (FD) | 12.0 years |
| 7.1% (PPF) | 10.1 years |
| 9% (debt mutual fund) | 8.0 years |
| 12% (equity) | 6.0 years |
| 24% (credit card debt) | 3.0 years — debt doubles! |

## Emergency Fund Formula
```
Monthly essential expenses (Rent + Food + Utilities + Transport + EMIs) = ₹X
Emergency fund target = ₹X × 6 months

Where to keep it:
  50% in savings account (instant access, T+0)
  50% in liquid mutual fund (T+1 redemption, slightly better return)
```

## CIBIL Score Factors
| Factor | Weight | How to Optimise |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Payment history | 35% | Pay ALL bills on time, always |
| Credit utilisation | 30% | Keep below 30% of credit limit |
| Credit history length | 15% | Don't close old credit cards |
| Credit mix | 10% | Have both credit cards and loans |
| New credit inquiries | 10% | Don't apply for multiple loans at once |
Target score: ≥750 (range 300-900)

## Retirement Corpus Formula
```
Monthly expense needed in retirement = ₹E
Annual expense = ₹E × 12
Corpus needed = Annual expense × 25 (4% withdrawal rule)
= ₹E × 300

Example: Need ₹60,000/month → ₹60,000 × 300 = ₹1.8 crore
```
