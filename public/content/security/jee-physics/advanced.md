# JEE Physics Advanced Topics

## Fluid Mechanics
- Pressure in fluid: P = P₀ + ρgh (Pascal's law)
- Buoyancy: F_b = ρ_fluid × V_submerged × g (Archimedes)
- Continuity equation: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (incompressible flow)
- Bernoulli: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant (along streamline)
- Applications: Venturi meter, Pitot tube, atomizer

## Surface Tension and Viscosity
- Surface tension T: force per unit length
- Excess pressure in bubble: ΔP = 4T/r (soap bubble, 2 surfaces); 2T/r (drop, 1 surface)
- Capillary rise: h = 2T cosθ / (ρgr)
- Viscosity: F = ηA(dv/dy); Poiseuille: Q = πr⁴ΔP/(8ηL)

## Semiconductor Physics
- p-n junction: contact potential, depletion region
- Forward bias: reduces barrier, large current; Reverse bias: increases barrier, small current
- Diode equation: I = I₀(e^(V/nVT) - 1)
- Transistor configurations: CE most common; α = IC/IE; β = IC/IB; β = α/(1-α)
- Logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR truth tables

## Nuclear Physics
- Binding energy: E_b = (Zm_p + Nm_n - M_nucleus)c²
- BE per nucleon peaks at Fe-56 (most stable nucleus)
- Q-value of reaction: Q = (initial mass - final mass)c²
- Alpha decay: ₄He nucleus emitted; Z→Z-2, A→A-4
- Beta-minus: neutron → proton + e⁻ + antineutrino; Z→Z+1
- Gamma: nuclear energy level transition; no change in Z, A

## Wave Optics — Detailed

### Interference
- Constructive: path diff = nλ; Destructive: (2n+1)λ/2
- Young's double slit: fringe width β = λD/d
- Intensity: I = 4I₀cos²(δ/2), where δ = 2π×(path diff)/λ
- YDSE with thin film: extra path = 2μt (film of refractive index μ, thickness t)

### Diffraction
- Single slit: minima at a sinθ = mλ
- Central maximum width = 2λ/a (angular)
- Diffraction limit: θ_min = 1.22λ/D (Rayleigh criterion)

## Special Topics

### Communication Systems
- Modulation index: AM m = Am/Ac; FM β = Δf/fm
- Bandwidth AM = 2fm; FM ≈ 2(Δf + fm) by Carson's rule
- Signal-to-noise ratio advantages of FM

### Nuclear Reactor
- Critical mass: minimum for sustained chain reaction
- Moderator: slows neutrons (heavy water, graphite)
- Control rods: absorb neutrons (cadmium, boron)
- Coolant: removes heat

### Photoelectric Effect Summary
- Threshold frequency: f₀ = φ/h (below this, no emission regardless of intensity)
- Maximum KE: KE_max = hf - φ = eV_stop
- Intensity affects number of photoelectrons, not KE
- Einstein's explanation won Nobel Prize 1921
