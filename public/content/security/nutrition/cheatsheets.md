# Nutrition Cheatsheet

## Macronutrients at a Glance
| Macronutrient | Calories/g | Daily Target | Key Sources |
|--------------|-----------|------------|------------|
| Carbohydrates | 4 kcal | 45-65% calories | Whole grains, legumes, fruits, vegetables |
| Proteins | 4 kcal | 0.8-2g/kg body weight | Meat, fish, dairy, eggs, legumes, tofu |
| Fats | 9 kcal | 20-35% calories | Olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish |
| Alcohol | 7 kcal | Not a nutrient | — |

## Key Vitamins Quick Reference
| Vitamin | Key Function | Deficiency Signs |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| A | Vision, immune, epithelial | Night blindness, dry skin |
| D | Bone health, immune, mood | Rickets, osteoporosis, fatigue |
| C | Antioxidant, collagen synthesis | Scurvy (gum bleeding, poor healing) |
| B12 | Nerve function, red blood cells | Megaloblastic anaemia, neuropathy |
| Folate | DNA synthesis, cell division | Neural tube defects (in pregnancy) |
| Iron | Haemoglobin, oxygen transport | Iron-deficiency anaemia |
| Calcium | Bone and teeth, muscle | Osteoporosis, muscle cramps |

## Healthy Plate Method
```
½ plate: Non-starchy vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, peppers, salad)
¼ plate: Lean protein (chicken, fish, lentils, tofu, eggs)
¼ plate: Complex carbohydrates (brown rice, whole wheat roti, sweet potato)
Side: Healthy fat (olive oil, avocado, a small handful of nuts)
Drink: Water (plain, no sugar)
```

## Protein Targets by Activity Level
| Goal | Protein (per kg body weight) |
|------|------------------------------|
| Sedentary adult | 0.8 g/kg |
| Moderately active | 1.0-1.2 g/kg |
| Endurance athletes | 1.2-1.6 g/kg |
| Muscle building | 1.6-2.2 g/kg |
| Cutting (preserving muscle) | 2.0-2.4 g/kg |

## Fat Types Summary
| Fat Type | Examples | Health Effect |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Saturated | Butter, ghee, coconut oil, red meat | Limit — raises LDL cholesterol |
| Trans (artificial) | Hydrogenated oils, many processed foods | AVOID — worst for heart |
| Monounsaturated (MUFA) | Olive oil, avocado, almonds | Beneficial — heart healthy |
| Omega-3 PUFA | Salmon, sardines, flaxseed, walnuts | Anti-inflammatory, cardioprotective |
| Omega-6 PUFA | Vegetable oils | Generally OK; balance with omega-3 |

## Omega-3 Food Sources
| Food | Omega-3 Content |
|------|----------------|
| Salmon (100g) | 2.2g EPA+DHA |
| Mackerel (100g) | 2.6g EPA+DHA |
| Sardines (100g) | 1.5g EPA+DHA |
| Flaxseeds (1 tbsp) | 2.3g ALA |
| Walnuts (30g) | 2.6g ALA |
| Chia seeds (1 tbsp) | 2.0g ALA |

Note: ALA (plant source) must be converted to EPA/DHA; conversion is only ~5-10% efficient. Direct EPA/DHA from fatty fish or algae-based supplements is more bioavailable.
