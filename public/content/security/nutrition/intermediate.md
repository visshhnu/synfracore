# Nutrition Intermediate

## Energy Balance and Weight Management

### Caloric Balance
- Weight gain: calories in > calories out
- Weight loss: calories in < calories out
- Maintenance: calories in = calories out
- 1 kg fat ≈ 7700 kcal; 500 kcal deficit/day → ~0.5kg/week loss

### Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)
Energy burned at rest:
- Mifflin-St Jeor: Males: 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) - 5×age + 5
- Females: 10×weight + 6.25×height - 5×age - 161
- Multiply by activity factor: sedentary ×1.2; light ×1.375; moderate ×1.55; active ×1.725

### Macronutrient Distribution
| Goal | Carbs | Protein | Fat |
|------|-------|---------|-----|
| General health | 45-65% | 10-35% | 20-35% |
| Weight loss | 40-50% | 30-40% | 20-30% |
| Muscle gain | 45-55% | 25-35% | 20-30% |
| Endurance sports | 55-65% | 15-25% | 20-30% |

## Blood Sugar and Glycemic Index

### Glycemic Index (GI)
- Low GI (<55): oats, legumes, most fruits — slow blood sugar rise
- Medium GI (55-70): brown rice, whole wheat bread
- High GI (>70): white bread, white rice, glucose, sports drinks — rapid spike

### Glycemic Load (GL)
GL = (GI × carb grams per serving) / 100
Better measure than GI alone because considers serving size.
Low GL: <10; Medium: 11-19; High: >20

### Insulin Response
- High-GI foods → rapid insulin spike → hunger returns quickly
- Protein and fat slow gastric emptying, lower GI of a meal
- Fiber significantly lowers glycemic response

## Protein Quality and Timing

### Protein Digestibility Score (PDCAAS)
Measures protein quality based on amino acid content and digestibility:
- Score 1.0 (highest): whey, eggs, milk, soy
- Score 0.5-0.9: legumes, some grains
- Score <0.5: some plant sources

### Protein Timing
- Post-workout protein: within 30-60 minutes after resistance training
- Leucine threshold: ~2.5-3g leucine per meal triggers muscle protein synthesis
- Distribute protein evenly across meals (vs one large serving) for better synthesis
- Before sleep: slow-digesting protein (casein) may improve overnight muscle recovery

## Micronutrient Interactions

### Iron Absorption
- Heme iron (meat): 15-35% absorbed
- Non-heme iron (plant): 2-20% absorbed
- Enhancers: Vitamin C (dramatically increases non-heme absorption), meat factor
- Inhibitors: calcium, polyphenols (tea, coffee), phytates (whole grains, legumes)
- Separate iron-rich meals from calcium-rich foods

### Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Require dietary fat for absorption
- Eat fat-soluble vitamin foods with healthy fats (e.g., salad with olive oil)
- Store in body fat — toxicity possible with excessive supplementation
