# Nutrition Advanced Topics

## Nutrigenomics

### Gene-Diet Interactions
- Different people respond differently to same diet based on genetics
- MTHFR gene variants: affect folate metabolism; need methylated folate
- APOE genotype: affects response to dietary fat and cardiovascular risk
- Lactase gene: determines lactose tolerance
- Caffeine metabolism: CYP1A2 gene variants affect coffee's cardiovascular effects

### Personalized Nutrition
Moving beyond population-based guidelines to individual recommendations based on:
- Genetic testing (nutrigenomics)
- Gut microbiome analysis
- Blood biomarker testing
- Continuous glucose monitoring
- Metabolomics

## Phytochemicals and Functional Foods

### Key Phytochemicals
- **Polyphenols**: Antioxidant; berries, dark chocolate, green tea, red wine; cardiovascular benefits
- **Carotenoids**: Beta-carotene (carrots, sweet potato) → Vitamin A; lycopene (tomatoes) — prostate cancer protection
- **Glucosinolates**: Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage); cancer-protective; activate detox enzymes
- **Allicin**: Garlic; antimicrobial, cardiovascular benefits
- **Curcumin**: Turmeric; anti-inflammatory; bioavailability enhanced by piperine (black pepper)
- **Isoflavones**: Soy; phytoestrogens; may reduce menopausal symptoms

### Anti-Inflammatory Nutrition
Mediterranean diet pattern:
- High: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, olive oil, fish
- Moderate: poultry, eggs, dairy
- Low: red meat, processed food, sweets
- Associated with reduced cardiovascular disease, cognitive decline, cancer risk

## Gut-Nutrition Connection

### Dietary Fiber and Microbiome
- Diverse plant foods → diverse microbiome
- Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs): produced by bacteria fermenting fiber; butyrate (colonocyte fuel), propionate (liver), acetate (energy)
- Resistant starch: cooked and cooled rice/potato; feeds bacteria; lower glycemic response

### Fermented Foods Research
- Randomized controlled trial (Sonnenburg lab, 2021): high-fermented food diet increases microbiome diversity and reduces inflammatory markers more than high-fiber diet
- 16-week intervention, significant improvements in 19 inflammatory proteins

## Special Diets Evidence Review

### Ketogenic Diet
- Very low carb (<50g/day), high fat, moderate protein
- Achieves ketosis: fat → ketones for brain fuel
- Evidence: epilepsy (strong), weight loss (effective short-term), type 2 diabetes (promising)
- Risks: keto flu, electrolyte imbalance, nutrient deficiencies, sustainability
- Long-term evidence limited

### Intermittent Fasting (IF)
- 16:8 (eat in 8-hour window), 5:2 (2 days restricted), alternate day fasting
- Mechanisms: autophagy, insulin sensitivity, circadian alignment
- Evidence: weight loss comparable to continuous caloric restriction; additional metabolic benefits possible
- Considerations: not suitable for everyone; may affect muscle mass if protein insufficient
