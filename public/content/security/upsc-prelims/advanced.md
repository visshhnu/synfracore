# UPSC Prelims Advanced Strategies

## Paper 1 (GS) — 100 Questions, 200 Marks

### High-Yield Subject Areas
- Indian Polity: 15-20 questions every year
- History (Modern India): 12-15 questions
- Geography: 12-15 questions
- Economy: 10-15 questions
- Environment and Ecology: 10-15 questions
- Science and Technology: 10-12 questions
- Current Affairs: 20-25 questions (across subjects)

### Elimination Strategy
UPSC uses 4-option MCQs with -0.66 (1/3 of 2) negative marking.
- Strong elimination (2 wrong options identified): ALWAYS attempt
- Moderate confidence (1 wrong identified): attempt if analytical
- Pure guess: skip (expected value = 0)
- "Statement-based" questions (Assertion-Reason, Match following): use 1 certain statement to eliminate options

### Statement-Based Questions
"Which of the following statements is/are correct?"
1. Find the most likely wrong statement using knowledge
2. Eliminate options containing that statement
3. Find the most certainly correct statement
4. Cross-reference with remaining options
This often narrows from 4 to 2 options, improving odds significantly.

## Paper 2 (CSAT) — 80 Questions, 200 Marks (Qualifying: 66 marks)

### Comprehension Strategy
- Read questions FIRST, then passage (focused reading)
- Answers are in the passage, not general knowledge
- "Inference" questions: look for what is logically implied, not stated
- "Assumption" questions: find unstated premise that must be true for argument

### Basic Numeracy
- Data sufficiency: very common in CSAT
- Logical reasoning: syllogisms, Venn diagrams, critical reasoning
- English comprehension: 3-4 passages, analytical questions

## Advanced Subject Content

### Environment and Ecology (10-15 Qs)
- Biodiversity: Convention on Biological Diversity, Nagoya Protocol
- Climate change: IPCC reports, Paris Agreement targets
- Environmental laws: Wildlife Protection Act, Forest Conservation Act, Environment Protection Act
- Biosphere reserves, national parks, Ramsar wetlands
- Pollution: CPCB, SPCB, PIC, Basel Convention

### Science and Technology (10-12 Qs)
- Space: ISRO missions, international space (ISS, Artemis program)
- Biotechnology: CRISPR, mRNA vaccines, gene therapy
- Nuclear: NPT, CTBT, India's civilian nuclear program
- IT: AI policy, data protection bill, cybersecurity
- Defence: DRDO systems, INS launches

### Economy (10-15 Qs)
- Monetary policy: RBI tools (repo, CRR, SLR, OMOs)
- Fiscal policy: budget components, deficits
- International: WTO, IMF, World Bank, BRICS, G20
- Financial inclusion: Jan Dhan, UPI, PMJJBY, PMSBY
