# Patient Documentation Fundamentals

## Importance of Clinical Documentation
Medical records serve multiple purposes:
- Continuity of care across providers
- Legal record of care provided
- Basis for coding and billing
- Quality measurement and reporting
- Research and public health data

## Types of Healthcare Documentation

### History and Physical (H&P)
Required within 24 hours of admission. Contains:
- Chief complaint
- History of present illness (HPI)
- Past medical, surgical, family, social history
- Review of systems (ROS)
- Physical examination findings
- Assessment and plan

### Progress Notes
Daily documentation of patient's condition and care.
Common formats: SOAP, APSO, narrative.

### Operative Reports
Detailed description of surgical procedure:
- Pre-operative and post-operative diagnosis
- Surgeons, assistants, anesthesiologist
- Procedure description step by step
- Findings, complications, specimens

### Discharge Summary
Summary at end of inpatient stay:
- Admission and discharge dates/diagnoses
- Hospital course
- Procedures performed
- Condition at discharge
- Discharge instructions and follow-up plan

## SOAP Note Format

**S** — Subjective: Patient's complaints, symptoms in their own words
**O** — Objective: Vital signs, physical exam findings, lab/imaging results
**A** — Assessment: Diagnoses, clinical impressions
**P** — Plan: Treatment orders, medications, referrals, follow-up

Example:
```
S: Patient complains of 3-day history of productive cough and fever
O: Temp 38.9°C, HR 98, RR 22, SpO2 95% on RA. Breath sounds 
   decreased right base with dullness to percussion.
A: 1. Community-acquired pneumonia, right lower lobe
   2. Hypoxemia
P: 1. Chest X-ray ordered, sputum culture
   2. Azithromycin 500mg daily × 5 days
   3. Acetaminophen PRN fever
   4. Follow-up in 1 week
```

## Medical Record Completion Standards
- Timeliness: H&P within 24 hours, discharge summary within 30 days
- Legibility: readable by others
- Authenticity: signed and dated by responsible provider
- Completeness: all required elements present
