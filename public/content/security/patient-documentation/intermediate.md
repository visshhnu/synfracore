# Patient Documentation Intermediate

## Documentation for Coding Accuracy

### Specificity Requirements
Vague documentation → unspecified codes → lower payment, poor data
Better documentation:
- "Heart failure" → "Acute on chronic systolic heart failure"
- "Diabetes" → "Type 2 diabetes with stage 3 CKD"
- "Fracture" → "Closed displaced femoral shaft fracture, right"

### Physician Queries
When documentation is:
- Unclear or ambiguous
- Incomplete for coding purposes
- Inconsistent (different terms in different notes)

A query asks the physician to clarify/confirm:
- Is this condition a complication of the procedure?
- Is the anemia related to the blood loss during surgery?
- What type of heart failure: systolic vs diastolic?

**Compliant queries**: Multiple-choice format, non-leading, multiple options including "clinically undetermined"

## E/M Documentation (2021 Guidelines)

### Medical Decision Making (MDM)
| Element | Minimal | Low | Moderate | High |
|---------|---------|-----|---------|------|
| Problems | Self-limited | 2+ self-limited or 1 stable chronic | 1 uncontrolled OR new problem | 1+ severe threatening life/limb |
| Data | None | Lab or imaging ordered | Independent interpretation | Independent interpretation + external records |
| Risk | OTC meds | Prescription drugs | Prescription drug mgmt | Drug threatening life, hospitalization |

### Time-Based Documentation
"Total time on date of encounter" can determine E/M level.
Must document: total time and that it was spent on the date of the visit.
99213: 20-29 min | 99214: 30-39 min | 99215: 40-54 min (established patient)

## Nursing Documentation

### Nursing Notes
- Assessment findings
- Medications administered (time, route, dose, patient response)
- Procedures performed
- Patient/family education
- Response to treatments

### Flowsheets
- Vital signs (Q4H, Q8H, etc.)
- Intake and output
- Pain scores
- Nursing assessments (skin, falls risk, nutrition)

### Care Plans
- Nursing diagnoses (different from medical diagnoses)
- Goals
- Interventions
- Evaluation

## Operative Documentation

### Key Elements Required
- Pre-op and post-op diagnoses (must match — guides coding)
- Surgeon's name and assistants
- Anesthesia type
- Procedure performed
- Detailed narrative of surgical steps
- Findings and complications
- Specimens sent to pathology
- Estimated blood loss
- Patient condition at end of procedure
