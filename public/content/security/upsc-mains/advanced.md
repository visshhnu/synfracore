# UPSC Mains Advanced Strategies

## Paper Structure (Mains Written)

### General Studies Papers (GS I-IV)
- GS I: Indian Heritage & Culture, History, Geography — 250 marks
- GS II: Governance, Constitution, Polity, International Relations — 250 marks
- GS III: Technology, Economic Development, Environment, Security — 250 marks
- GS IV: Ethics, Integrity, Aptitude — 250 marks

### Optional Subject Papers
- 2 papers × 250 marks = 500 marks
- Most important for final rank differentiation
- Choose based on: background, interest, material availability, previous toppers' choices

### Essay Paper (Paper I)
- 2 essays from 4 choices (2 sections)
- 125 marks each; 3-hour paper
- "Substantive depth + inclusive breadth" formula
- Not just information — own analysis, multiple perspectives, conclusion with conviction

## GS IV — Ethics Paper Strategy

### Theory Section
- Attitude: formation, function, influence on behavior
- Emotional intelligence: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills
- Ethical theories: utilitarianism, deontology (Kant), virtue ethics, care ethics
- Philosophers: Aristotle, Rawls, Bentham/Mill, Kant, Gandhian ethics
- Public service values: integrity, impartiality, non-partisanship, dedication

### Case Study Approach
Given an ethical dilemma with stakeholders, constraints, possible actions:
1. Identify stakeholders and their interests
2. Identify the ethical dimension (conflict of values)
3. List possible options (not just binary)
4. Evaluate each option against ethical frameworks
5. Choose the best option with justification
6. Address implementation challenges
Avoid: "I will follow rules" as only answer — demonstrate moral reasoning.

## Answer Writing for GS Papers

### Structure for 15-Mark Questions (250 words)
```
Introduction (30-40 words): Context, definition, or striking fact
Body Para 1 (60 words): Main argument / first dimension
Body Para 2 (60 words): Second dimension / counterpoint
Body Para 3 (60 words): Third dimension / contemporary relevance
Conclusion (30-40 words): Way forward / balanced view / own reasoned stance
```

### For 10-Mark Questions (150 words)
- Shorter introduction (20 words)
- 2 body paragraphs (50-60 words each)
- Crisp conclusion (20-30 words)
- Still use examples/data/case

## Current Affairs Integration

### For GS I
- Historical events connected to present (e.g., partition → current Indo-Pak relations)
- Art/culture schemes and UNESCO listings
- Geographical disasters linked to climate change

### For GS II
- Parliamentary debates and constitutional amendments
- Judicial pronouncements (SC orders)
- International summits and treaties India signed

### For GS III
- New technologies and their societal impact
- Economic survey data points
- Environmental agreements and India's commitments
