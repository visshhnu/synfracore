# SSC Exams — Combined Graduate Level (CGL) and Other Exams

## SSC — Staff Selection Commission

SSC recruits for Group B and C posts in Central Government departments, ministries, and organizations. It is one of India's largest recruiters.

## SSC CGL — Exam Pattern (Most Popular)

### Tier I (Computer Based Test)
- General Intelligence & Reasoning: 25 questions, 50 marks
- General Awareness: 25 questions, 50 marks
- Quantitative Aptitude: 25 questions, 50 marks
- English Comprehension: 25 questions, 50 marks
- **Total: 100 questions, 200 marks, 60 minutes**
- Negative marking: -0.5 per wrong answer

### Tier II (Computer Based Test — 3 papers)
**Paper I (Compulsory for all):**
- Mathematical Abilities: 30 questions, 90 marks
- Reasoning & General Intelligence: 30 questions, 90 marks
- English Language & Comprehension: 45 questions, 135 marks
- General Awareness: 25 questions, 75 marks
- Computer Knowledge: 20 questions, 60 marks
- **Total: 150 questions, 450 marks, 2 hours 30 minutes**

**Paper II (For JSO posts):** Statistics — 100 questions, 200 marks

**Paper III (For AAO posts):** General Studies (Finance & Economics) — 100 questions, 200 marks

## Syllabus — Key Topics

### General Intelligence & Reasoning
Analogies, similarities and differences, space visualization, spatial orientation, problem solving, analysis, judgment, decision making, visual memory, discrimination, observation, relationship concepts, arithmetical reasoning, verbal and figure classification, arithmetic number series, non-verbal series, coding-decoding, statement-conclusion, syllogistic reasoning, matrix, word building, seating arrangements, blood relations.

### General Awareness
India and its neighbours, history (ancient, medieval, modern), geography (India + world), polity (Constitution, governance), economics (Indian economy, budget basics), science (physics, chemistry, biology), current events, sports, books and authors, important days, technology.

### Quantitative Aptitude
Number systems, computation of whole numbers, decimals, fractions, percentages, ratio & proportion, square roots, averages, interest (SI & CI), profit & loss, discount, partnership, mixture & alligation, time & distance, time & work, mensuration (2D & 3D), trigonometry, data interpretation (table, graph, histogram).

### English Comprehension
Spot the error, fill in the blanks, synonyms/antonyms, spelling correction, idioms & phrases, one-word substitution, improvement of sentences, active/passive voice, direct/indirect speech, comprehension passage.

## Preparation Strategy

**Timeline: 6–9 months for serious preparation**

1. **Build concepts first (2 months):** Cover quant, reasoning, English grammar fundamentals
2. **Practice (2 months):** 50 subject-wise questions daily, solve previous papers
3. **Current affairs (ongoing):** Read 1 national newspaper daily, make notes of key facts
4. **Mock tests (2 months):** 1 full mock daily in the final 2 months, analyze every error
5. **Revision (1 month):** Static GK notes, formula sheets, weak area focus

**Cut-offs (CGL Tier I General — approximate):** 140–160 out of 200. Varies significantly year to year.
