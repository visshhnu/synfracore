## Mathematics

**Q: Number system — find HCF and LCM of 36, 48, 60.**
```
Prime factorisation:
  36 = 2² × 3²
  48 = 2⁴ × 3
  60 = 2² × 3 × 5

HCF = product of LOWEST powers of common primes
    = 2² × 3 = 4 × 3 = 12

LCM = product of HIGHEST powers of all primes
    = 2⁴ × 3² × 5 = 16 × 9 × 5 = 720

Quick check: HCF × LCM = product of numbers (only for TWO numbers)
12 × 720 ≠ 36×48×60 (rule is for two numbers only, not three)
```

**Q: Average age of 5 people is 30. If one aged 40 is replaced by someone aged 20, new average?**
```
Old sum of ages = 5 × 30 = 150
New sum = 150 - 40 + 20 = 130
New average = 130/5 = 26

Shortcut: Change in total = -40 + 20 = -20
Change in average = -20/5 = -4
New average = 30 - 4 = 26
```

## General Intelligence

**Q: Coding-decoding — if PENCIL is coded as RGPEKN, decode MARBLE.**
```
P → R (P+2) | E → G (E+2) | N → P (N+2) | C → E (C+2) | I → K (I+2) | L → N (L+2)
Pattern: each letter shifted +2 in alphabet

MARBLE: M+2=O | A+2=C | R+2=T | B+2=D | L+2=N | E+2=G
MARBLE → OCDTNG
```

## General Awareness

**Q: What is the gauge used in Indian Railways? What are different gauges?**
```
GAUGE = distance between the two rails

Indian Railways gauges:
  Broad Gauge (BG): 1676 mm (5 ft 6 in) — STANDARD in India, 90% of network
  Metre Gauge (MG): 1000 mm (1 metre) — being converted to BG (Project Unigauge)
  Narrow Gauge (NG): 762 mm or 610 mm — hill railways, Darjeeling, Kalka-Shimla

World standard gauge (used in Europe): 1435 mm
India uses broader gauge for stability in subcontinent conditions

Fastest train: Vande Bharat Express (160 km/h design speed)
Longest platform: Hubballi (1,505 m)
First railway in India: Bombay (VT) to Thane, 1853 (34 km)
```
