# JEE Physics Certification Guide

## JEE Overview

| Exam | Authority | Marks | Duration |
|------|-----------|-------|----------|
| **JEE Main** | NTA | 300 (PCM) | 3 hours |
| **JEE Advanced** | IIT (rotating) | 360 | 6 hours (2 papers) |

Physics carries 100 marks in JEE Main and 120 marks in JEE Advanced.

---

## JEE Physics Syllabus

```
CLASS 11 TOPICS:
  Kinematics: equations of motion, projectile, relative motion, graphs
  Laws of Motion: Newton's laws, friction, circular motion, pseudo force
  Work, Energy, Power: work-energy theorem, conservative forces, power
  Rotational Motion: torque, moment of inertia, angular momentum, rolling
  Gravitation: Kepler's laws, orbital velocity, escape velocity, satellites
  Properties of Matter: elasticity, viscosity, surface tension, Bernoulli
  Thermal Physics: thermodynamics laws, kinetic theory, heat transfer

CLASS 12 TOPICS:
  Electrostatics: Coulomb, Gauss, potential, capacitors, dielectrics
  Current Electricity: Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's laws, Wheatstone bridge
  Magnetic Effects: Biot-Savart, Ampere, force on conductor, cyclotron
  EMI and AC: Faraday, Lenz, inductance, AC circuits, resonance, transformer
  Optics: reflection, refraction, lenses, mirrors, interference, diffraction
  Modern Physics: photoelectric effect, de Broglie, Bohr model, radioactivity
  Semiconductor: p-n junction, diode, transistor (CE configuration)
```

## High-Yield JEE Physics Topics

```
MOST TESTED IN JEE MAIN (2019-2024 analysis):
  1. Rotational mechanics (10-15 marks consistently)
  2. Electrostatics + Capacitors (10-12 marks)
  3. Current electricity (8-10 marks)
  4. Optics — ray + wave (8-10 marks)
  5. Modern physics (8-10 marks)

KEY FORMULAS YOU MUST MEMORISE:
  Projectile: Range = v²sin2θ/g | Max height = v²sin²θ/2g
  Circular: centripetal a = v²/r = ω²r | Tension at top: T = mv²/r - mg
  
  Capacitors: Series: 1/C = Σ(1/Cᵢ) | Parallel: C = ΣCᵢ
  Energy: U = ½CV² = Q²/2C
  
  Ohm's law circuits: V = IR | P = I²R = V²/R
  Kirchhoff: ΣI = 0 (junction) | ΣV = 0 (loop)
  
  Lens formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f | Magnification: m = v/u
  
  Photoelectric: KEmax = hf - φ | λ = h/p (de Broglie)
  Bohr: rn = n²a₀ | En = -13.6/n² eV
  Radioactivity: N = N₀e^(-λt) | T½ = 0.693/λ
```

## Revision Notes
```
JEE MAIN: 20 questions (integer + MCQ), 100 marks, 30 min time budget
JEE ADVANCED: 2 papers, mixed question types (multi-correct, paragraph based, integer)

HIGH PRIORITY: Rotational mechanics | Electrostatics | Modern Physics | Optics
PROBLEM APPROACH: draw diagram → identify knowns → apply formula → check units

BOOKS: HC Verma Concepts of Physics (Vol 1 and 2) — MUST | DC Pandey for practice
Irodov Problems (optional, for JEE Advanced level)
```
