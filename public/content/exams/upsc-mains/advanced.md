# UPSC Mains Advanced Preparation

## Answer Writing Techniques

```
STRUCTURE FOR 15-MARK ANSWER (250 words):
  Para 1 - Introduction (30-40 words):
    Define the term / give context / state the issue
    Example: "Constitutional morality, as articulated by Dr. Ambedkar..."
  
  Para 2-4 - Body (150-170 words):
    2-4 dimensions: historical, constitutional, social, economic, political
    Use: examples, data, committee reports, Supreme Court judgments
    Format: sub-headings for long answers make reading easier
  
  Para 5 - Conclusion (30-40 words):
    Way forward / balanced assessment / positive note
    Avoid: personal opinions, casual tone, conclusion without substance

10-MARK ANSWER (150 words): Same structure, compressed
  Intro (20 words) + Body (100 words, 2-3 dimensions) + Conclusion (30 words)

KEYWORDS THAT EXAMINERS LOOK FOR:
  GS1: trajectory, continuity, transformation, evolution, synthesis
  GS2: federal structure, constitutional provisions, policy gaps, reforms needed
  GS3: sustainable development, inclusive growth, technological intervention
  GS4: ethical dimension, stakeholders, public interest, integrity, dilemma

COMMON MISTAKES:
  Listing facts without analysis (most common)
  No conclusion / vague conclusion
  Missing "way forward" component
  Factual errors in data (cite approximate or avoid specific numbers)
  Too long introduction (examiners want substance quickly)
```

## GS4 Ethics Deep Dive

```
MORAL THINKERS (frequently asked):
  Aristotle: virtue ethics, golden mean, eudaimonia (flourishing)
  Kant: categorical imperative (act as if your action could be universal law)
  Bentham/Mill: utilitarianism (greatest good for greatest number)
  Gandhi: truth (satya) and non-violence (ahimsa), trusteeship theory
  Rawls: justice as fairness, veil of ignorance, difference principle
  Kohlberg: stages of moral development (pre-conventional, conventional, post-conventional)

EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE (frequently asked):
  Goleman's 5 components: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills
  Application in public service: dealing with citizen grievances, team management
  EQ vs IQ: both needed for effective administration, EQ more important for leadership

CASE STUDY APPROACH:
  Identify all stakeholders (citizens, govt, officers, private parties)
  List all ethical dimensions (conflict of interest, fairness, transparency)
  Identify options available and consequences of each
  Choose ethically sound option with justification
  Common dilemmas: whistleblowing, following illegal orders, corruption, media pressure
```
