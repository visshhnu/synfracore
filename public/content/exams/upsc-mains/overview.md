# UPSC Mains — Overview

## What is UPSC Mains?

UPSC Civil Services Mains is the second stage of India's most prestigious exam — the Civil Services Examination (CSE). It qualifies candidates for IAS (Indian Administrative Service), IPS (Indian Police Service), IFS (Indian Foreign Service), and other Group A/B central services.

## GS Paper Topics in Detail

**GS1 (250 marks):**
- Modern Indian History (1857 to Independence)
- Post-Independence consolidation and reorganization
- World History (18th-20th century)
- Indian Society: diversity, women, poverty, urbanization
- Geography: physical, Indian, world economic geography

**GS2 (250 marks):**
- Indian Constitution: features, amendments, schedules
- Parliament, state legislatures, executive, judiciary
- Governance: transparency, RTI, e-governance
- Social justice: welfare schemes, vulnerable sections
- International Relations: India and its neighbors, global bodies (UN, WTO, WHO)

**GS3 (250 marks):**
- Indian Economy: planning, growth, development
- Agriculture: land reforms, food security, crop patterns
- Infrastructure: energy, roads, ports, airports
- Environment & Ecology: biodiversity, climate change
- Science & Technology: space, defence, biotechnology
- Internal security: extremism, cyber security

**GS4 (250 marks):**
- Ethics and Human Interface
- Attitude, Aptitude, Foundational Values
- Emotional Intelligence
- Case Studies (very important — 2-3 case studies, 25 marks each)
- Public administration values, integrity, accountability

## Answer Writing Strategy

Unlike Prelims, Mains tests your ability to articulate — both knowledge AND expression matter.

Key rules:
1. **Structure** — Introduction (1 para) → Body (3-5 paras) → Conclusion
2. **Headings and subheadings** — examiners appreciate organized answers
3. **Data and examples** — schemes, committees, constitutional provisions, international comparisons
4. **Diagrams** — flowcharts and maps where relevant
5. **Word limit** — 10-mark questions: 150 words; 15-mark: 200 words; 20-mark: 250 words

## Resources
- **NCERT books** (6-12th) — foundation for all GS papers
- **The Hindu newspaper** — daily reading essential
- **Vision IAS / Insights on India** — monthly current affairs compilation
- **Vajiram & Ravi** — standard notes widely used
- **Previous year questions (PYQs)** — most important, understand what's being asked
