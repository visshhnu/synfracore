# NEET Biology — Previous Year Pattern Questions

## Cell Biology & Cell Division

**Q1.** The powerhouse of the cell is:
(a) Nucleus    (b) Ribosome    **(c) Mitochondria**    (d) Golgi apparatus

Mitochondria produce ATP through oxidative phosphorylation. Have their own DNA (circular, like prokaryotes) — supports endosymbiotic theory.

---

**Q2.** During which phase of mitosis do chromosomes align at the cell equator?
(a) Prophase    (b) Anaphase    **(c) Metaphase**    (d) Telophase

Metaphase: chromosomes at metaphase plate. Spindle fibres attach to centromeres. Best phase for karyotyping — chromosomes most condensed and distinct.

---

**Q3.** Which of the following is NOT a function of the smooth endoplasmic reticulum?
(a) Lipid synthesis
(b) Detoxification of drugs
(c) Calcium storage in muscle cells
**(d) Protein synthesis**

Protein synthesis occurs at ribosomes on rough ER. Smooth ER: lipids, steroids, detox, calcium.

## Genetics & Molecular Biology

**Q4.** In a dihybrid cross between RRYY and rryy (R=round, r=wrinkled, Y=yellow, y=green), what proportion of F2 shows round yellow phenotype?
**(a) 9/16**    (b) 3/16    (c) 7/16    (d) 1/16

Classic 9:3:3:1 ratio. Round Yellow = 9/16.

---

**Q5.** The process of gene expression involves:
(a) Replication → Transcription → Translation
**(b) Transcription → Translation**
(c) Translation → Transcription
(d) Replication → Translation

DNA → mRNA (Transcription) → Protein (Translation). Replication duplicates DNA, not gene expression.

---

**Q6.** Which enzyme is responsible for DNA replication?
(a) RNA polymerase    **(b) DNA polymerase III**    (c) Helicase    (d) Ligase

DNA polymerase III is the main replication enzyme in prokaryotes. DNA polymerase I replaces RNA primers. Helicase unwinds. Ligase joins Okazaki fragments.

## Plant Physiology

**Q7.** Which plant hormone is responsible for apical dominance?
**(a) Auxin (IAA)**    (b) Cytokinin    (c) Gibberellin    (d) Ethylene

Auxins produced at apical bud suppress lateral bud growth (apical dominance). Removing apex allows lateral buds to grow (used in pruning/topping plants).

---

**Q8.** The site of photosynthesis in plants is:
(a) Mitochondria    **(b) Chloroplast**    (c) Nucleus    (d) Vacuole

Light reactions in thylakoid membranes. Calvin cycle (dark reactions) in stroma. Chlorophyll a is the primary pigment; Chlorophyll b, carotenoids are accessory.

---

## Human Physiology

**Q9.** Normal blood pH range is:
(a) 6.8–7.0    **(b) 7.35–7.45**    (c) 7.8–8.0    (d) 6.5–7.0

pH < 7.35 = acidosis (respiratory or metabolic). pH > 7.45 = alkalosis. Maintained by bicarbonate buffer system.

---

**Q10.** The pacemaker of the heart is:
(a) Bundle of His    (b) AV node    **(c) SA node (Sinoatrial node)**    (d) Purkinje fibres

SA node generates 60-100 impulses/minute. AV node (40-60/min) is backup pacemaker. Purkinje fibres distribute impulse to ventricles.
