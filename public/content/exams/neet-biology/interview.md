# NEET Biology — Important Questions

**What is the difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells?**
Prokaryotic cells (bacteria, cyanobacteria) have no membrane-bound nucleus — DNA floats freely as a circular chromosome in the nucleoid region. They lack membrane-bound organelles, have 70S ribosomes, and are generally smaller (1-10 μm). Cell wall contains peptidoglycan. Eukaryotic cells (plants, animals, fungi, protists) have a true nucleus bounded by a double membrane. They contain membrane-bound organelles (mitochondria, ER, Golgi), have 80S ribosomes (except mitochondria/chloroplasts which have 70S), and are larger (10-100 μm).

**Explain Mendel's Laws of Inheritance.**
Law of Dominance: in F1 heterozygotes, only one trait (dominant) expresses; the other (recessive) is masked. Law of Segregation: allele pairs separate during gamete formation so each gamete carries one allele. Proven by F2 ratio of 3:1 for monohybrid cross. Law of Independent Assortment: genes for different traits assort independently during gamete formation — applicable when genes are on different chromosomes. Proven by 9:3:3:1 ratio in dihybrid cross.

**What is the Central Dogma of Molecular Biology?**
DNA → (Transcription) → mRNA → (Translation) → Protein. DNA is transcribed into mRNA in the nucleus by RNA polymerase. mRNA travels to ribosomes in cytoplasm where translation occurs — tRNA molecules bring amino acids that match mRNA codons, and ribosomes join them into a polypeptide chain. Reverse transcription (RNA → DNA, done by retroviruses like HIV) is an exception.

**Explain the nitrogen cycle.**
Nitrogen fixation: atmospheric N₂ → NH₃ by Rhizobium (symbiotic in root nodules) and Azotobacter (free-living). Nitrification: NH₃ → NO₂ (Nitrosomonas) → NO₃ (Nitrobacter). Assimilation: plants absorb NO₃, animals eat plants. Ammonification: decomposers break down organic nitrogen → NH₃. Denitrification: NO₃ → N₂ (Pseudomonas, Thiobacillus) — returns N₂ to atmosphere.
