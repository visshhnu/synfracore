# NEET Biology — Overview

## What is NEET?

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is the single national entrance exam for MBBS, BDS, AYUSH, and other medical programs in India. Conducted by NTA (National Testing Agency), it is the most competitive exam in India with 20+ lakh candidates and limited seats.

## NEET Exam Pattern

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3 hours 20 minutes |
| **Total Marks** | 720 |
| **Questions** | 180 (90 Biology, 45 Physics, 45 Chemistry) |
| **Marking** | +4 correct, -1 wrong |
| **Mode** | Offline (pen and paper) |

## Biology Weightage in NEET: 360 Marks (50% of total!)

Biology is the most important subject in NEET. A high score in Biology can compensate for average performance in Physics/Chemistry.

### Biology Sections
- **Botany**: 45 questions (180 marks)
- **Zoology**: 45 questions (180 marks)

## Study Strategy

**Focus on NCERT:** NEET Biology questions are 90%+ directly from NCERT textbooks (Class 11 and 12). Every diagram, every table, every example in NCERT has been asked in NEET.

**Must-do chapters:**
- Genetics: Mendelian genetics, linkage, mutation
- Evolution: Darwinism, evidence, human evolution
- Human Physiology: all systems (circulatory, excretory, nervous, reproductive)
- Ecology: ecosystems, biodiversity, environmental issues
- Cell Biology: cell division, biomolecules, enzymes
