# UPSC Ethics, Integrity and Aptitude — GS Paper 4

## What is GS Paper 4?

GS Paper 4 tests moral reasoning, not factual recall. It carries 250 marks with two sections:
- Section A: Theory (ethics, integrity, aptitude) — approximately 125 marks
- Section B: Case Studies — approximately 125 marks

## Core Concepts

### Ethics Theories
- **Consequentialism**: Results matter — greatest good for greatest number (Mill, Utilitarianism)
- **Deontology**: Duty-based — moral rules regardless of consequences (Kant, Categorical Imperative)
- **Virtue Ethics**: Character-based — what a virtuous person would do (Aristotle)

### Integrity in Public Service
- **Probity**: Uprightness and honesty in official dealings
- **Objectivity**: Decisions based on merit, not personal interest
- **Accountability**: Responsible to the public and superior authority
- **Transparency**: Open decision-making processes
- **Non-partisanship**: Free from political bias

### Emotional Intelligence
Four components: Self-awareness, Self-management, Social awareness, Relationship management.

## Case Study Answer Format

1. Identify all ethical issues and conflicts
2. List stakeholders and how each is affected
3. Present 3-4 options with pros and cons
4. Choose best course of action with ethical justification
5. Address short-term and long-term consequences
