# State PSC Exams — Complete Guide

## What are State PSC Exams?

Every Indian state has a Public Service Commission (PSC) that recruits for Group I, II, and III posts in the state government. These are among the most prestigious jobs in each state.

## Common Exam Pattern (Group I Level)

### Preliminary Examination
- General Studies: 150–200 MCQs, 2–2.5 hours
- Topics: History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Science & Technology, Current Affairs, State-specific GK
- Qualifying: 60–65% for General, 55–60% for reserved categories

### Main Examination (Written — Descriptive)
**Paper 1: General Studies I** (History, Geography, Social Issues)
**Paper 2: General Studies II** (Polity, Governance, Economy)
**Paper 3: General Studies III** (Technology, Environment, Disaster Management)
**Paper 4: General Studies IV** (Ethics, Integrity, Aptitude)
**Optional Paper 1 & 2** (Subject chosen by candidate — literature, history, public admin, economics, etc.)
**Language Paper** (State language + English)
**Essay** (1–2 essays on social/contemporary topics)

### Interview/Personality Test
- 75–100 marks
- Tests personality, communication, awareness of state affairs

## State-Specific Content — What to Prepare

### History
- Ancient history of the state (kingdoms, dynasties, archaeological sites)
- Medieval history (local rulers, invasions)
- Modern history (freedom movement, notable personalities from the state)
- Post-independence development in the state

### Geography
- Physical features (rivers, mountains, forests, soil types)
- Administrative divisions (districts, taluks/tehsils)
- Agriculture (main crops, irrigation projects)
- Industry and economy of the state

### Polity (State-specific)
- State Legislature (Assembly, Council if any)
- High Court jurisdiction, District Courts
- Governor's role and powers
- State-specific constitutional provisions
- Panchayati Raj in the state

### Current Affairs (State-focused)
- Budget of the state (major schemes, allocations)
- Schemes launched by state government
- Notable appointments (CM, Ministers, Chief Secretary, DGP)
- Major projects (infrastructure, welfare)
- State economy (GDP contribution, industries)

## Preparation Strategy

**UPSC + State = Efficient Preparation**

70% of the syllabus overlaps with UPSC Civil Services. Preparing for UPSC gives a strong foundation for State PSC exams.

**State-specific additions (30%):**
1. State history and culture (1–2 books specific to your state)
2. State geography (atlases and official data)
3. State government schemes (official website + newspapers)
4. State language paper (practice writing, comprehension, grammar)

**Resources:**
- Previous 10 years question papers (your state PSC website)
- State government official website (for schemes, policies)
- State-specific books published by local publishers
- National newspapers + 1 state/regional newspaper

**Key difference from UPSC Mains:** State PSC mains tests more state-specific knowledge. Generic UPSC answers about national schemes need to be supplemented with state implementation details.
