# UPSC Prelims — Previous Year Pattern Questions

## GS Paper 1 — Pattern Questions by Topic

### Indian History & Culture

**Q1.** The Doctrine of Lapse, introduced by Lord Dalhousie, was primarily used to:
(a) Expand British trade in India
(b) Annex states where rulers died without natural male heirs
(c) Suppress the 1857 Revolt
(d) Introduce English education

**Answer: (b)** States like Satara (1848), Jhansi (1853), Nagpur (1854) were annexed under this doctrine.

---

**Q2.** The Poona Pact (1932) was an agreement between:
(a) Gandhi and Jinnah
(b) Gandhi and Lord Irwin
(c) Gandhi and Ambedkar
(d) Congress and Muslim League

**Answer: (c)** Ended Gandhi's fast against Communal Award; provided reserved seats for depressed classes within general electorate.

---

**Q3.** Which of the following is correctly matched?
(a) Battle of Plassey — 1764
(b) Battle of Buxar — 1757
(c) Third Battle of Panipat — 1761
(d) Battle of Seringapatam — 1799 (First)

**Answer: (c)** Third Battle of Panipat (1761) — Marathas vs Ahmad Shah Abdali. Plassey=1757, Buxar=1764.

---

### Indian Polity

**Q4.** Which Article of the Indian Constitution deals with the procedure for amendment?
(a) Article 356    (b) Article 352    (c) Article 368    **(d) Article 370**

Wait — (c) is correct: Article 368 — Amendment procedure. Article 370 was J&K special status (now abrogated).

**Answer: (c)** Article 368 provides for amendment of the Constitution.

---

**Q5.** Consider the following statements about the DPSP:
1. They are enforceable by courts
2. They are fundamental in the governance of the country
3. The state shall endeavour to apply them while making laws

Which is/are correct?
(a) 1 only    (b) 1 and 2    (c) 2 and 3    (d) 1, 2, and 3

**Answer: (c)** DPSPs are NOT enforceable (courts cannot compel), but are fundamental in governance.

---

### Indian Economy

**Q6.** "Stagflation" refers to:
(a) High inflation + high growth
(b) Low inflation + low growth
(c) High inflation + economic stagnation (low growth + high unemployment)
(d) Deflation + economic growth

**Answer: (c)** Stagflation is a difficult economic condition — conventional tools to fight inflation worsen unemployment.

---

**Q7.** The Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) rate is:
(a) Lower than the repo rate
(b) Equal to the repo rate
(c) Higher than the repo rate
(d) Equal to the reverse repo rate

**Answer: (c)** MSF = Repo + 25 bps. Banks borrow from MSF under stressed conditions, pledging G-Secs.

---

### Environment & Ecology

**Q8.** Which of the following is/are correctly matched?
1. Ramsar Convention — Wetland conservation
2. CITES — Trade in endangered species
3. Basel Convention — Hazardous waste
4. Kyoto Protocol — Ozone layer protection

(a) 1, 2, 3    (b) 2, 3, 4    (c) 1, 3, 4    (d) 1, 2, 3, 4

**Answer: (a)** Kyoto Protocol is about greenhouse gases/climate, NOT ozone. Montreal Protocol = ozone.

---

**Q9.** The term "carbon sequestration" refers to:
(a) Releasing CO2 into atmosphere
(b) Capturing and storing atmospheric CO2
(c) Measuring carbon in atmosphere
(d) Trading carbon credits

**Answer: (b)** Forests, oceans, soil absorb/store CO2. Carbon capture and storage (CCS) is artificial sequestration.

**Q11.** A passage comprehension question — Read the following and answer:

"The concept of sustainable development recognises that economic growth must be balanced with environmental protection. The 1987 Brundtland Report defined it as 'development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.'"

Which best captures the main idea?
(a) Economic growth should be stopped
(b) Environmental protection is more important than development
(c) Present and future needs must both be considered in development planning
(d) The 1987 report failed to address development concerns

**Answer: (c)** Directly stated in the passage — intergenerational equity.

## Scoring Strategy

- 200 questions in GS Paper 1 (2 hours) — impossible to attempt all
- Target: 80–90 questions with 85%+ accuracy > 100 questions with 60% accuracy
- Negative marking: -1/3 for wrong answer
- Cut-off typically: 100–110 out of 200 for General category
- Skip if less than 60% confident — eliminate 2 options first, then decide
