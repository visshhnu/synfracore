# NEET Chemistry — Fundamentals

## Physical Chemistry

```
MOLE CONCEPT:
  1 mole = 6.022×10²³ particles (Avogadro's number)
  Molar mass = mass of 1 mole in grams
  Moles = mass/molar mass = volume(L)/22.4 (at STP, gas)
  Molarity M = moles/litre | Molality m = moles/kg solvent
  Normality N = equivalents/litre | Mole fraction x = nₐ/(nₐ+n_b)

ATOMIC STRUCTURE:
  Bohr's model: Energy = -13.6/n² eV (hydrogen)
  Quantum numbers: n (shell), l (subshell, 0 to n-1),
                   mₗ (orbital, -l to +l), mₛ (±½)
  Aufbau: fill lowest energy first
  Pauli exclusion: max 2 electrons/orbital, opposite spins
  Hund's rule: max unpaired electrons in degenerate orbitals
  Order: 1s 2s 2p 3s 3p 4s 3d 4p 5s 4d 5p...

CHEMICAL BONDING:
  Ionic: high electronegativity difference (>1.7) → transfer of electrons
  Covalent: sharing electrons
  Bond order = (bonding - antibonding electrons)/2
  VSEPR: shape determined by electron pairs around central atom
    2 BP: linear (BeCl₂) | 3 BP: trigonal planar (BF₃)
    4 BP: tetrahedral (CH₄) | 3 BP + 1 LP: pyramidal (NH₃)
    2 BP + 2 LP: bent (H₂O) | 5 BP: trigonal bipyramidal (PCl₅)
    6 BP: octahedral (SF₆)
```

## Organic Chemistry Basics

```
General Organic Chemistry (GOC):
  Hybridization: sp³ (tetrahedral, 109.5°), sp² (planar, 120°), sp (linear, 180°)
  Inductive effect: electron withdrawal (-I) or donation (+I) through σ bonds
    -I order: -NO₂ > -CN > -F > -Cl > -Br > -I > -OR > -OH > -R
    +I order: -C(CH₃)₃ > -CH(CH₃)₂ > -CH₂CH₃ > -CH₃
  Mesomeric effect: through π bonds/lone pairs (resonance)
  Carbocation stability: 3°>2°>1° (hyperconjugation + inductive)
  Carbanion stability: 1°>2°>3° (opposite, inductive destabilizes)
  Free radical: 3°>2°>1° (hyperconjugation)

ISOMERISM:
  Structural: chain, position, functional group, metamerism, tautomerism
  Stereoisomerism: geometrical (cis-trans), optical (chirality)
    Chiral carbon: 4 different groups attached
    Enantiomers: non-superimposable mirror images (rotate plane polarized light)
    R/S configuration: CIP priority rules
```
