# NEET Chemistry — Important Questions

**What is Hund's rule of maximum multiplicity?**
When electrons are filling degenerate orbitals (same energy, e.g., three 2p orbitals), one electron enters each orbital before any orbital gets a second electron. All unpaired electrons in such a configuration have the same spin (parallel). This gives the maximum spin multiplicity and the lowest energy state. Example: carbon has configuration 1s² 2s² 2p² — the two 2p electrons are in separate 2p orbitals with parallel spins, not paired in one 2p orbital.

**Explain Le Chatelier's Principle with an example.**
When a system at equilibrium is disturbed (by changing concentration, pressure, or temperature), it shifts to oppose the change and re-establish equilibrium. Example: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ + heat. Increasing N₂ → shifts forward (more NH₃). Increasing pressure → shifts to side with fewer moles (forward, 4 moles → 2 moles). Increasing temperature → shifts backward (reverse is endothermic, absorbs heat). These principles guide industrial optimization — Haber process uses high pressure, moderate temperature.

**What is Markovnikov's rule and what is its exception?**
Markovnikov's rule: in addition of HX to an asymmetric alkene, hydrogen adds to the carbon with more hydrogen atoms (or equivalently, the halide adds to the more substituted carbon). Reason: more substituted carbocation is more stable. Example: CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr → CH₃CHBrCH₃ (2-bromopropane). Exception (Anti-Markovnikov/Peroxide effect): in presence of organic peroxides, HBr adds in reverse — via free radical mechanism. Only HBr shows this (not HCl or HI due to bond energies).

**What is the difference between a galvanic cell and electrolytic cell?**
Galvanic (Voltaic) cell: converts chemical energy to electrical energy spontaneously. Reaction has negative ΔG, positive EMF. Anode is negative (oxidation), cathode is positive (reduction). Example: Daniel cell (Zn/ZnSO₄ || CuSO₄/Cu). Electrolytic cell: uses electrical energy to drive non-spontaneous chemical reactions. Needs external power source. Cathode is negative (reduction), anode is positive (oxidation). Examples: electroplating, electrolysis of water, chlor-alkali process.
