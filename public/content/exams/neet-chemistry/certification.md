# NEET Chemistry Certification Guide

## NEET Chemistry Overview

Chemistry in NEET carries 180 marks (45 questions × 4 marks). It is split into Physical Chemistry (~25%), Organic Chemistry (~35%), and Inorganic Chemistry (~40% — NEET has higher inorganic weightage than JEE).

## Syllabus

```
PHYSICAL CHEMISTRY (class 11 + 12):
  Basic Concepts: mole concept, stoichiometry, empirical/molecular formula
  Atomic Structure: Bohr model, quantum numbers, electronic configuration
  Chemical Bonding: ionic, covalent, VSEPR, hybridisation, MO theory
  States of Matter: ideal gas laws (PV=nRT), kinetic theory, real gases
  Thermodynamics: enthalpy, entropy, Gibbs energy, spontaneity
  Equilibrium: Kc, Kp, Le Chatelier, acid-base equilibrium, buffer
  Redox Reactions: oxidation states, balancing by oxidation number method
  Electrochemistry: EMF, Nernst equation, Faraday's laws, corrosion
  Chemical Kinetics: rate laws, order, molecularity, Arrhenius equation
  Solutions: colligative properties, Henry's law, vapour pressure
  Surface Chemistry: adsorption, catalysis, colloids, emulsions

INORGANIC CHEMISTRY (heavy in NEET):
  Classification of Elements: periodic trends, blocks
  Hydrogen: position in periodic table, isotopes, water, H₂O₂
  s-block: Li, Na, K, Mg, Ca, Ba — properties, compounds, anomalous behaviour
  p-block (Class 11): Groups 13-14 — boron family, carbon family
  p-block (Class 12): Groups 15-18 — N, P, O, S, Halogens, Noble gases
  d-block: transition metals, alloys, potassium dichromate, permanganate
  f-block: lanthanides and actinides (general properties only)
  Coordination Compounds: nomenclature, bonding theories, isomerism
  Metallurgy: ore concentration, extraction, purification methods

ORGANIC CHEMISTRY:
  Basic Principles: IUPAC naming, isomerism, electronic effects
  Hydrocarbons: alkanes (combustion, halogenation), alkenes (addition), alkynes
  Environmental Chemistry: types of pollution, smog, acid rain
  Haloalkanes and Haloarenes: SN1, SN2, elimination
  Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers: reactions and distinctions
  Aldehydes and Ketones: nucleophilic addition, oxidation, reduction
  Carboxylic Acids: acidity, reactions, derivatives
  Amines: basicity, reactions, Diazonium salts
  Biomolecules: carbohydrates, proteins, nucleic acids, vitamins, hormones
  Polymers: types, examples, uses
```

## High Yield NEET Chemistry

```
MOST MARKS (2019-2024 analysis):
  Inorganic p-block: 8-12 marks — highly factual from NCERT
  Organic reactions: 10-15 marks — mechanisms and name reactions
  Coordination compounds: 5-8 marks — IUPAC naming, VBT, CFT
  Thermodynamics + Equilibrium: 6-8 marks
  Biomolecules + Polymers: 4-6 marks — NCERT direct questions
```

## Revision Notes
```
NCERT INORGANIC = NEET INORGANIC: almost entirely from NCERT
Read NCERT Chemistry Part 1 and 2 multiple times
Every reaction, every property, every use mentioned = potential question

ORGANIC: understand mechanism → easier than memorising each reaction
PHYSICAL: calculation practice essential (equilibrium, kinetics, electrochemistry)

LAST 30 DAYS: revise inorganic daily (fades fastest from memory)
BOOKS: NCERT + MTG Objective Chemistry + Narendra Awasthi (Physical)
PYQ: 2010-2024 chemistry papers mandatory
```
