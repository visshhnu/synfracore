# NEET Mock Tests and Revision Strategy

## Why Mock Tests are Critical

NEET has 720 marks, 180 questions, 3 hours, and negative marking. Speed and accuracy both matter. Candidates who attempt 30+ full mocks typically score 60+ marks higher than those who only study subjects.

## Mock Test Schedule

| Phase | Activity | Frequency |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 3 months before | Chapter-wise tests (30 min) | Daily |
| 2 months before | Subject-wise tests (1 hour) + 2 full mocks per week | Daily |
| 1 month before | Full mocks only (3 hours) | 1 mock per day |

## Analyzing Every Mock (Most Skipped Step)

For every wrong answer, record:
1. Topic
2. Why wrong: Concept unclear / Silly mistake / Guessed / Negative marked unnecessarily
3. What to revise

After 10 mocks, identify your top 5 weak topics and schedule focused revision.

## Subject-wise Revision Strategy

### Biology (360 marks — 50% of NEET)
Pure memorization plus application. Use spaced repetition:
- Day 0: Study fresh
- Day 1: 30-minute revision
- Day 3: 15-minute revision
- Day 7: 10-minute revision
- Day 21: Quick glance

### Physics (Key chapters)
Laws of Motion, Optics, Modern Physics, Thermodynamics. Keep a handwritten formula sheet per chapter.

### Chemistry (Key areas)
- Organic: Named reactions (Aldol, Cannizaro, Sandmeyer, Reimer-Tiemann)
- Inorganic: p-Block, d-Block, Coordination compounds
- Physical: Electrochemistry, Chemical Kinetics
