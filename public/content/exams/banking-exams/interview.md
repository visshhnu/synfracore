# Banking Exams (SBI/IBPS/RBI) — Key Topics

## Quantitative Aptitude

**Q: A sum doubles in 8 years at SI. At what rate?**
```
Simple Interest: SI = PRT/100
Sum doubles means SI = P (principal)
P = PRT/100 → 1 = RT/100 → R = 100/T = 100/8 = 12.5%
```

**Q: Pipes A and B can fill a tank in 12 and 18 hours. Together?**
```
A's rate = 1/12 per hour | B's rate = 1/18 per hour
Combined rate = 1/12 + 1/18 = 3/36 + 2/36 = 5/36 per hour
Time = 36/5 = 7.2 hours = 7 hours 12 minutes

Tip: If rates are 1/a + 1/b → Time = ab/(a+b)
= 12×18/(12+18) = 216/30 = 7.2 hours ✓
```

**Q: Train 150m long crosses a pole in 15s. Speed in km/h?**
```
Speed = distance/time = 150/15 = 10 m/s
To convert m/s to km/h: multiply by 18/5
= 10 × 18/5 = 36 km/h

Crossing a platform of length L:
Distance = train length + platform length
```

## Reasoning Ability

**Q: In a row of 25 students, Ravi is 10th from left and 18th from right. Row size?**
```
Total = position from left + position from right - 1
= 10 + 18 - 1 = 27
But given 25 students → contradiction. Re-read: he is 10th from left.
Position from right = 25 - 10 + 1 = 16th from right
```

## English Language

**Q: Common error correction patterns in banking exams.**
```
Subject-verb agreement:
  Singular subject → singular verb
  'The team ARE playing' → 'The team IS playing' (collective noun = singular)
  'Neither of them ARE' → 'Neither of them IS' (neither = singular)

Common confusions:
  Affect (verb) vs Effect (noun): 'Rain affects crops' | 'The effect of rain'
  Than vs Then: comparison vs time
  Its vs It's: possessive vs it is
  Principal vs Principle: head of school vs rule

Sentence correction tips:
  Read all options before answering
  Identify the ERROR first (not just 'sounds wrong')
  Common errors: tense, preposition, articles (a/an/the)
```

## Banking Awareness

**Q: What is the CRR and how does RBI use it?**
```
CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio): % of bank's deposits held with RBI
Current CRR (2024): 4%
If deposits = ₹100 crore → must keep ₹4 crore with RBI

RBI uses CRR to control money supply:
  Increase CRR → banks have less to lend → money supply falls → controls inflation
  Decrease CRR → banks have more to lend → money supply rises → stimulates growth

SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio): % kept in liquid assets (gold/govt securities)
Current SLR: 18%
Repo Rate: rate at which banks borrow from RBI
Reverse Repo: rate at which banks park excess with RBI
```
