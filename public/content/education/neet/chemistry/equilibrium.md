# Equilibrium (NEET Chemistry)

## Why This Chapter Matters
Equilibrium has 6-10 marks in NEET Chemistry. Kc, Kp, Le Chatelier's principle, pH calculations, buffer solutions, and Ksp are all tested extensively.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Chemical Equilibrium
For: aA + bB ‚áĆ cC + dD
Kc = [C]^c[D]^d / [A]^a[B]^b (equilibrium constant in terms of concentration)
Kp = Kc(RT)^őĒn (őĒn = moles gas product - moles gas reactant)
Kp = Kc when őĒn = 0

### 2. Le Chatelier's Principle
System opposes any change:
Add reactant ‚Üí forward reaction | Add product ‚Üí backward reaction
Increase pressure ‚Üí side with fewer gas moles
Increase temperature ‚Üí endothermic direction
Catalyst ‚Üí no shift (both directions equally)

### 3. Ionic Equilibrium ‚ÄĒ pH
Kw = [H‚Āļ][OH‚ĀĽ] = 10‚ĀĽ¬Ļ‚Āī at 25¬įC
pH = -log[H‚Āļ] | pOH = -log[OH‚ĀĽ] | pH + pOH = 14

Strong acid HCl (0.1M): [H‚Āļ] = 0.1M ‚Üí pH = 1
Weak acid HA: Ka = [H‚Āļ][A‚ĀĽ]/[HA] | [H‚Āļ] = ‚ąö(Ka √ó C) | pH = ¬Ĺ(pKa - log C)
Buffer: pH = pKa + log([A‚ĀĽ]/[HA]) (Henderson-Hasselbalch)

### 4. Solubility Product (Ksp)
For AgCl ‚áĆ Ag‚Āļ + Cl‚ĀĽ:
Ksp = [Ag‚Āļ][Cl‚ĀĽ] = s¬≤ where s = solubility
Common ion effect: adding Ag‚Āļ or Cl‚ĀĽ reduces solubility of AgCl

For Ag‚āāCrO‚āĄ ‚áĆ 2Ag‚Āļ + CrO‚āĄ¬≤‚ĀĽ:
Ksp = [Ag‚Āļ]¬≤[CrO‚āĄ¬≤‚ĀĽ] = (2s)¬≤(s) = 4s¬≥

## PYQs (NEET)

**NEET 2023:** pH of 0.001M HCl solution?
HCl fully ionises ‚Üí [H‚Āļ] = 0.001 = 10‚ĀĽ¬≥ M ‚Üí pH = 3

**NEET 2022:** For the reaction N‚āā + 3H‚āā ‚áĆ 2NH‚āÉ, Kp < Kc because:
őĒn = 2-4 = -2 (negative) ‚Üí Kp = Kc(RT)^(-2) ‚Üí Kp < Kc

**NEET 2021:** A buffer solution contains weak acid and its salt. This resists pH change because:
When H‚Āļ added ‚Üí reacts with A‚ĀĽ (conjugate base). When OH‚ĀĽ added ‚Üí reacts with HA (weak acid). Both neutralise without significant pH change.
