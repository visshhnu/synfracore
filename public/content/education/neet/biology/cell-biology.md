# Cell — The Unit of Life

## Why This Chapter Matters
Cell biology is the most important chapter for NEET Biology — 8-12 marks every year. Cell organelles, cell wall, plasma membrane, nucleus, and differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells are frequently tested.

## Prerequisites
- Basic cell structure from Class 9 (NCERT)
- Microscopy (light vs electron microscope)

---

## Core Concepts

### 1. Cell Theory
Schleiden (1838) — all plants are made of cells
Schwann (1839) — all animals are made of cells
Virchow (1855) — "Omnis cellula e cellula" — cells arise from pre-existing cells
**Modern Cell Theory:** Cell is the structural and functional unit of life.

### 2. Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

| Feature | Prokaryotic | Eukaryotic |
|---|---|---|
| Nucleus | No true nucleus (nucleoid region) | True membrane-bound nucleus |
| Size | 1-10 μm | 10-100 μm |
| Membrane-bound organelles | Absent | Present (mitochondria, ER, Golgi) |
| Ribosome | 70S (50S+30S) | 80S (60S+40S) |
| DNA | Circular, no histone | Linear, with histones |
| Cell wall | Present (peptidoglycan in bacteria) | Present in plants (cellulose), fungi (chitin), absent in animals |
| Examples | Bacteria, Archaea, Mycoplasma | All other organisms |

### 3. Plasma Membrane (Cell Membrane)

**Fluid Mosaic Model** — Singer and Nicolson (1972):
- Phospholipid bilayer (fluid — can move laterally)
- Proteins embedded (mosaic — scattered proteins)
- Cholesterol — maintains fluidity at different temperatures

**Phospholipid:** Hydrophilic head (faces water) + Hydrophobic tail (faces inward)

**Membrane transport:**
- Passive: diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion (no energy — along concentration gradient)
- Active: pumps (Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase) — against gradient, requires ATP
- Endocytosis: phagocytosis (solid), pinocytosis (liquid)
- Exocytosis: release of materials from cell

### 4. Cell Wall

Plants: Cellulose (primary wall) + Lignin/Suberin/Pectin (secondary wall)
Fungi: Chitin
Bacteria: Peptidoglycan (murein)
Plants have plasmodesmata — cytoplasmic connections through cell walls

### 5. Nucleus

- **Nuclear envelope** = outer + inner nuclear membrane with nuclear pores
- **Nucleoplasm** = fluid inside nucleus
- **Chromatin** = DNA + proteins (histone) — forms chromosomes during cell division
- **Nucleolus** = site of rRNA synthesis and ribosome assembly — disappears during cell division
- **Nuclear matrix** = fibrous network providing structural support

Eukaryotic DNA is linear, associated with histone proteins (H1, H2A, H2B, H3, H4).
**Nucleosome** = 8 histone proteins (octamer) + DNA wrapped around them.

### 6. Mitochondria

- Double membrane (outer smooth, inner folded = cristae)
- Matrix contains: enzymes, circular DNA, 70S ribosomes
- **Site of aerobic respiration** (Krebs cycle in matrix, ETC on inner membrane)
- "Powerhouse of the cell"
- Semiautonomous — own DNA and ribosomes
- Evolutionary origin: endosymbiosis theory (engulfed bacteria)

### 7. Chloroplast

- Double membrane (outer, inner + thylakoid membrane)
- Grana (stack of thylakoids) = light reaction
- Stroma = dark reaction (Calvin cycle)
- Contains: DNA (circular), 70S ribosomes, enzymes
- Found in leaf mesophyll cells, guard cells
- Semiautonomous — own DNA and ribosomes

### 8. Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

**Rough ER:** Ribosomes attached — protein synthesis + modification
**Smooth ER:** No ribosomes — lipid synthesis, detoxification, calcium storage

### 9. Golgi Body (Golgi Complex)

- Stack of membrane-bound sacs (cisternae)
- cis face (receives from ER) → trans face (sends to destination)
- Functions: protein modification, packaging, secretion, lysosome formation
- "Trafficking policeman of the cell"

### 10. Lysosomes

- Contain hydrolytic enzymes (acid hydrolases)
- pH 4.5-5.0 (acidic)
- "Suicide bags" — can digest cell contents (autolysis)
- Formed by Golgi body
- Digest worn-out organelles (autophagy) and foreign particles

### 11. Vacuoles

- Plants: large central vacuole (tonoplast membrane), provides turgor pressure
- Animals: small, temporary food vacuoles or contractile vacuoles

### 12. Cytoskeleton

**Microfilaments:** Actin — cell movement, cytokinesis
**Microtubules:** Tubulin — cilia/flagella formation, spindle fibres, cell shape
**Intermediate filaments:** Give mechanical strength

### 13. Cilia and Flagella

Structure: 9+2 arrangement of microtubules
9 doublet microtubules surrounding 2 central singlet microtubules
Used for movement
Cilia (shorter, many) | Flagella (longer, few or one)

## NEET Important Points

**Smallest cell:** Mycoplasma (PPLO — Pleuropneumonia-like organisms)
**Largest cell:** Ostrich egg
**Longest cell:** Nerve cells (neurons)

**Cell without nucleus:** RBC (mature), platelets, sieve tube cells (phloem)
**Cell with multiple nuclei:** Muscle fibres (syncytium), osteoclasts, liver cells

**Robert Hooke (1665)** — coined the term "cell" while observing cork cells
**Leeuwenhoek** — first observed living cells (bacteria)

## PYQs (NEET)

**NEET 2023:** Which of the following is not a feature of prokaryotic cells?
(A) 70S ribosomes (B) Circular DNA (C) Membrane-bound nucleus (D) Cell wall
Answer: C — Prokaryotes do NOT have a membrane-bound nucleus.

**NEET 2022:** The fluid mosaic model of plasma membrane was proposed by:
(A) Singer and Nicolson (B) Davson and Danielli (C) Robertson (D) Gorter and Grendel
Answer: A

**NEET 2021:** Mitochondrial matrix contains all EXCEPT:
(A) Circular DNA (B) 70S ribosomes (C) Enzymes for Krebs cycle (D) Grana
Answer: D — Grana are found in chloroplasts, NOT in mitochondria.

**NEET 2020:** Which cell organelle is involved in protein sorting and packaging?
(A) ER (B) Golgi apparatus (C) Lysosome (D) Vacuole
Answer: B
