# Data Interpretation

## Why This Chapter Matters
Data Interpretation (DI) carries 10-15 marks in SSC CGL Tier 2 and 5-8 marks in Tier 1. Speed and accuracy in reading charts, tables, pie charts, and bar graphs determine your rank.

## Types of DI Questions

### 1. Tables
Easiest form of DI. Data in rows and columns.
Read row and column headings carefully. Don't confuse rows with columns.
Calculate % change, ratio, average from table values.

### 2. Bar Graphs
Read bar heights carefully. Multiple bars: compare them.
% change = (new-old)/old × 100.

### 3. Pie Charts
Represent % of total. All slices add to 360° or 100%.
Value of a slice = (Angle/360) × Total OR (Percentage/100) × Total.
If total is not given but ratio of two items needed — use percentages directly.

### 4. Line Graphs
Show trends over time. Slope = rate of change.
Steepest rise/fall → maximum rate of change.

### 5. Mixed DI
Two graphs together — table + pie chart or bar + line. Most complex.

## Key Skills

### Approximation
In SSC DI, exact calculation is rarely needed. Round numbers to nearest convenient value.
Example: 4830/97 ≈ 4800/100 = 48. Saves 60 seconds.

### % Calculation Shortcuts
10% of X = X/10 | 5% = X/20 | 25% = X/4 | 33.33% = X/3
For 17% of 342: 17% ≈ 10%+5%+2% = 34.2+17.1+6.84 = 58.14 ≈ 58.

### Ratio Shortcuts
If two quantities are in ratio 3:5, out of total 800:
First = 3/(3+5) × 800 = 300 | Second = 500.

## Solved DI Set

**Table:** Sales of a company (units in thousands)

| Year | Product A | Product B | Total |
|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| 2019 | 120 | 80 | 200 |
| 2020 | 100 | 90 | 190 |
| 2021 | 140 | 110 | 250 |
| 2022 | 150 | 130 | 280 |
| 2023 | 160 | 120 | 280 |

Q1: % increase in Total sales from 2019 to 2022?
= (280-200)/200 × 100 = 40%

Q2: In which year was Product B's share of total highest?
2020: 90/190=47.4% | 2021: 110/250=44% | 2022: 130/280=46.4% | 2023: 120/280=42.9%.
Highest: 2020 (47.4%)

Q3: Average sales of Product A over all 5 years?
(120+100+140+150+160)/5 = 670/5 = 134 thousand.

Q4: Ratio of Product A to Product B in 2021?
140:110 = 14:11.

## PYQs (SSC)

**SSC CGL 2023 (Pie Chart):** A pie chart shows expenditure. Education=72°, Food=144°, Others=144°. If total expenditure is Rs 50,000, find education spend.
Education = (72/360)×50000 = 0.2×50000 = Rs 10,000.

**SSC CHSL 2022 (Bar graph):** Production in 2020=500, 2021=600. % increase?
= (600-500)/500 × 100 = 20%.

## Revision Notes
```
DI STRATEGY:
1. Read all headings and units first (before questions)
2. Identify what each question asks (%, ratio, absolute, average)
3. Use approximation wherever possible
4. Check if total is given or calculable

KEY FORMULAS:
% change = (new-old)/old × 100
Pie chart value = (angle/360) × total = (%/100) × total
Average = sum/count
Ratio x:y out of total T → first = xT/(x+y)

APPROXIMATION TIPS:
Always check if options are far apart (large gap → rough calculation fine)
If options close → precise calculation needed
Round intermediate values to nearest 5 or 10
```
