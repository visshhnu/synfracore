# Reading Comprehension and Cloze Test for SSC

## Why This Chapter Matters
RC and Cloze Test together give 10-15 marks in SSC CGL Tier 1 and up to 40 marks in Tier 2 English paper. The SSC pattern uses medium-difficulty passages with direct factual and mild inference questions.

## Reading Comprehension — SSC Pattern

### What SSC RC Tests
1. Direct factual questions (most common) — answer directly in passage
2. Inference questions — not stated but implied
3. Vocabulary in context — meaning of underlined word
4. Title/Main idea — what the passage is about
5. Author's tone — critical, appreciative, neutral, concerned

### SSC-Specific Strategy
SSC RC passages are typically 200-300 words with 5 questions.
Questions are mostly factual — find the exact line, pick the closest option.
Vocabulary questions: use context, not dictionary meaning.
Time: spend max 5-6 minutes per RC set.

### 5-Step Method
1. Read questions first (30 seconds) — know what to search for
2. Skim passage — get topic, structure
3. For each factual question — find the exact paragraph/sentence
4. For inference — what logically follows (eliminate extreme options)
5. Verify by re-reading the relevant sentence

### Common Traps in SSC RC
**Trap 1:** Option uses exact words from passage but changes meaning.
**Trap 2:** Option is true in general but NOT said in the passage.
**Trap 3:** Option answers a slightly different question than asked.
**Trap 4:** "All of the following except" — read all options carefully.

## Cloze Test — SSC Pattern

### What SSC Tests
A passage with 10 blanks. 4 options per blank.
Tests: vocabulary + grammar + context understanding.
New pattern: 5 blanks with sentence-completion options (full sentences, not single words).

### Approach
1. Read full passage for overall meaning
2. Identify part of speech needed (noun/verb/adjective/connector)
3. Check grammar (singular/plural, tense consistency)
4. Check context (does word fit the meaning of surrounding sentences)
5. Eliminate wrong options (wrong grammar or wrong meaning)

### High-Frequency Words in SSC Cloze
**Connectors:** However, Therefore, Moreover, Nevertheless, Consequently, Furthermore, Albeit, Whereas, Hence

**Formal vocabulary:** Endeavour, Proliferate, Advocate, Alleviate, Accumulate, Sustain, Perceive, Constitute, Facilitate, Implement

### Grammar Points in Cloze
**If + present → will (real condition):** If it rains, we will stay.
**If + past → would (hypothetical):** If it rained, we would stay.
**Prepositions after common verbs:** Look INTO, Deal WITH, Result IN, Account FOR, Consist OF

## Solved Examples

### RC Example
**Passage (excerpt):** "The industrial revolution transformed societies from agrarian economies to manufacturing-based economies. While it brought prosperity and technological innovation, it also created significant social challenges including child labour, unsafe working conditions, and environmental degradation."

**Q1:** What was the main effect of the industrial revolution?
Answer: Transformed agrarian economies to manufacturing-based economies. (Direct fact)

**Q2:** Which of these was NOT mentioned as a social challenge?
(A) Child labour (B) Poverty (C) Unsafe conditions (D) Environmental degradation
Answer: B — Poverty is NOT mentioned (unsafe conditions, child labour, and environmental degradation are).

**Q3:** The author's tone toward industrial revolution is:
Answer: Balanced/Objective — mentions both positive (prosperity, innovation) and negative (social challenges).

### Cloze Example
"Education is the most powerful __(1)__ which you can use to change the world. The __(2)__ of education lies not in passing examinations, but in preparing students __(3)__ life's challenges."

(1) weapon/tool/method/art → **weapon** (Nelson Mandela quote, context of power/change)
(2) aim/purpose/requirement/fact → **purpose** (what education is FOR)
(3) for/to/about/regarding → **for** (prepare FOR challenges = correct preposition)

## PYQs (SSC)

**SSC CGL 2023 (Cloze):** "The committee __(blank)__ the proposal unanimously."
(A) accept (B) accepts (C) accepted ✓ (D) accepting
[Past tense matches the rest of the passage]

**SSC CHSL 2022 (RC):** If passage says "The policy was implemented in 2010 and abolished in 2015," the question "For how many years was the policy active?" = 5 years.

## Revision Notes
```
SSC RC STRATEGY:
Read questions → skim → find relevant line → verify
Most questions are FACTUAL (directly in passage)
Inference: what LOGICALLY follows (not what you know from outside)
Vocabulary: use CONTEXT, not dictionary

SSC CLOZE STRATEGY:
Read whole passage first (context)
Part of speech → grammar check → meaning check → eliminate
Common: wrong tense, wrong preposition, wrong meaning

CONNECTORS TO KNOW:
Contrast: however, nevertheless, although, despite, whereas
Addition: moreover, furthermore, additionally, also
Result: therefore, hence, consequently, thus
Concession: albeit, even though, while
```
