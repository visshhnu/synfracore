# UPSC Prelims — Indian History and Polity

## Why This Chapter Matters
History and Polity together account for 40-50 questions in UPSC Prelims GS Paper 1 — the largest single chunk. Modern India's freedom movement and Constitutional provisions are highest-weightage areas.

## Indian History

### Ancient India — High-Yield Facts

**Indus Valley Civilisation (2600-1900 BCE):**
Sites: Harappa (Punjab, Pakistan), Mohenjo-daro (Sindh), Lothal (Gujarat — only port city), Kalibangan (Rajasthan — pre-Harappan + Harappan), Dholavira (Gujarat — only site with sign board inscription), Rakhigarhi (Haryana — largest IVC site in India).
Key features: Town planning, Great Bath (Mohenjo-daro), no iron, bronze age, pictographic script (undeciphered), standardised weights and measures, no temples found, evidence of trade with Mesopotamia.

**Vedic Period (1500-600 BCE):**
Rig Veda: oldest text, 1028 hymns, River Saraswati mentioned (now dried).
Later Vedic: shift from nomadic to settled agriculture; caste system crystallised; Upanishads (philosophy — "Brahma is reality, world is illusion").
16 Mahajanapadas: Magadha rose to prominence.

**Maurya Empire (322-185 BCE):**
Chandragupta Maurya: founded with help of Chanakya (Arthashastra — treatise on statecraft).
Ashoka (268-232 BCE): Kalinga War (261 BCE) → converted to Buddhism → Dhamma (moral code, not Buddhism per se) → rock edicts and pillar edicts across empire → sent missionaries to Sri Lanka, Greece, Egypt.
Megasthenes (Greek ambassador): wrote Indica.

**Post-Maurya Empires:**
Shunga, Satavahana, Kushana (Kanishka — 4th Buddhist council), Gupta.
Gupta Empire (320-550 CE): "Golden Age" — science, art, literature. Aryabhata (zero, pi, heliocentric theory), Kalidasa (Shakuntala, Meghaduta), Varahamihira, Brahmagupta.
Chandra Gupta II (Vikramaditya): Chinese traveller Fa-hien visited.
Hunas invasion → decline of Gupta.

**Chola Empire (South India):**
Rajaraja I, Rajendra I (naval expeditions to Southeast Asia).
Bronze sculptures, temple architecture (Brihadeeswarar temple at Thanjavur).

### Medieval India — High-Yield Facts

**Delhi Sultanate (1206-1526):**
Qutb-ud-din Aibak: 1st Sultan, started Qutb Minar.
Iltutmish: 1st Sultan to receive investiture from Caliph. Completed Qutb Minar.
Razia Sultana: 1st and only woman Sultan.
Alauddin Khilji: Market reforms (4 markets — grain, cloth, cattle, slaves/animals), Khambat conquest.
Muhammad bin Tughlaq: Transfer of capital (Delhi→Daulatabad — failed), Token currency (failed). Called "wisest fool" or "mix of opposites."
Firuz Shah Tughlaq: Welfare works — hospitals, canals, abolition of torture.
Timur's invasion (1398): Delhi sacked, weakened Tughlaq.

**Mughal Empire (1526-1857):**
Babur: First Battle of Panipat (1526) vs Ibrahim Lodi. Used gunpowder.
Humayun: Defeated by Sher Shah Suri, recovered empire.
Sher Shah Suri (1540-1545): Grand Trunk Road, postal system, land revenue reforms (precursor to Mughal system).
Akbar (1556-1605): Din-i-Ilahi (syncretic religion), Ibadat Khana (house of worship — debates), Navratnas (9 gems at court), Fatehpur Sikri, Todar Mal (land revenue), Abul Fazl (Ain-i-Akbari, Akbarnama). Married Jodha Bai (Rajput alliance).
Shah Jahan (1628-1658): Taj Mahal (1632-1653 in memory of Mumtaz Mahal), Red Fort, Jama Masjid.
Aurangzeb (1658-1707): Reimposed Jizya, banned music in court, Deccan campaigns (drained treasury), Hindu temples destroyed. Longest-reigning Mughal but initiated decline.

**Bhakti and Sufi Movements:**
Bhakti: Devotion over ritual, opposed caste. Ramananda, Kabir (both Hinduism-Islam synthesis), Mirabai, Tukaram, Guru Nanak (Sikhism founded), Chaitanya.
Sufi: Islamic mysticism. Orders: Chishti (Moinuddin Chishti, Ajmer — most popular in India), Suhrawardi, Qadiri, Naqshbandi.

### Modern India — Critical for UPSC

**British India Timeline:**
1600: East India Company founded (charter by Elizabeth I).
1757: Battle of Plassey (Clive vs Siraj-ud-Daulah) — British political power begins.
1764: Battle of Buxar — British defeat combined force of Nawab of Oudh, Mughal Emperor, Bengal Nawab → more decisive than Plassey.
1773: Regulating Act — first Parliamentary control of EIC. Warren Hastings became 1st Governor-General.
1793: Permanent Settlement (Cornwallis) — zamindari system in Bengal, Bihar, Odisha.
1829: Sati banned (Lord William Bentinck, after campaign by Ram Mohan Roy).
1853: First railway (Bombay to Thane, 34km) and telegraph.
1857: Sepoy Mutiny / First War of Independence. Centres: Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur (Nana Sahib), Lucknow (Begum Hazrat Mahal), Jhansi (Rani Lakshmibai). Causes: Enfield rifle cartridge (greased with pig/cow fat), annexation policy, doctrine of lapse.
1858: Crown rule replaces EIC. Queen Victoria's Proclamation.
1885: INC founded. A.O. Hume, Dadabhai Naoroji, Dinshaw Wacha. First president: W.C. Bonnerjee. First session: Bombay.
1905: Partition of Bengal (Curzon) → Swadeshi Movement → INC split (Moderates vs Extremists).
1906: Muslim League founded (Dhaka).
1909: Morley-Minto Reforms — separate electorate for Muslims.
1919: Rowlatt Act → Jallianwala Bagh (Amritsar, April 13, Baisakhi day, Gen. Dyer, 379 killed officially) → Gandhi returned knighthood, others returned titles.
1920: Non-Cooperation Movement (Gandhi's first mass movement).
1929: Lahore Congress → Poorna Swaraj (complete independence) declared → 26 Jan 1930 celebrated as Independence Day.
1930: Civil Disobedience Movement → Salt March (Dandi March, March 12 - April 6, 241 miles).
1931: Gandhi-Irwin Pact → CDM suspended → Round Table Conference (London) — unsuccessful.
1935: Government of India Act — established federation (didn't work), provincial autonomy, dyarchy at centre, separate electorates.
1940: Lahore Resolution (Pakistan Resolution) — Muslim League demands separate state.
1942: Cripps Mission (failed) → Quit India Movement ("Do or Die", August 8) → mass arrests, underground resistance.
1945-46: INA trials → public sympathy for INA. Naval Mutiny (1946). Cabinet Mission (1946) — federal plan rejected.
1947: Mountbatten Plan (June 3) → Indian Independence Act → August 15, 1947 — Independence. Partition into India and Pakistan.
1948: Gandhi assassinated (January 30, by Nathuram Godse).
1950: Constitution came into force (January 26).

## Indian Polity

### Constitutional Framework

**Salient features:**
Longest written constitution (448 articles, 12 schedules — originally 395 articles, 8 schedules).
Federal with unitary bias (Centre stronger than States).
Borrowed from: UK (PM, cabinet), US (fundamental rights, judicial review, President), Ireland (DPSP, Rajya Sabha nomination), Australia (concurrent list), Canada (strong centre, residuary powers with centre), Germany (emergency), South Africa (amendment procedure), Japan (procedure for law).

**Preamble:**
"We, the people of India, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic and to secure to all its citizens:
Justice... Liberty... Equality... Fraternity..."
"Socialist" and "Secular" added by 42nd Amendment (1976).

**Types of constitutions:**
Rigid: Requires special procedure to amend (India's fundamental provisions).
Flexible: Amended by simple majority (some provisions of Indian Constitution).
India's constitution: partly rigid, partly flexible.

**Constitutional amendments:**
Simple majority (Art 368 not required): Some provisions.
Special majority + ratification by half of states: Federal provisions (Art 368, 2nd proviso).
Special majority alone (Art 368): Most amendments.

### Fundamental Rights (Part III, Art 12-35)

Six fundamental rights:
1. Right to Equality (Art 14-18): Equality before law, no discrimination, abolition of untouchability (Art 17), titles abolished (Art 18).
2. Right to Freedom (Art 19-22): Six freedoms in Art 19 (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession). Art 20: protection in conviction. Art 21: Life and liberty (MOST expanded by courts). Art 21A: Right to education (6-14 years, added by 86th Amendment 2002). Art 22: Protection from arbitrary arrest.
3. Right against Exploitation (Art 23-24): No forced labour (trafficking, begar). No child labour below 14 in hazardous employment.
4. Right to Freedom of Religion (Art 25-28).
5. Cultural and Educational Rights (Art 29-30): Minorities can establish educational institutions.
6. Right to Constitutional Remedies (Art 32): Ambedkar called it "Heart and Soul of Constitution." Five writs: habeas corpus (produce the body — wrongful detention), mandamus (we command — to perform duty), prohibition (inferior court to stop exceeding jurisdiction), certiorari (quash decision), quo warranto (by what authority — to hold public office).

**Suspension during Emergency:** Art 358 (Art 19 suspended during national emergency), Art 359 (enforcement of any FR can be suspended by Presidential order during emergency — except Art 20 and 21).

### DPSP (Part IV, Art 36-51) and Fundamental Duties (Art 51A)

DPSP: Non-justiciable (cannot be enforced in court) but fundamental in governance. Directive to state for welfare state.
Categories: Gandhian (Art 40 village panchayats, Art 43 cottage industries), Socialistic (Art 39 equal pay, Art 41 right to work), Liberal-Intellectual (Art 44 uniform civil code, Art 45 early childhood care).

Fundamental Duties: Added by 42nd Amendment (1976), 10 duties. 11th duty added by 86th Amendment (2002): Parent/guardian to provide education to child 6-14 years.
Non-justiciable (can't sue someone for not following). Swaran Singh Committee recommended.
