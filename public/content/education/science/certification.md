## High Yield Topics

```
CHEMISTRY (Board High Marks):
  Chemical reactions: types (combination, decomposition, displacement, redox)
  Balancing equations: conservation of mass
  Acids and bases: pH scale, indicators (litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange)
  Metals: reactivity series (K>Ca>Na>Mg>Al>Zn>Fe>Pb>H>Cu>Hg>Ag>Au)
  Carbon compounds: covalency of carbon, saturated/unsaturated, functional groups

PHYSICS (Numerical Heavy):
  Electricity: V=IR, P=VI=I²R=V²/R, series (I same) vs parallel (V same)
  Lens/Mirror: 1/f = 1/v ± 1/u (sign convention critical), magnification
  Light: reflection laws, refraction, total internal reflection, prism

BIOLOGY (Diagram Based):
  Nephron diagram | Brain diagram | Reproductive system diagrams
  Mendel's experiment: TT×tt → Tt (F1) → 3:1 ratio (F2)
  Food chain, ecosystem, biogeochemical cycles
```

## Revision Notes
```
BOARD EXAM STRATEGY:
  NCERT is sufficient for 85%+ marks in science
  Practice diagrams: label neatly, use pencil for diagrams
  Numericals: write formula → substitute → solve showing units

PRACTICAL EXAM (20 marks):
  Know all NCERT experiments and observations
  Common practicals: pH testing, Ohm's law, lens focal length, photosynthesis

NTSE/OLYMPIAD EXTRA:
  More application-based, less direct NCERT
  Study beyond textbook: phenomena and explanations
  Time management: many questions in limited time
```
