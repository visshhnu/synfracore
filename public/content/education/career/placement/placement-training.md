# Placement Training — Complete Guide

## Why This Chapter Matters
Campus placements are the bridge from college to career. Understanding the full recruitment process — aptitude, group discussion, technical interview, HR interview — and preparing systematically determines whether you get your dream company.

## Phase 1 — Aptitude Test

### What to Prepare (by round)
**Quantitative:** Percentage, profit-loss, time-work, speed-distance, number system, probability, permutation-combination.
**Verbal:** Reading comprehension, sentence correction, vocabulary (synonyms, antonyms, fill-in-the-blanks).
**Logical Reasoning:** Series, analogy, blood relations, direction, seating arrangement, syllogism.
**Technical:** C/C++, Java, Python output questions; data structures; DBMS queries; OS concepts.

### Preparation Timeline
6 months before placements: Start aptitude basics (1 hour/day).
3 months before: Mock tests daily. IndiaBix, PrepInsta, previous company papers.
1 month before: Company-specific papers. Speed improvement.

### Mock Test Strategy
Attempt 1 mock per day for 30 days before placement season.
After each mock: analyse wrong answers — understand WHY, not just WHAT.
Time yourself strictly — exam conditions matter.

## Phase 2 — Group Discussion (GD)

### What Evaluators Look For
Communication clarity | Leadership | Listening skills | Subject knowledge | Initiating or summarising | Not interrupting | Bringing others in

### GD Topics (Common Types)
**Social:** Gender equality, education system, reservation policy, social media impact.
**Business:** Startups vs corporates, work from home, gig economy.
**Current affairs:** AI and jobs, climate change, digital India.
**Abstract:** "Change is the only constant", "A stitch in time saves nine".

### GD Tips
Open the GD if you have a strong point — first impression matters.
If you can't open, make a strong early point in the first 2 minutes.
Listen actively — refer to what others said (shows you're paying attention).
Don't be loud, but don't be silent. Aim for 3-4 meaningful contributions.
If discussion goes off-track, summarise and bring back: "I think we've covered X and Y, but haven't discussed Z..."
Conclude confidently if asked to.

## Phase 3 — Technical Interview

### Core Topics by Role

**Software Development Engineer (SDE):**
Data Structures: Array, LinkedList, Stack, Queue, Tree, Graph, Heap, HashTable.
Algorithms: Sorting, Searching, Dynamic Programming, Greedy, BFS, DFS.
OOP Concepts: Encapsulation, Inheritance, Polymorphism, Abstraction.
DBMS: Normalisation (1NF,2NF,3NF,BCNF), SQL, Transactions, ACID.
OS: Process vs Thread, Deadlock, Paging, Scheduling algorithms.
System Design (senior): Load balancing, Caching, Microservices, CAP theorem.

**DevOps/Cloud Engineer:**
Linux commands, Shell scripting, Docker, Kubernetes basics.
CI/CD pipeline concepts, Git workflow.
Cloud: AWS/Azure/GCP core services.

**Data Analyst/Data Scientist:**
SQL (JOIN, GROUP BY, subquery, window functions).
Statistics (mean, median, mode, regression, hypothesis testing).
Python (Pandas, NumPy basics).
Machine learning concepts (supervised vs unsupervised, overfitting).

### Technical Interview Approach
"Tell me about a project" → State problem, your role, tech stack, challenges, outcome.
Code questions → Think aloud. Clarify edge cases before coding.
If you don't know → say "I'm not certain but I think..." or "I'd look it up to confirm."
Don't pretend to know what you don't.

## Phase 4 — HR Interview

Standard questions and frameworks (see Interview Preparation chapter):
"Tell me about yourself" | "Why this company" | "Strengths/Weaknesses" | "5-year plan"
Salary negotiation | Notice period | Relocation willingness

### Placement Day Checklist
Print: 5 copies of resume, all mark sheets, certificates (original + copy).
Dress: Formals. Conservative. No strong perfume.
Arrive: 30 minutes early.
Carry: Pen, notepad, water, snack.
Phone: Switch to silent or airplane mode.
Research: Company's products, recent news, tech stack used.

## Resources for Placement Prep

**DSA:** LeetCode (start Easy), HackerRank, Codeforces.
**Company papers:** PrepInsta company-specific pages, Glassdoor interview experiences.
**GD topics:** InsightsIAS for current affairs, Group Discussion Ideas website.
**Mock interviews:** Pramp (peer mock interviews), Interviewing.io.

## Salary Negotiation

Research: Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, LinkedIn Salary for the role and location.
Never first number: "I'm flexible, what's the budgeted range?"
If pressed: Salary = Market rate + 20-30% buffer.
Don't accept on spot: "I'd like 24-48 hours to consider." Always okay.
Negotiate components: Base, variable, joining bonus, annual appraisal.
