# Resume Building

## Why This Chapter Matters
A strong resume is the difference between getting shortlisted and being ignored. Whether you are a fresher or experienced professional, a well-structured, ATS-optimised resume is critical for every job application.

## Resume vs CV

**Resume:** 1-2 pages. Tailored for a specific job. Most common in India for industry roles.
**CV (Curriculum Vitae):** Longer, detailed academic record. Used for research, academic, and government positions.

## ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

Most companies use ATS software to filter resumes BEFORE a human reads them.
ATS scans for keywords from the job description.

**ATS-friendly resume rules:**
- Use standard fonts: Calibri, Arial, Times New Roman (11-12 pt body, 14-16 pt heading)
- Avoid tables, text boxes, graphics, headers/footers, fancy designs
- Use standard section headings: "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills"
- Include keywords verbatim from the job description

## Resume Structure (For Fresher / 0-2 years)

```
[FULL NAME]                          Phone | Email | LinkedIn | GitHub
[City, State]

OBJECTIVE / SUMMARY (2-3 lines)
A brief statement of your goal and value proposition for this specific role.

EDUCATION
Degree — Institution — Year — Percentage/CGPA
Relevant coursework, academic projects, honours

SKILLS
Technical: Python, Java, SQL, AWS, Kubernetes...
Tools: VS Code, Git, Docker, Jira...
Soft skills: limit to 3-4 specific ones (avoid generic: "team player")

PROJECTS (most important for fresher)
Project Name | Tech Stack | [GitHub link if available]
- What problem it solved
- Your specific contribution
- Measurable result if possible

INTERNSHIPS / EXPERIENCE
Company Name | Role | Duration
- Use action verbs: "Developed", "Implemented", "Reduced", "Led"
- Quantify results: "Reduced API response time by 40%"
- Focus on impact, not just tasks

CERTIFICATIONS
AWS Certified Solutions Architect | Year
Google Cloud Professional | Year

EXTRA-CURRICULAR / ACHIEVEMENTS (optional)
Hackathon wins, open source contributions, college leadership
```

## Resume Structure (For Experienced / 3+ years)

Same structure but WORK EXPERIENCE comes FIRST (before education).
Summary section becomes crucial — state your years of experience, domain, and key achievement.

**Example summary:**
"DevOps Engineer with 5+ years of experience designing and implementing CI/CD pipelines on AWS and GCP. Reduced deployment time by 70% at [Company]. AWS Certified Solutions Architect with expertise in Kubernetes, Terraform, and monitoring with Prometheus/Grafana."

## Quantification — The Most Important Skill

Convert every achievement to a number:
❌ "Improved application performance"
✅ "Improved API response time by 40%, reducing server costs by Rs 2.5 lakh/month"

❌ "Led a team"
✅ "Led a 5-member team to deliver a microservices migration 2 weeks ahead of schedule"

❌ "Managed AWS infrastructure"
✅ "Managed 200+ EC2 instances across 3 regions, maintaining 99.9% uptime"

## Action Verbs for Different Roles

**Technical:** Developed, Implemented, Architected, Deployed, Automated, Integrated, Optimised, Debugged, Containerised, Migrated

**Leadership:** Led, Managed, Mentored, Coordinated, Directed, Facilitated, Initiated

**Analysis:** Analysed, Evaluated, Researched, Identified, Assessed, Monitored, Tracked

**Results:** Reduced, Improved, Increased, Accelerated, Streamlined, Saved, Delivered

## Common Resume Mistakes

❌ Generic objective ("To work in a good company...")
❌ Including photo, date of birth, marital status (not needed for most companies)
❌ Using "I" in resume (write in third person implicitly: "Developed..." not "I developed...")
❌ Spelling and grammar errors (will get you rejected immediately)
❌ Using same resume for every job (tailor for each job description)
❌ Listing responsibilities instead of achievements
❌ Adding skills you barely know (will be exposed in interview)
