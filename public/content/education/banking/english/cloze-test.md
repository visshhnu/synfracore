# Cloze Test

## Why This Chapter Matters
Cloze Test (fill in the blanks in a passage) has 5-10 marks in banking exams. The new pattern is harder — double fillers and contextual vocabulary. Master both old and new patterns.

## Types

### 1. Traditional Cloze Test
A passage with 10 blanks. 5 options given for each blank. Choose the most appropriate word.
Focus: Vocabulary + Grammar + Context

### 2. Double Fillers (New Pattern)
Two blanks in a single sentence. Both blanks must be filled by the correct pair from given options.
Example: "The economy __ steadily despite __ global headwinds."
(A) grew / strong (B) contracted / weak (C) grew / strengthening (D) declined / weak
Answer: A — "grew" and "strong" both fit grammatically and contextually.

### 3. New Pattern Cloze (Most Recent)
A passage is given. Multiple sentences in each answer option. Choose the option where ALL words fit correctly in the blanks of that sentence.

## Approach

### For Traditional Cloze:
1. Read the entire passage first without stopping at blanks
2. Get the overall theme/context
3. For each blank: what part of speech is needed? (noun/verb/adjective/adverb)
4. Check grammatical fit (singular/plural, tense)
5. Check contextual fit (does the word make sense in the sentence?)
6. Eliminate obviously wrong options

### For Double Fillers:
1. Fill first blank — eliminate options that don't work
2. Check second blank among remaining options
3. Both must work together — the pair creates the meaning

## Common Words in Banking Cloze Tests

**Economy/Finance:** liquidity, solvency, fiscal, monetary, deficit, surplus, inflation, recession, stimulus, yield, benchmark, sovereign, repo rate, credit, collateral

**General formal vocabulary:** imperative, albeit, notwithstanding, pursuant, augment, curtail, mitigate, proliferate, stringent, deterrent, lucrative, volatile, prudent, resilient

**Connectors:** however, nonetheless, therefore, hence, moreover, consequently, albeit, albeit, whereas, thereby

## Grammar Rules for Cloze Tests

**Subject-Verb Agreement:** "The committee ___ decided" → has (singular committee)
**Article usage:** a + consonant sound | an + vowel sound | the + specific
**Preposition after specific words:**
- consist OF, depend ON, agree WITH, differ FROM, result IN, lead TO, refer TO
- concerned ABOUT, responsible FOR, similar TO, different FROM

**Tense consistency:** If passage is in past tense, blank likely takes past tense too
