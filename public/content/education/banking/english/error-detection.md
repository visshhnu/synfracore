# Error Detection and Correction

## Why This Chapter Matters
Error Detection has 5-10 marks in banking exams. The question gives a sentence divided into parts — find the part with grammatical error. Speed matters — each question should take 30-45 seconds max.

## Common Error Types

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement
The verb must agree with its subject in number (singular/plural).

Rule: Singular subject → singular verb. Plural subject → plural verb.

Tricky cases:
- Collective nouns: "The committee HAS decided" (committee = singular entity)
- "Each/every/either/neither" → singular verb: "Each of the students HAS submitted"
- "A number of" → plural: "A number of students WERE present"
- "The number of" → singular: "The number of students WAS large"
- Intervening phrase: "The box of apples IS on the table" (subject = box, not apples)

### 2. Tense Errors
Consistency within sentence and across related clauses.

Common errors:
- "She has went to market" → "has GONE" (present perfect needs past participle)
- "If I WILL go..." → "If I GO..." (condition clause: no will)
- "He said that he IS coming" → "he WAS coming" (reported speech: tense shifts back)
- "By tomorrow, she will FINISH" → "will HAVE FINISHED" (future perfect for completed future action)

### 3. Preposition Errors
Fixed prepositions that must be memorised:

| Phrase | Correct Preposition |
|---|---|
| Accused OF | not accused for |
| Congratulate ON | not congratulate for |
| Married TO | not married with |
| Suffer FROM | not suffer with |
| Concerned ABOUT | not concerned for |
| Differ FROM | not differ with (for things) |
| Agree WITH (person) | Agree TO (proposal) |
| Die OF (disease) | Die FROM (wound/accident) |

### 4. Article Errors
A/An/The — one of the most common error types.

A vs An: based on SOUND, not spelling
"An hour" (h is silent — vowel sound)
"A university" (u sounds like "yu" — consonant sound)
"An honest man" (h silent)
"A one-time event" (one sounds like "won" — consonant)

The — used when: previously mentioned, unique, specific, part of name
Wrong: "The life is short" (general statement — no article needed)
Wrong: "I am the doctor" (profession — no article or "a")
Correct: "The sun rises in the east" (unique sun)

### 5. Pronoun Errors
Pronoun must agree with antecedent (the noun it refers to) in number and gender.

"Everyone must submit THEIR assignment" — everyone is singular but "their" is now accepted as gender-neutral; traditional: "his or her"
"The team celebrated ITS victory" — team (singular) → its
"Each of the students submitted HIS/HER work"

### 6. Comparison Errors
- "She is more smarter" → "more smart" OR "smarter" (not both)
- "He is the most tallest" → "the tallest"
- "This book is superior than that" → "superior TO"
- "I prefer tea than coffee" → "prefer tea TO coffee"

### 7. Redundancy
Using two words that mean the same thing:
- "Return back" → just "return"
- "Revert back" → just "revert"
- "Advance planning" → just "planning"
- "Mutual cooperation" → just "cooperation"
- "Widow woman" → just "widow"

## Approach to Error Detection Questions

1. Read the full sentence once for overall sense
2. Identify the subject and verb — check agreement
3. Check tense consistency
4. Check prepositions (especially after verbs that have fixed prepositions)
5. Check articles
6. Check for redundancy or double negatives

## PYQs

**SBI PO 2023:** "Neither of the students have submitted their assignment on time."
Error: "have submitted" → "HAS submitted" (neither = singular → singular verb)

**IBPS PO 2022:** "She has been working in this company since five years."
Error: "since five years" → "FOR five years" (duration = for; specific point = since)

**IBPS Clerk 2022:** "The committee have decided to postpone the meeting."
Error: "have decided" → "HAS decided" (committee as single entity = singular)

## Revision Notes
```
SUBJECT-VERB:
Singular: each, every, either, neither, anyone, everyone → singular verb
A number of → plural | The number of → singular
Collective noun (committee, team) → singular when acting as one

TENSE:
Perfect tenses: use past participle (has gone, had seen, will have finished)
Conditional (if): no will in if-clause (if I go, not if I will go)
Reported speech: tense shifts back (said he IS → said he WAS)

PREPOSITIONS (memorise):
accused OF | congratulate ON | married TO | suffer FROM
die OF (disease) | die FROM (accident) | agree WITH (person) | agree TO (proposal)
superior TO | prefer TO | differ FROM

ARTICLES:
An + vowel SOUND (an hour, an MBA)
A + consonant SOUND (a university, a one-day event)
The + specific/unique/previously mentioned

COMPARISON: more + adj (no -er) | most + adj (no -est) | superior/inferior/prefer + TO (not than)
REDUNDANCY: revert back, return back, advance planning, mutual cooperation
```
