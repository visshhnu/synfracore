# Reading Comprehension

## Why This Chapter Matters
Reading Comprehension (RC) has 10-15 marks in SBI PO, IBPS PO, and every banking exam. It is the most important English section. Speed and accuracy in reading and answering in under 8 minutes is the goal.

## Types of RC Questions

### 1. Main Idea / Central Theme
Question: "The passage is primarily about..." or "The best title for this passage is..."
Strategy: Read the first and last paragraph carefully. The main idea is usually stated in opening or summarized at the end. All body paragraphs should support it.

### 2. Factual Questions
Question: "According to the passage, which of the following is TRUE?"
Strategy: Go back to the passage. Find the exact sentence. Don't rely on memory. Bank RCs often use "distractor" options that seem right but contradict one specific word in the passage.

### 3. Inference Questions
Question: "It can be inferred from the passage that..."
Strategy: The answer is NOT directly stated but logically follows. Look for what the author implies. Eliminate options that are too extreme or not supported by passage logic.

### 4. Vocabulary in Context
Question: "The word 'X' in the passage most nearly means..."
Strategy: Read the sentence with the word removed. What word would make sense? Then check which option fits best in context. The word may have multiple meanings — context determines which one applies here.

### 5. Tone and Attitude
Question: "The author's tone in the passage is..."
Common tones: critical, appreciative, neutral/objective, sarcastic, concerned, optimistic, pessimistic, analytical
Strategy: Look for adjectives and verbs the author uses. Strong negative words = critical. Balanced view = neutral/analytical.

### 6. Title Selection
Strategy: Title should cover the WHOLE passage, not just one paragraph. Too specific = wrong. Too general = wrong. Should capture the central theme.

## Reading Strategy — 5-Step Approach

**Step 1:** Skim the questions FIRST (30 seconds) — know what to look for
**Step 2:** Read the passage actively — underline key terms, note structure
**Step 3:** Identify topic of each paragraph in one word (margin note)
**Step 4:** Answer questions — go back to passage for each answer
**Step 5:** Verify — re-read the sentence around your answer to confirm

## Common Traps in Banking RCs

**Trap 1 — Extreme language:** Options with "always," "never," "completely," "only" are usually wrong unless the passage explicitly states these.

**Trap 2 — Out of scope:** Option mentions something true but NOT discussed in the passage. Always wrong.

**Trap 3 — Opposite meaning:** Distractor that says exactly the opposite of what the passage says. Very common in banking exams.

**Trap 4 — Partially correct:** Option with two parts — one correct, one wrong. Entire option must be correct.

## Previous Year Pattern

**SBI PO 2023:** Long RC (600-700 words) on economy/banking topic with 7-10 questions including 2 vocabulary questions.
**IBPS PO 2022:** Two medium RCs (350-400 words each) on social/environmental topics.
**IBPS Clerk 2022:** Short RC (200-250 words) with 5 direct factual questions.

## Revision Notes
```
QUESTION TYPES:
Main idea: first + last paragraph
Factual: go back to passage, find exact line
Inference: what logically follows (not directly stated)
Vocabulary: context meaning (not dictionary meaning)
Tone: author's attitude through word choice

TRAPS TO AVOID:
Extreme words (always/never)
Out of scope (true but not in passage)
Opposite of passage
Partially correct options

5-STEP STRATEGY:
1. Read questions first
2. Read passage actively
3. Note paragraph topics
4. Answer with passage reference
5. Verify by re-reading surrounding sentence
```
