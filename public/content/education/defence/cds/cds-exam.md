# CDS (Combined Defence Services) Exam

## Why This Chapter Matters
CDS is conducted by UPSC twice a year for entry into Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. Three papers: English (100 marks), General Knowledge (100 marks), Elementary Mathematics (100 marks). This guide covers the pattern and key content.

## CDS Exam Structure

**For IMA, INA, AFA:**
- English: 100 marks, 120 questions
- General Knowledge: 100 marks, 120 questions
- Elementary Mathematics: 100 marks, 100 questions

**For OTA (Officers Training Academy):**
- English: 100 marks only
- General Knowledge: 100 marks only
- No Mathematics

**Negative marking:** 1/3 mark deducted for wrong answer.

## Paper 1 — English

### Key Grammar Topics
Same as NDA English but at a slightly higher level.

**Spotting Errors:** Read the sentence, identify which underlined part has grammatical error.

**Sentence Arrangement (Para Jumbles):** Rearrange sentences to form coherent paragraph. Find opener (no pronoun dependency), use connectors (however, therefore, moreover) as clues.

**Fill in the Blanks:** Vocabulary + grammar. Context determines the correct word.

**Comprehension Passages:** 4-5 questions per passage. Main idea, inference, vocabulary.

**Ordering of Words:** Arrange jumbled words to make correct sentence.

**Synonyms and Antonyms:** High-frequency vocabulary from previous years.

### Important Vocabulary for CDS
**Formal/Legal vocabulary:** Abate, Abrogate, Adjudicate, Acquiesce, Ameliorate, Amicable, Approbation, Auspicious

**Military vocabulary:** Sortie, Reconnaissance, Debriefing, Deployment, Echelon, Flank, Ordnance, Salvo

## Paper 2 — General Knowledge

### Indian and World History
**Key focus for CDS:** Modern India (British era to Independence) and Post-Independence India

**Post-independence India:**
First President: Dr. Rajendra Prasad
First PM: Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru
First General Election: 1952
Republic Day: 26 January 1950 (Constitution came into force)

**Wars fought by India:**
1947-48: First Kashmir War
1962: Sino-Indian War (China attacked)
1965: Indo-Pakistan War
1971: Bangladesh Liberation War (creation of Bangladesh, Shimla Agreement 1972)
1999: Kargil War (Pakistan-backed infiltration, Operation Vijay)

### Indian Constitution and Polity
**Key facts:**
Constitution drafted by: Constituent Assembly chaired by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar (drafting committee chairman)
Constitution adopted: 26 November 1949 | Came into force: 26 January 1950
Total articles: 448 (originally 395), 12 schedules, 104 amendments (as of 2023)
Longest constitution in the world.

**Fundamental Rights (Part III, Articles 12-35):**
1. Right to Equality (Art 14-18)
2. Right to Freedom (Art 19-22)
3. Right against Exploitation (Art 23-24)
4. Right to Freedom of Religion (Art 25-28)
5. Cultural and Educational Rights (Art 29-30)
6. Right to Constitutional Remedies (Art 32) — "Heart and Soul of Constitution" (Ambedkar)

**Directive Principles (Part IV):** Non-justiciable but fundamental to governance.
**Fundamental Duties (Part IVA, Art 51A):** Added by 42nd Amendment.

**Parliament:**
Rajya Sabha: Upper house, 250 members max (238 elected + 12 nominated), 6-year terms
Lok Sabha: Lower house, 552 members max, 5-year term
Money Bill: originates only in Lok Sabha

**Emergency provisions:** National Emergency (Art 352), State Emergency/President's Rule (Art 356), Financial Emergency (Art 360)

## Paper 3 — Elementary Mathematics

### Key Topics
**Number System:** HCF, LCM, divisibility rules, fractions, decimals
**Algebra:** Equations (linear, quadratic), polynomials, progressions
**Trigonometry:** Ratios, identities, heights and distances
**Geometry:** Triangles, circles, polygons, 3D shapes
**Mensuration:** Area, perimeter, volume of all standard shapes
**Statistics:** Mean, median, mode, range
**Data Interpretation:** Tables, bar graphs, pie charts

### High-Yield Topics for CDS Maths

**Mensuration formulas:**
Cylinder: CSA=2πrh, Volume=πr^2h
Cone: CSA=πrl (l=slant height), Volume=(1/3)πr^2h
Sphere: TSA=4πr^2, Volume=(4/3)πr^3
Frustum: Volume=(πh/3)(R^2+r^2+Rr)

**Time and Work:**
If A takes a days and B takes b days: Together = ab/(a+b) days

**Simple and Compound Interest:**
SI = PRT/100 | CI: A = P(1+R/100)^T | CI-SI = P(R/100)^2 for 2 years

## PYQs (CDS)

**CDS 2023 (English):** Arrange in correct sequence: P(The police) Q(have arrested) R(the thief) S(who had) T(stolen the car)
Correct: P-Q-R-S-T = "The police have arrested the thief who had stolen the car."

**CDS 2022 (GK):** Operation Vijay was conducted in:
Kargil (1999) — to evict Pakistani-backed intruders from Kargil heights.

**CDS 2021 (Maths):** A train 150m long passes a pole in 15 seconds. Its speed in km/h?
Speed = 150/15 = 10 m/s = 10 × 18/5 = 36 km/h.
