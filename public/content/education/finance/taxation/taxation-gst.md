# Taxation — Income Tax and GST

## Why This Chapter Matters
Taxation knowledge is essential for CA Foundation, B.Com exams, finance careers, and everyday personal finance. GST revolutionised India's indirect tax system in 2017.

## Income Tax

### 1. Basic Concepts
Direct tax: paid directly by income earner to government (Income tax, Corporate tax).
Indirect tax: collected by seller from buyer and paid to government (GST, Customs).

**Assessment Year (AY):** Year in which income is assessed and taxed. AY 2024-25 = income earned in FY 2023-24.
**Previous Year (PY):** Year in which income is earned. Also called Financial Year (FY).

### 2. Heads of Income
Income is taxed under 5 heads:
1. Salaries: basic salary, HRA, allowances, perquisites.
2. House Property: rental income (actual or notional), minus standard deduction 30%.
3. Profits and Gains from Business/Profession.
4. Capital Gains: short-term (held ≤ 2 years) or long-term (held > 2 years). Different tax rates.
5. Income from Other Sources: interest, dividends, gambling winnings.

**Gross Total Income (GTI):** Sum of income under all 5 heads.
**Deductions (Chapter VIA — most important):**
Section 80C (up to Rs 1.5 lakh): EPF, PPF, ELSS, LIC, NSC, home loan principal, children's tuition.
Section 80D: Health insurance premium (up to Rs 25,000 + Rs 25,000 for parents).
Section 80G: Donations (50% or 100% of donation, subject to limits).

**Total Income = GTI - Deductions.**

### 3. Tax Slabs (Old Regime FY 2024-25)

| Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to Rs 2.5 lakh | Nil |
| Rs 2.5-5 lakh | 5% |
| Rs 5-10 lakh | 20% |
| Above Rs 10 lakh | 30% |

Surcharge for income above Rs 50 lakh. Health and Education Cess: 4% on tax.
Rebate u/s 87A: Full tax rebate if income ≤ Rs 5 lakh (effectively zero tax).

**New Tax Regime (default from FY 2024-25):**
Lower rates but most deductions NOT available (except NPS employer contribution, gratuity).
Simpler: Rs 3 lakh nil, then 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30% in slabs.

### 4. GST (Goods and Services Tax)

**Implemented:** 1 July 2017. Replaced: Sales tax, VAT, excise, service tax, entry tax, etc.
**One nation, one tax** — uniform tax throughout India.

**Types of GST:**
CGST: Central GST (central government share).
SGST: State GST (state government share).
IGST: Integrated GST (on inter-state supply — full amount, then split between centre and state).
UTGST: UT GST (for Union Territories without legislature).

**GST Rates:** 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%.
Essential goods (food grains, health): 0%.
Basic necessities (tea, spices): 5%.
Most goods and services: 12% or 18%.
Luxury items (cars, AC, tobacco): 28%.

**Input Tax Credit (ITC):**
Business can claim credit for GST paid on inputs (raw materials, services).
Net GST payable = Output tax - Input tax credit.
This avoids cascading effect (tax on tax).

**GST Council:** Joint forum of Centre and States. Chaired by Finance Minister of India.
GSTIN: 15-digit unique identification number for every GST-registered business.

**Returns:** GSTR-1 (outward supplies), GSTR-3B (summary return), GSTR-9 (annual return).
