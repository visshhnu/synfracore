# Resources and Development

## Why This Chapter Matters
This is Geography Chapter 1 in Class 10 — 4-6 marks in boards every year. Resource classification, land use, soil types, and conservation methods are the key topics tested.

## Core Concepts

### 1. What is a Resource?
Everything in the environment which can be used to satisfy human needs — provided it is:
- Technologically accessible
- Economically feasible
- Culturally acceptable

Resources are not static — something becomes a resource when humans find a way to use it.

### 2. Classification of Resources

**On the basis of origin:**
Biotic: living things (plants, animals, fish)
Abiotic: non-living things (rocks, minerals, water, metals)

**On the basis of exhaustibility:**
Renewable: can be regenerated (solar energy, wind, water, forests)
Non-renewable: formed over millions of years, cannot be quickly renewed (coal, oil, minerals)

**On the basis of ownership:**
Individual: owned privately (land, house, pond)
Community/Common property: accessible to all community members (common pastures, village ponds)
National: owned by the state (forests, rivers, minerals, ocean within territorial limit)
International: beyond national jurisdiction (open oceans, celestial bodies, Antarctica)

**On the basis of development:**
Potential: found but not used (Rajasthan wind/solar — vast potential)
Developed/Stock: surveyed and being used
Reserves: currently not being used but can be used in future

### 3. Resource Planning in India
Resources are not evenly distributed. India has:
- Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, MP: mineral-rich but underdeveloped
- Rajasthan: huge renewable energy potential
- Arunachal Pradesh: water resources but lacks capital

Resource planning involves:
1. Identification and inventory of resources
2. Planning for development with appropriate technology
3. Matching with overall national development plans

### 4. Land Resources

**Land degradation:**
- Deforestation (most important cause)
- Mining and quarrying
- Overgrazing
- Salinisation (waterlogging in irrigated areas)
- Floods and wind erosion

**Conservation:**
- Afforestation (planting trees)
- Contour ploughing (across slopes)
- Terrace farming
- Check dams
- Strip cropping

**Land use categories:**
Forest land | Barren/uncultivable | Permanent pastures | Miscellaneous tree crops | Cultivable waste | Fallow lands | Net sown area

India's net sown area is highest in Punjab and Haryana. Net sown area has increased since independence but forest area has remained largely unchanged.

### 5. Soil Resources

Soil is the most important renewable natural resource — food grows in it.

**Types of Soil in India:**

| Soil Type | Where Found | Characteristics | Crops |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alluvial | Indo-Gangetic plain, river valleys, coastal | Most fertile, good water retention | Rice, wheat, sugarcane, cotton |
| Black/Regur | Deccan Plateau (Maharashtra, MP, Gujarat) | High moisture retention, self-ploughing, rich in Ca/Mg | Cotton (best), jowar, linseed |
| Red and Yellow | Eastern and Southern India | Red due to iron oxide, poor in nitrogen | Rice, wheat, millet on fertile patches |
| Laterite | Western Ghats, NE hills, Odisha, Chhattisgarh | Formed by leaching (heavy rain washes nutrients), acidic, poor in nutrients | Tea, coffee, cashew (after treatment) |
| Arid/Desert | Rajasthan, Gujarat | Sandy, poor in nutrients, high salt | Drought-resistant crops with irrigation |
| Forest soil | Mountain regions | Coarse, less fertile, acidic in upper slopes | Tea, coffee, spices |

**Soil conservation methods:**
- Contour bunding (embankments along contour lines)
- Terrace cultivation (hill slopes cut into steps)
- Strip cropping (different crops in alternating strips)
- Shelter belts (rows of trees to block wind — Rajasthan, plains)
- Mulching (covering soil between plants)

## PYQs

**2023:** Distinguish between potential resources and stock resources.
Potential resources: available in a region but not yet used due to lack of technology or capital (e.g., wind energy in Rajasthan, uranium in Ladakh). Stock resources: those that have been assessed for use but due to lack of technology cannot be utilised (e.g., hydrogen as fuel — exists but technology not yet cost-effective at scale).

**2022:** Why is resource planning essential in India?
India has unequal distribution of resources — some states are mineral-rich but poor, others have human resources but lack minerals. Resource planning prevents over-exploitation, ensures sustainable use, and makes sure development reaches all regions. Without planning, resources are wasted, degraded, or only benefit some people.

**2021:** What is black soil? State its characteristics and the crop best suited for it.
Black soil (Regur/Cotton soil) is found in the Deccan plateau. Characteristics: high moisture retention, deep cracks when dry (self-ploughing), rich in calcium, potassium, and magnesium but poor in nitrogen. Best suited crop: cotton (also called black cotton soil). Also supports jowar, linseed, and sugarcane.
