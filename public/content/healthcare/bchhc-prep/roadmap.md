# BCHHC Certification Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–2)
- ICD-10-CM code structure and conventions
- NEC vs NOS, Excludes1 vs Excludes2
- Combination codes (HTN, DM)
- Placeholder X and 7th character rules

## Phase 2 — Home Health Specific (Weeks 3–4)
- Medicare home health coverage criteria
- OASIS types, timing, who completes
- PDGM: 5 factors and 12 clinical groups
- Primary diagnosis selection rules
- Homebound documentation requirements

## Phase 3 — Complex Coding (Weeks 5–6)
- Wound coding (L89, L97, T81) and 7th characters
- CVA sequela coding (I69.3xx series)
- HTN combination codes (I11, I12, I13)
- Diabetes complication codes and Z79.4
- Infection coding with A/B organism codes

## Phase 4 — Exam Prep (Weeks 7–8)
- Timed practice case studies
- Mock BCHHC-style exam questions
- Review error patterns and weak areas
- Form 485 and plan of care compliance

## Recommended Study Resources
- CMS ICD-10-CM Official Guidelines (free at CMS.gov)

- AAPC Home Health Coder study materials
- SynfraCore BCHHC mock exams and coding scenarios

## 8-Week Study Plan

### Weeks 1-2: ICD-10-CM Foundation
- Code structure: 3-7 characters, decimal after 3rd
- NEC vs NOS distinction
- Excludes1 vs Excludes2
- Placeholder X and 7th character rules
- Combination codes: HTN (I11/I12/I13), DM complications

### Weeks 3-4: OASIS and Home Health Coverage
- Four Medicare coverage criteria: homebound, skilled service, physician care, reasonable/necessary
- OASIS types: SOC, ROC, REC, SCIC, DC — timing for each
- Who can complete OASIS: SN, PT, SLP (not OT independently)
- Primary diagnosis selection rules for home health and PDGM
- Plan of Care (Form 485) requirements

### Weeks 5-6: PDGM Deep Dive
- Five classification factors: timing, admission source, clinical group, functional level, comorbidity
- 12 clinical groups — which primary diagnoses map to each
- LUPA thresholds per clinical group
- Homebound documentation: specific language that supports vs. generic that doesn't

### Weeks 7-8: Complex Scenarios and Mock Exams
- CVA sequela: I69.3xx series, never acute stroke codes in home health
- Wound coding: L89 vs L97 vs T81, 7th character A/D/S selection
- Full complex patient coding (CHF + CKD + DM + anemia + insulin)
- Timed practice exams under exam conditions
- Review error patterns and weak areas
