# CLAT — Previous Year Pattern Questions

## Legal Reasoning

**Q1.** Principle: A person is liable for the natural and probable consequences of his actions.
Fact: A throws a stone at a dog. The stone misses the dog and hits B, causing injury.
Question: Is A liable to B?

**Answer: Yes.** Throwing a stone in a public place where people are present makes injury to a person a natural and probable consequence. A failed to take care, and the injury to B was foreseeable.

---

**Q2.** Principle: Nobody can transfer a better title than he himself has.
Fact: X steals Y's car and sells it to Z, who buys it in good faith for full value.
Question: Can Z claim ownership against Y?

**Answer: No.** X had no title to the car. He cannot transfer what he does not have (*nemo dat quod non habet*). Z, despite being a bona fide purchaser, cannot get good title from a thief. Y can recover the car from Z.

---

**Q3.** Principle: An agreement without consideration is void except when it is made on account of natural love and affection between parties standing in a near relation.
Fact: A, for natural love and affection, promises to give his son B Rs 5,000. A puts this promise in writing and registers it.
Question: Is this a valid contract?

**Answer: Yes.** Section 25, Exception 1 of the Indian Contract Act — a written and registered agreement on account of natural love and affection between near relations is valid even without consideration. Father-son = near relation.

## Logical Reasoning

**Q4.** All actors are dancers. No dancer is a singer. Conclusions:
I. No actor is a singer. II. Some actors are singers.

**Answer: Only I follows.** If all actors are dancers and no dancer is a singer, then no actor can be a singer. Conclusion I follows. II is the opposite and doesn't follow.

**Q5.** Statements: Some books are pens. All pens are erasers.
Conclusions: I. Some books are erasers. II. All erasers are pens.

**Answer: Only I follows.** Some books are pens + All pens are erasers → Some books are erasers (valid syllogism). II reverses a universal statement — not valid.

(a) Fundamental Rights provisions of the Constitution
**(b) Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993**
(c) Civil Procedure Code
(d) A Constitutional Amendment

**Q8.** The right to education is guaranteed under which Article of the Indian Constitution?
(a) Article 21  (b) Article 21A  (c) Article 45  (d) Article 19
**Answer: Article 21A** — added by 86th Amendment 2002. Free and compulsory education 6-14 years.
