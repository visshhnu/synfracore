# Legal Fundamentals

## What Is Law?
Law is a system of rules created and enforced by social or governmental institutions to regulate behaviour. In India, law is derived from the Constitution, Parliamentary legislation, state legislation, judicial precedent (common law), and customary law.

## Sources of Indian Law
| Source | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| Constitutional Law | Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, Union-State relations |
| Statute Law | IPC, CPC, CrPC, Contract Act, Companies Act |
| Precedent (Case Law) | Supreme Court and High Court judgments |
| Customary Law | Personal laws — Hindu, Muslim, Christian |
| International Law | Treaties, UN conventions ratified by India |

## Indian Legal System Structure
```
Supreme Court of India (apex — final authority)
    ↓
High Courts (each state/UT — appellate and original)
    ↓
District Courts (Sessions Court — criminal / Civil Court — civil)
    ↓
Subordinate Courts (Magistrate Courts, Family Courts, Labour Courts)
    ↓
Tribunals (NCLT, NCLAT, ITAT, NGT, TDSAT)
```

## Key Branches of Law
- **Criminal Law**: IPC 1860, CrPC 1973 — offences against the state/individual
- **Civil Law**: CPC 1908 — disputes between private parties
- **Constitutional Law**: Constitution of India 1950
- **Contract Law**: Indian Contract Act 1872
- **Company Law**: Companies Act 2013
- **Property Law**: Transfer of Property Act 1882
- **Family Law**: Hindu Marriage Act, Muslim Personal Law, Christian Marriage Act
- **Labour Law**: Industrial Disputes Act, PF Act, ESI Act, Minimum Wages Act
