# AIBE Cheatsheet — At-a-Glance Reference

## Acts → Key Sections Map

```
CONSTITUTION
  Art 14    Equal protection
  Art 19    Six freedoms (speech/assembly/movement/profession)
  Art 20    Protection in conviction
  Art 21    Right to life & liberty
  Art 21A   Right to education (6-14 yrs)
  Art 22    Protection against arbitrary arrest
  Art 32    Writs to SC | Art 226 Writs to HC
  Art 368   Amendment — ordinary / special majority / + state ratification

BNS 2023 (replaces IPC 1860)
  Sec 3(5)  Common intention
  Sec 63    Rape definition
  Sec 101   Murder
  Sec 103   Culpable homicide not amounting to murder
  Sec 109   Attempt to murder
  Sec 190   Unlawful assembly / common object
  Sec 303   Theft
  Sec 318   Cheating
  Sec 351   Defamation

BNSS 2023 (replaces CrPC 1973)
  Sec 173   FIR registration (cognizable)
  Sec 35    Arrest without warrant (cognizable)
  Sec 187   Remand (60/90 days)
  Sec 258   Bail conditions
  Sec 482   Anticipatory bail

BSA 2023 (replaces Indian Evidence Act 1872)
  Sec 3     Definitions
  Sec 32    Dying declaration
  Sec 63    Electronic records (certificate required)
  Sec 101   Burden of proof on asserter
  Sec 111   Burden shifts on accused in special cases

CONTRACT ACT
  Sec 10    Valid contract conditions
  Sec 11    Competent parties (major + sound mind)
  Sec 14    Free consent
  Sec 17    Fraud
  Sec 19    Voidable if consent by coercion/fraud/misrepresentation
  Sec 56    Frustration of contract
  Sec 73    Compensation on breach
  Sec 74    Liquidated damages

CPC
  O.7       Plaint
  O.8       Written statement (WS within 30 days)
  O.39      Temporary injunction
  O.21      Execution of decree
  Sec 9     Civil court jurisdiction
  Sec 10    Res sub judice (stay)
  Sec 11    Res judicata (bar to re-litigation)
  Sec 96    First appeal
  Sec 115   Revision
```

| District Court | Above ₹20 lakh |

| Consumer District Commission | Up to ₹1 crore |
| Consumer State Commission | ₹1–2 crore |
| NCDRC | Above ₹2 crore |

## Criminal Court Hierarchy

```
Judicial Magistrate 1st Class  → Max 3 yrs imprisonment
Chief Judicial Magistrate      → Max 7 yrs
Sessions Court                 → Unlimited (including death, with HC confirmation)
High Court                     → All criminal appellate + original writ
Supreme Court                  → Final appellate, Art 32, advisory
```

## Limitation Periods — Most Tested

| Cause of Action | Period |
|----------------|--------|
| Simple money suit | 3 years from date of default |
| Suit on Bill of Exchange/Promissory Note | 3 years |
| Suit for possession of immovable property | 12 years (private) / 30 years (government) |
| Appeal to High Court | 90 days |
| Appeal to Supreme Court | 90 days |
| Consumer complaint | 2 years |
| RTI first appeal | 30 days | Second appeal 90 days |
