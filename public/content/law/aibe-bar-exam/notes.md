# AIBE Quick Notes & Mnemonics

## Memory Tricks for Key Provisions

### Contract Act — CLOFFL (Valid Contract Elements)
- **C**onsideration
- **L**awful object
- **O**ffer and acceptance
- **F**ree consent
- **F**ree from voidability
- **L**egal capacity (competent parties)

### CPC Order Numbers
- **O.7** = Plaint (7 letters in "Plaint")
- **O.8** = Written Statement
- **O.39** = Temporary Injunction (39 = TI = reverse TI)
- **O.21** = Execution of Decree

### FIR Timeline — 60/90 Rule (CrPC/BNSS)
- Offence punishable up to 10 years: charge sheet within **60 days**
- Offence punishable with death/life/10+ years: charge sheet within **90 days**
- Failure = accused entitled to default bail

### Five Writs — HMPCQ
**H**abeas — **M**andamus — **P**rohibition — **C**ertiorari — **Q**uo Warranto

### Fundamental Rights — Article Numbers
14–18: Right to Equality  
19–22: Right to Freedom  
23–24: Right against Exploitation  
25–28: Right to Religion  
29–30: Cultural/Educational Rights  
32: Right to Constitutional Remedies

## Frequently Confused Pairs

| Term A | Term B | Difference |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Cognizable offence | Non-cognizable | Police can arrest without warrant in cognizable |
| Bailable offence | Non-bailable | Bail is right in bailable; discretion in non-bailable |
| Void contract | Voidable contract | Void = no legal effect; Voidable = can be rescinded |
| Admission | Confession | Admission = any party; Confession = accused in criminal case |
| First appeal | Revision | Appeal = decided on merits; Revision = jurisdictional error |
| Certiorari | Prohibition | Certiorari = after order passed; Prohibition = before order |
| Murder | Culpable homicide | Murder = always intention; CHnaMM = intention without the aggravated circumstances |
| FIR | Complaint | FIR = cognizable offence to police; Complaint = magistrate |
