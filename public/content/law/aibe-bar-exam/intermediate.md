## Legal Drafting — Key Documents

### Vakalatnama (Power of Attorney to Advocate)

A Vakalatnama authorises an advocate to appear and act on behalf of a client. Key contents:
- Name and address of client
- Name and enrollment number of advocate  
- Name of court and case details
- Powers granted (to appear, file applications, receive notices, compromise)
- Limitation on powers (if any)
- Signatures of client and advocate, date

### Legal Notice

Structure of a proper legal notice:
1. "To" block — name, address of recipient
2. "From" block — name, address of client, through advocate
3. Subject line — concise description
4. Facts — numbered paragraphs, chronological
5. Legal basis — which law/right violated
6. Demand — what action required
7. Time limit — usually 15/30/60 days
8. Consequence of non-compliance — "legal proceedings will be initiated"
9. "Without prejudice" clause if applicable

### Plaint Structure (Order 7 CPC)

1. Name and address of court
2. Case number (left blank initially)
3. Details of plaintiff: name, father's name, age, address, occupation
4. Details of defendant
5. Cause of action — when and where it arose
6. Facts constituting cause of action (numbered paragraphs)
7. Relief sought
8. Jurisdiction — territorial, pecuniary, subject-matter
9. Valuation of suit for court fees
10. Verification — signed by plaintiff, declared true to knowledge/belief

## Professional Ethics — Bar Council Rules

**An Advocate shall NOT:**
- Advertise or solicit work (Rule 36)
- Use name/services for unauthorised practice
- Accept brief if likely to be a witness
- Act on instructions of any person other than client or client's authorised agent
- Charge fees contingent on success in litigation (no "success fees" in Indian courts)
- Acquire interest in litigation

**An Advocate SHALL:**
- Maintain client confidentiality (perpetual duty, even after relationship ends)
- Not appear against former client in same/related matter
- Be honest with court — never mislead
- Refuse to represent client if it involves suppressing material facts
- Attend case — if unable, brief another advocate with client's consent

**Contempt of Court:**
- Civil contempt: wilful disobedience of court order/undertaking
- Criminal contempt: publication that scandalises court, prejudices proceedings
- An advocate cannot use abusive language in court — amounts to contempt
