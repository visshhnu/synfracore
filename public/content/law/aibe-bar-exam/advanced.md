## Special and Modern Laws

### POCSO Act 2012 (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences)
- Applies to all children under 18 years
- Penetrative sexual assault: minimum 20 years (Sec 4)
- Aggravated penetrative assault: minimum 20 years to life/death (Sec 6)
- Sexual assault: minimum 3 years (Sec 8)
- Special courts: POCSO cases tried in designated Special Courts
- Mandatory reporting (Sec 19): Any person aware of offence must report to SJPU/police
- Child-friendly procedures: camera trial, support person, no direct cross-examination

### Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002 (PMLA)
- Attaches and confiscates proceeds of crime
- Enforcement Directorate (ED) investigates
- Predicate offences listed in Schedule
- Burden of proof: on accused to prove property is not proceeds of crime
- Bail is extremely difficult — Sec 45 twin conditions (rarely satisfied)

### Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016 (IBC)
- Time-bound resolution: 180 days + 90 days extension
- Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) initiated before NCLT
- Resolution Professional manages company during CIRP
- Committee of Creditors approves resolution plan
- Waterfall: CIRP costs → secured creditors → workmen dues → unsecured creditors → equity

### Right to Information Act 2005 (RTI)
- Any citizen can request information from public authority
- Response within 30 days (48 hours if life/liberty at stake)
- First Appeal to designated officer within 30 days
- Second Appeal to Central/State Information Commissioner within 90 days
- Exemptions: national security, cabinet papers, personal privacy, trade secrets (Sec 8)
- Public authority cannot deny information that cannot be denied in parliament

### RERA 2016 (Real Estate Regulation and Development Act)
- Mandatory registration of real estate projects + agents
- Disclosure of project details, layout, land status, approvals
- Delayed possession: developer pays interest at SBI MCLR + 2%
- Defects liability: 5 years structural defects
- RERA Adjudicating Officer for compensation; RERA Authority for general complaints
- Appeals to RERA Appellate Tribunal → High Court
