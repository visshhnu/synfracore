# AIBE Fundamentals — Core Legal Subjects

## Constitutional Law Quick Reference

### Fundamental Rights (Articles 12–35)

| Article | Right | Key Points |
|---------|-------|------------|
| 14 | Equality before law | Applies to all persons, not just citizens. Prohibits arbitrary classification |
| 15 | Non-discrimination | On grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth. Art 15(3): special provision for women/children |
| 16 | Equal opportunity in public employment | Reservations permissible under 16(4) |
| 19 | Six freedoms | Speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession — all with reasonable restrictions |
| 20 | Protection in conviction | No ex post facto law, no double jeopardy, no self-incrimination |
| 21 | Right to life | Most expanded FR — includes privacy, livelihood, speedy trial, education |
| 21A | Right to education | Free and compulsory education 6–14 years |
| 22 | Protection against arrest | Must be informed of grounds, right to legal counsel, produced before magistrate within 24 hours |
| 25–28 | Religious freedom | Freedom of conscience, right to manage religious affairs |
| 32 | Right to constitutional remedies | Direct approach to SC for FR violation. Five writs |

### Five Writs — Quick Recall

**H**abeas Corpus — produce the body (detention)  
**M**andamus — command a duty (public body failing duty)  
**P**rohibition — stop inferior court (excess jurisdiction)  
**C**ertiorari — quash inferior court order (error/excess)  
**Q**uo Warranto — by what authority (public office challenge)

### Key Concepts

**Mens rea (mental element):**
- **Intention** — highest culpability (murder requires intention)
- **Knowledge** — knew consequences likely (culpable homicide)
- **Recklessness** — conscious disregard of risk
- **Negligence** — failure to exercise reasonable care

**Common intention vs common object:**
- Sec 3(5) BNS (old Sec 34 IPC): Common intention — prior meeting of minds, all equally liable
- Sec 190 BNS (old Sec 149 IPC): Unlawful assembly — 5+ persons, common object, every member liable

## Contract Act 1872 — Essentials

**Valid contract requires (Sec 10):**
1. Offer (Sec 2(a)) + Acceptance (Sec 2(b)) = Agreement
2. Lawful consideration (Sec 2(d)) — quid pro quo
3. Competent parties (Sec 11) — major, sound mind, not disqualified
4. Free consent (Sec 14) — no coercion, undue influence, fraud, misrepresentation, mistake
5. Lawful object (Sec 23) — not forbidden, fraudulent, against public policy
6. Not expressly declared void (Sec 24–30)

**Types of contract:**
- **Void contract** (Sec 2(j)): No legal effect from the beginning (e.g. contract with minor)
- **Voidable contract** (Sec 2(i)): Valid but can be rescinded by aggrieved party (e.g. undue influence)
- **Illegal contract**: Not enforceable + collateral agreements also void

**Breach and remedies (Sec 73–75):**
- **Damages** — compensation for loss naturally arising from breach
- **Specific performance** — court orders actual performance (Specific Relief Act)
- **Injunction** — court restrains breach of negative stipulation
- **Quantum meruit** — payment for work already done before breach

## Indian Evidence Act / Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam 2023

**Burden of proof (Sec 101–111 IEA / corresponding BSA sections):**
- Whoever asserts a fact must prove it
- In criminal cases: prosecution must prove beyond reasonable doubt
- In civil cases: plaintiff proves on balance of probabilities (preponderance)

**Relevant facts (Sec 6–55 IEA):**
- Facts forming part of same transaction (res gestae) — Sec 6
- Facts showing motive, preparation, conduct — Sec 8
- Facts making existence of other facts probable/improbable — Sec 11
- Admissions (Sec 17–23): statements against own interest, relevant but not conclusive
- Confessions (Sec 24–30): only to magistrate are fully admissible; to police — only if leads to discovery (Sec 27)

**Electronic evidence — Sec 65B IEA / Sec 63 BSA:**
- Electronic record is admissible if accompanied by a certificate
- Certificate must state: computer used in ordinary course, output represents stored info, computer was functioning properly
- Landmark: *Arjun Panditrao Khotkar v Kailash Kushanrao Gorantyal (2020)*: certificate mandatory, without it evidence inadmissible

## CPC — Civil Procedure Quick Reference

**Jurisdiction:** Courts can only hear cases they have authority over:
- **Subject-matter** — Family courts for matrimonial disputes, Consumer commissions for consumer disputes
- **Territorial** — defendant resides or cause of action arose within court's area
- **Pecuniary** — monetary value within court's limit

**Res judicata (Sec 11):** Same matter cannot be litigated again between same parties once finally decided.  
**Res sub judice (Sec 10):** Court will stay suit if same matter pending in another court.

**Temporary injunctions (Order 39):** Three conditions:
1. Prima facie case in favour of plaintiff
2. Irreparable injury if not granted
3. Balance of convenience in plaintiff's favour
