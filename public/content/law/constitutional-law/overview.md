# Constitutional Law — Indian Constitution

## The Constitution of India: Foundation Document

Adopted on **26 November 1949**, in force from **26 January 1950**. World's longest written constitution — originally 395 Articles, 8 Schedules. Currently: ~470 Articles, 12 Schedules, 25 Parts.

## Fundamental Rights (Part III — Articles 12–35)

These are **justiciable** — courts can enforce them. Can be suspended during Emergency (except Arts 20, 21).

### Article 14 — Equality Before Law
- No person shall be denied equality before law or equal protection of laws
- State cannot discriminate arbitrarily — must have **intelligible differentia** + **rational nexus** to object

### Article 19 — Six Freedoms
1. Speech and Expression
2. Peaceful Assembly
3. Form Associations
4. Free Movement within India
5. Reside and Settle
6. Practice any Profession / Trade

Each freedom has **reasonable restrictions** under Art 19(2)–(6) — sovereignty, security, public order, morality, contempt of court, defamation.

### Article 21 — Right to Life and Personal Liberty
- Most expansive FR after *Maneka Gandhi* case (1978)
- Includes: right to privacy (*Puttaswamy, 2017*), livelihood, health, education, speedy trial, dignity, environment

### Article 32 — Right to Constitutional Remedies ("Heart and Soul")
Five writs:
- **Habeas Corpus** — Produce the body (unlawful detention)
- **Mandamus** — Command a public body to perform its duty
- **Prohibition** — Stop inferior court from exceeding jurisdiction
- **Certiorari** — Quash order of inferior court
- **Quo Warranto** — By what authority do you hold this office?

## Directive Principles (Part IV — Articles 36–51)

**Non-justiciable** but fundamental to governance. Courts cannot enforce them, but Parliament and States must keep them in mind when making law.

Key DPSPs:
- Art 38: State to secure social order for welfare
- Art 39: Equal pay for equal work, equal access to means of livelihood
- Art 39A: Free legal aid (implemented via Legal Services Authorities Act)
- Art 44: **Uniform Civil Code** (UCC) — still a directive, not implemented
- Art 45: Free and compulsory education for children (now FR under Art 21A)
- Art 48A: Protect and improve environment and safeguard forests/wildlife

---

## Fundamental Duties (Part IVA — Article 51A)

Added by **42nd Amendment 1976** (Swaran Singh Committee). Originally 10, now **11 duties** (added by 86th Amendment 2002).

Key duties: Respect Constitution/National Flag/Anthem, defend the country, promote harmony, preserve culture, protect environment, develop scientific temper, safeguard public property, strive for excellence.

---

## Constitutional Amendments — Article 368

**Simple majority** (ordinary bills): matters under Art 5, 169, etc.  
**Special majority** (2/3 of members present + voting AND majority of total strength): most amendments  
**Special majority + State ratification** (½ of State legislatures): federal provisions — Lists, election of President, SC/HC jurisdiction

**Basic Structure** (Kesavananda Bharati, 1973): Parliament cannot destroy the basic structure even via Art 368. Includes: Supremacy of Constitution, Rule of Law, Judicial Review, Secularism, Federalism, Free Elections.

## Federal Structure: Union-State Relations

### Legislative Relations (Arts 245–255)
- **Union List** (List I): 98 subjects — Defence, Foreign Affairs, Atomic Energy, Currency, Railways
- **State List** (List II): 59 subjects — Public Order, Police, Agriculture, Public Health
- **Concurrent List** (List III): 52 subjects — Education, Forests, Marriage, Bankruptcy, Electricity

### Administrative Relations (Arts 256–263)
- Inter-State Council (Art 263) for coordination
- States must comply with Union laws/Executive directions

### Financial Relations (Arts 268–293)
- Finance Commission (Art 280) — recommends Centre-State tax devolution
- GST Council (Art 279A) — cooperative federalism in taxation

## Emergency Provisions

| Type | Article | Grounds | Effect |
|------|---------|---------|--------|
| National Emergency | 352 | War / External Aggression / Armed Rebellion | FRs 19 suspended; Union can legislate on State List |
| President's Rule | 356 | Failure of Constitutional Machinery in State | State government dismissed; Parliament governs state |
| Financial Emergency | 360 | Threat to financial stability | Never invoked |

**Imposed:** Cabinet recommendation → Presidential Proclamation → Parliamentary approval (1 month, special majority)

## Practice Questions

**Q1.** Which Article provides the right to move Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights?
**A:** Article 32 (Dr Ambedkar called it the "heart and soul" of the Constitution)

**Q2.** What is the Basic Structure Doctrine?
**A:** Laid down in *Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala (1973)* — certain features of the Constitution (judicial review, separation of powers, federalism, secularism, democracy) cannot be amended even by Parliament under Article 368.

**Q3.** DPSP vs Fundamental Rights — which prevails?
**A:** FRs are justiciable and generally prevail, but *Minerva Mills (1980)* held that both must be harmoniously construed. Art 31C gives some DPSPs a shield — laws implementing Arts 39(b)/(c) cannot be challenged under Arts 14/19.

**Q4.** What are the three lists and which prevails in case of conflict?
**A:** Union List > Concurrent List > State List in case of conflict. On Concurrent List, Union law prevails under Art 254 unless State law has Presidential assent.
