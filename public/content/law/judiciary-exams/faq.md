# Judiciary Exams FAQ

**Q: Do I need any experience to apply for Civil Judge?**
A: No — most states do not require advocacy experience for Civil Judge (Jr Division). LLB degree + age eligibility is sufficient. Only District Judge (Direct) recruitment requires 7 years' advocacy experience.

**Q: What is the age limit?**
A: Typically 21–35 years for Civil Judge. Up to 45 years for District Judge (direct). Age relaxation for SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), PwD (10 years), and in-service candidates. Always check state-specific notification.

**Q: Can someone who studied in Hindi medium succeed in judiciary exams?**
A: Absolutely. Most state judiciary exams have option papers in Hindi and state regional languages. Mains can usually be answered in Hindi or English. The judgment writing exercise also has a Hindi option in most states. Language should not be a barrier.

**Q: Is coaching necessary for judiciary exams?**
A: Not mandatory but helpful for structured preparation, test series, and mock interviews. Many successful candidates have cleared through self-study using standard textbooks. Online test series (available ₹3,000–₹10,000/year) provide structured practice without expensive classroom coaching.

**Q: How many times can I attempt judiciary exams?**
A: Unlimited attempts within the age limit. Most states do not restrict the number of attempts — only age is the bar.

**Q: What happens after selection?**
A: Selected candidates undergo training at State Judicial Academy for 1–2 years, then posted as Civil Judge / Judicial Magistrate. Incremental salary increases, promotions based on performance + seniority. Elevation to High Court bench possible for outstanding judges (High Court collegium system).

**Q: Is UP PCS J the toughest judiciary exam?**
A: UP PCS J is considered among the most competitive due to the sheer number of applicants (2–4 lakh applications for ~300–400 posts). Delhi and Maharashtra exams are also highly competitive. However, syllabus and difficulty are broadly similar across states.

# Judiciary Exams FAQ — Complete

**Q: Do I need any experience to apply for Civil Judge?**

**Q: What is the age limit?**
Typically 21-35 years for Civil Judge Jr Division. Up to 45 years for District Judge (Direct). Age relaxation: SC/ST (5 years), OBC (3 years), PwD (10 years), in-service candidates (varies). Always check the specific state notification.

**Q: Can someone who studied in Hindi medium succeed?**
Absolutely. Most state judiciary exams offer option papers in Hindi and regional languages. Mains can be answered in Hindi or English. Judgment writing also has Hindi option in most states.

**Q: Is coaching necessary?**
Not mandatory. Many candidates clear through self-study using standard textbooks. Online test series (Rs 3,000-10,000/year) provide structured practice without expensive classroom coaching.

**Q: How many attempts are allowed?**
Unlimited attempts within the age limit. Most states have no attempt cap — only age is the limiting factor.

**Q: What happens after selection?**
Selected candidates undergo training at State Judicial Academy for 1-2 years, then posted as Civil Judge or Judicial Magistrate. Promotions based on performance and seniority. Merit-based elevation to High Court bench is possible for outstanding judges.

**Q: Is UP PCS J the toughest judiciary exam?**
UP PCS J is among the most competitive — 2-4 lakh applications for 300-500 posts. Delhi and Maharashtra are also highly competitive. Difficulty is broadly similar across states; competition level varies.

**Q: What is the salary after joining?**
Civil Judge Jr Division: Rs 77,840 basic + DA/HRA = approximately Rs 1.1-1.5 lakh/month all-in. Plus official residence, vehicle allowance, security. Pension on retirement. Increments every year. Promotion to Additional District Judge after DPC.

**Q: Can judiciary officers be transferred to other states?**
No — state judicial service officers serve within their state cadre. All India Judicial Service (AIJS) has been proposed but not implemented. High Court and Supreme Court judges can be transferred between courts by collegium recommendation.

**Q: What is the difference between Judicial Magistrate and Executive Magistrate?**
Judicial Magistrate: part of judiciary, appointed by/under High Court, handles criminal cases (trials, bail, remand). Executive Magistrate: part of executive, appointed under state government (SDM, Tehsildar), handles administrative orders (Section 144 CrPC, maintenance orders). Both are called Magistrates but serve very different functions.
