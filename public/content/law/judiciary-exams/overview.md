# Judiciary Exams — Civil Judge & District Judge

## Overview of Judicial Services in India

Judicial Services provide direct entry into the lower judiciary. These are among the most prestigious government positions — you become a judge from Day 1.

---

## Three-Tier Entry System

```
HIGHER JUDICIAL SERVICE (District Judge / ADD. District Judge)
  — Direct recruitment by High Court (Art 233)
  — Eligibility: Advocate with 7 years' practice OR from lower judicial service
  — Written exam + Interview conducted by State Public Service Commission + HC
        ↓
CIVIL JUDGE (Junior Division) / JUDICIAL MAGISTRATE
  — Entry-level judicial post
  — Eligibility: LLB + age 21–35 (varies by state), no practice required
  — Written exam conducted by State PSC / HC
  — Most competitive — thousands apply, 100–500 selected per state
        ↓
METROPOLITAN MAGISTRATE (in metro cities)
  — Equivalent to Judicial Magistrate
  — Same recruitment process
```

## Exam Pattern — Civil Judge (Jr Division) — Typical 3-Stage Process

### Stage 1: Preliminary (Objective)
- 150–200 MCQs
- Subjects: Law of Torts, Contract Act, Constitutional Law, IPC, CrPC, CPC, Evidence, Transfer of Property, Limitation, Local/Special Laws
- 2 hours
- Qualifying: 60% general, 55% reserved categories

### Stage 2: Mains (Descriptive)
**Paper I — General Knowledge & Language (200 marks)**
- Current affairs, Indian polity, history, geography
- Essay and precis writing in Hindi/English/state language

**Paper II — Law (300 marks)**
- Constitutional Law: 50 marks
- IPC/BNS + CrPC/BNSS: 75 marks
- CPC + Evidence: 75 marks
- Contract + TP Act: 50 marks
- Local and Special Laws: 50 marks

**Paper III — Law Practical (200 marks)**
- Drafting: judgment writing, pleadings, orders
- Problem-based questions requiring application of law

### Stage 3: Interview / Viva
- 75–100 marks
- Tests personality, current legal awareness, reasoning ability

## Preparation Strategy

### Year 1: Foundation
**Months 1–4:** Constitution + Constitutional Law thoroughly
- Read Bare Constitution (all 470 articles, all schedules)
- M.P. Jain or D.D. Basu for commentary
- Focus: FRs, DPSPs, Federalism, Emergency, Amendment

**Months 5–8:** Criminal Law
- BNS/IPC + BNSS/CrPC + BSA/Evidence
- Read bare acts daily, practice section recall
- Previous year papers: UP PCS J, MP Judicial Service

**Months 9–12:** Civil Law
- CPC + Contract Act + Transfer of Property + Limitation
- Order-by-order CPC (Order 1–51)
- Mock court exercises

### Year 2: Advanced + Revision
**Months 1–6:** Local Laws, Judgment Writing, Essay
- Study state-specific laws (Land Revenue, Local Bodies, Tenancy)
- Practice 2 judgments per week in proper format
- General knowledge newspaper reading daily

**Months 7–12:** Full test series
- Attempt full mock tests weekly
- Previous year papers of target state
- Group study for discussion of case analysis

## Judgment Writing Format

A proper judgment must have:

```
IN THE COURT OF [Court Name]
[Case Number]
[Date]

[Parties]
Plaintiff/Complainant: [Name]
vs
Defendant/Accused: [Name]

JUDGMENT

1. Brief facts:
   [Chronological summary of events]

2. Issues for determination:
   (i) Whether [Issue 1]?
   (ii) Whether [Issue 2]?

3. Findings on Issues:
   Issue No. 1: [Analysis + Finding]
   Issue No. 2: [Analysis + Finding]

4. Decision/Order:
   [Final order — relief granted/denied, sentence, costs]

   Sd/-
   [Name of Judge]
   [Designation]
   [Date]
```

## Key Differences: Civil Judge vs District Judge Exam

| Aspect | Civil Judge (Jr) | District Judge (Direct) |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Eligibility | LLB, no experience | 7 years advocacy |
| Syllabus depth | Moderate | Advanced + Local laws |
| Interview weight | Lower | Higher |
| Starting salary | ~₹70,000/month (7th PC) | ~₹1,20,000/month |
| Promotion | To Additional/District Judge | To HC (through elevation) |
| States with direct DJ | UP, Bihar, MP, Raj, others | Most states |

## Salary & Perks — Judicial Officers

**Civil Judge (Jr Division) — Entry Level:**
- Basic pay: ₹77,840 (7th Pay Commission)
- Total emoluments with DA/HRA: ₹1,10,000–₹1,50,000/month
- Government accommodation (bungalow)
- Vehicle + fuel allowance
- Security personnel
- Pension on retirement

**District and Sessions Judge:**
- Basic pay: ₹1,44,200
- Total: ₹2,20,000–₹2,80,000/month
- Official residence, vehicle, court staff

**High Court Judge (elevation from services):**
- Salary: ₹2,50,000/month
- Official bungalow, multiple support staff, Z-category security
