## What is Organic Farming?

Chemical fertilisers kill soil microorganisms over time creating a dependency cycle. Organic farming rebuilds soil biology. Feed the SOIL not the plant — healthy soil makes nutrients available naturally, produces food with no pesticide residue, and costs less after year 3 as input costs drop.

## Why Organic Farming?

- Year 1: Transition — yield may drop 10-20%
- Year 2: Soil recovers — same yield as chemical
- Year 3+: Better yield, near-zero input cost, 20-50% premium selling price

### Module 02 — Soil Preparation
*Compost, vermicompost, jeevamrut, mulching*

Good soil is dark, crumbly, sweet-smelling and full of earthworms. Compost is decomposed kitchen and farm waste. Vermicompost is 7x more nutritious. Jeevamrut activates billions of soil microorganisms.

**Topics covered:**

- How to make compost at home in 45-60 days — 🟢 Beginner
- Vermicompost — 7x nutrients vs regular compost — 🟢 Beginner
- Jeevamrut — Subhash Palekar ZBNF method — 🟡 Intermediate
- Soil testing: pH and nutrients — 🟡 Intermediate
- Mulching — 50% water saving — 🟢 Beginner

```bash
# COMPOST — ready in 45-60 days
# Ratio: 3 parts dry (leaves, cardboard) : 1 part wet (kitchen waste)
# Add cow dung as activator
# Turn every 7-10 days, keep moist
# Apply: 1-2 kg per sq.metre before planting

# JEEVAMRUT (for 200 litres):
# 10 kg desi cow dung + 5L cow urine
# 2 kg jaggery + 2 kg gram flour
# Handful soil from under large tree
# Ferment 48 hours, stir twice daily
# Use: 10% dilution, drench soil every 15 days
# Benefit: Activates 300 crore microorganisms per ml

# MULCHING: 3-4 inches dry leaves or rice straw
# Reduces watering by 50%, suppresses weeds
```

### Module 04 — Natural Pest Control
*Neem, panchagavya, organic sprays — zero chemicals*

Neem disrupts the life cycle of 200+ insect pests without harming bees or birds. Prevention is always better than cure. 80% of pest problems come from unhealthy soil or stressed plants.

**Topics covered:**

- Neem oil spray — works on 200+ insect pests — 🟢 Beginner
- Panchagavya — immunity booster and growth promoter — 🟡 Intermediate
- Chilli-garlic spray for insects — 🟢 Beginner
- Bordeaux mixture for fungal diseases — 🟡 Intermediate
- Yellow sticky traps for flying insects — 🟢 Beginner

```bash
# NEEM SPRAY (most important organic pesticide):
# 5 ml neem oil + 1-2 ml liquid soap per litre water
# Shake well, spray both sides of leaves in evening
# Every 7-10 days preventively
# Works on: aphids, whitefly, mites, caterpillars, fungal diseases

# CHILLI-GARLIC SPRAY:
# 10 green chillies + 10 garlic cloves blended in 1L water
# Strain, dilute 1:10, spray on plants

# BORDEAUX MIXTURE (fungal diseases):
# 100g copper sulphate + 100g lime in 10L water
# Apply before monsoon to prevent blight and rust
```

### Module 05 — Crop-Specific Organic Guides
*Rice, vegetables, fruits, spices, dal, cotton*

SRI rice method uses 50% less water and increases yield 30-50% with no chemicals. Turmeric is the easiest organic crop. Dal crops (legumes) fix nitrogen from air — they actually improve soil for the next crop.

**Topics covered:**

- Rice — SRI method: 50% less water, higher yield — 🟡 Intermediate
- Turmeric and ginger — easiest organic crops — 🟡 Intermediate
- Dal crops — nitrogen fixing, minimal inputs — 🟡 Intermediate
- Mango and banana organic orchard care — 🟡 Intermediate
- Organic cotton farming — 🔴 Advanced
- Corn and vegetable stage-wise inputs — 🟢 Beginner

```bash
# SRI METHOD FOR RICE (System of Rice Intensification):
# 1. Young seedlings 8-12 days old (2-leaf stage)
# 2. Single seedling per hole (not clumps)
# 3. Wide spacing: 25x25 cm (vs 15x15 traditional)
# 4. Alternate wet-dry irrigation (not flooding)
# 5. Mechanical weeder for weed control
# Organic inputs: compost + jeevamrut
# Result: 6-8 tonnes/acre vs 3-4 tonnes chemical

# TURMERIC:
# Plant: April-May (monsoon onset), spacing 30x30 cm
# Inputs: 5 kg compost per sq.m + jeevamrut every 30 days
# Harvest: 8-9 months | Yield: 20-25 tonnes/acre fresh

# DAL CROPS (moong, toor, chana, urad):
# Legumes fix atmospheric nitrogen — leave soil richer
# Inputs: Rhizobium seed treatment + 2 jeevamrut applications
```

### Module 06 — Water Conservation
*Drip irrigation, mulch, rainwater harvesting, farm pond*

Organic soil holds water much better than dead chemical soil — like a sponge vs concrete. Drip irrigation saves 60%. Mulching saves 50%. Organic farms survive droughts better because of superior soil water retention.

**Topics covered:**

- Drip irrigation — 60% water saving — 🟡 Intermediate
- Mulching — 50% water saving — 🟢 Beginner
- Rainwater harvesting for farm or terrace — 🟡 Intermediate
- Farm pond construction — 🟡 Intermediate
- Swales and contour bunds — 🟡 Intermediate
