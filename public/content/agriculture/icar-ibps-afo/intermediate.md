# ICAR JRF / IBPS AFO — Exam Preparation

## IBPS AFO Exam Pattern

**IBPS AFO (Agricultural Field Officer)** is a Specialist Officer exam for banking:
- **Prelims:** Reasoning (50), English (50), Quantitative (50) — Total 150 marks
- **Mains:** Agriculture General Knowledge (60 Q/60 marks) + Reasoning & Computer (30) + English (40) + Banking Awareness (40) = 170 marks
- **Cut-off (Agriculture GK section):** Typically 70%+

### MSP (Minimum Support Price)
- Announced by Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) based on CACP (Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices) recommendations
- Fixed for 23 crops: 14 Kharif + 6 Rabi + 2 commercial
- MSP formula: Cost A2+FL (actual costs + family labour) basis — recently C2+50% is recommended but not officially adopted
- **High-value MSP crops to remember:** Paddy (₹2183/qtl), Wheat (₹2275/qtl), Arhar/Tur, Sunflower, Sesame (highest MSP among oilseeds)

## ICAR JRF — Subject Areas

### Agronomy (High Weightage)
- Farming systems: Monocropping, mixed cropping, inter-cropping, crop rotation, relay cropping
- Critical periods of crop weed competition (CPWC): differs per crop
- Weed classification: grasses (narrow leaf), sedges, broadleaved
- Herbicides: pre-emergence (before germination) vs post-emergence
- Water use efficiency: ET ratio, irrigation scheduling, drip/sprinkler advantage

### Plant Breeding Concepts
- Heterosis (hybrid vigour): F1 hybrid > parent average. Exploited in maize, bajra, sunflower, cotton
- Inbreeding depression: loss of vigour with selfing in cross-pollinated crops
- Backcross breeding: transfer single gene from donor to adapted variety
- Pure line breeding: for self-pollinated crops (wheat, rice, gram)
- Polyploidy: bread wheat (hexaploid, 2n=42), sugarcane (polyploid)

### Plant Pathology — Major Disease Cycles
- **Blast of rice (Pyricularia oryzae):** Airborne conidia; attacks leaves, nodes, neck. Management: Tricyclazole, resistant varieties
- **Late blight of potato (Phytophthora infestans):** Oomycete; favours cool humid weather; caused Great Irish Famine (1845)
- **Wilt of pigeon pea (Fusarium udum):** Soil-borne; blocks vascular tissue; resistant varieties best option
- **Wheat rusts:** Yellow rust (cool climate, stripe pattern), Brown rust (moderate, circular pustules), Black rust (humid, large pustules)

### Soil Science
- **Soil pH:** Acidic (<6): add lime; Alkaline (>8.5): add gypsum + organic matter
- **Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC):** Measure of soil's ability to retain cations; higher = better fertility
- **Soil texture:** Sandy (fast drainage, low fertility), Clay (slow drainage, high fertility), Loam (ideal)
- **Organic matter benefits:** Improves structure, water holding, CEC, microbial activity
