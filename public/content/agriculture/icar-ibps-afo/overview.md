## IBPS AFO — Agricultural Field Officer

**Eligibility:** Degree in Agriculture/Allied subjects (Horticulture, Animal Husbandry, Fisheries, Forestry, Veterinary Science, Agricultural Engineering)

**Exam Pattern:**
- **Prelims:** Reasoning (35), English (35), Quantitative (35) — 105 marks, 1 hour
- **Mains:** Reasoning (50), English (40), Quantitative (50), General Awareness (40), Professional Knowledge (60) — 200 marks, 2.5 hours
- **Interview:** 100 marks

**Professional Knowledge Syllabus (AFO):**
- Agronomy: Crop production, soil-water-plant relationships
- Horticulture: Fruits, vegetables, flowers, post-harvest
- Agricultural Economics: Farm management, land reforms, cooperative farming
- Plant Pathology: Major diseases, pathogens, control measures
- Entomology: Pest identification, integrated pest management
- Animal Husbandry: Breeds, milk production, poultry, fisheries basics
- Soil Science: Soil classification, fertility, irrigation, drainage
- Agricultural Engineering: Farm machinery, water harvesting
- Agricultural Marketing: APMC, MSP, FCI, e-NAM
- Government Schemes: PM-KISAN, PMFBY, Kisan Credit Card, MIDH

## ICAR JRF — Junior Research Fellowship

**Eligibility:** B.Sc./B.Sc.(Hons) Agriculture or equivalent (55% for General, 50% for SC/ST)

**Exam Pattern:** 120 MCQs — Part A (30 Qs: General Science, Mathematics, Reasoning) + Part B (90 Qs: subject-specific)

**JRF benefits:**
- Fellowship: ₹31,000/month (first 2 years) → ₹35,000/month (after upgrading to SRF)
- Contingency grant: ₹20,000/year
- Leads to Ph.D. + career as scientist, professor, researcher at ICAR institutes

## Indian Agriculture at a Glance — Key Facts

**Contribution:** Agriculture contributes ~17% of India's GDP (2023-24); employs ~45% of workforce

**Cropping Seasons:**
- **Kharif (June–November):** Sown at monsoon onset. Rice, maize, cotton, soybean, groundnut, bajra, jowar
- **Rabi (October–March):** Sown post-monsoon. Wheat, barley, gram, mustard, peas, lentils
- **Zaid (March–June):** Short-duration summer crops. Watermelon, muskmelon, cucumber, vegetables

**Major Producing States:**

## Agricultural Policy — Key Schemes

**PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi):**
- ₹6,000/year (₹2,000 per installment) to all farmer families
- Directly to bank accounts
- Launched: February 2019

**PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana):**
- Crop insurance at subsidised premium: 2% for kharif, 1.5% for rabi, 5% for commercial/horticulture
- Actuarial premium paid by government (Centre + State 50:50)
- Implemented through empanelled insurance companies

**MSP (Minimum Support Price):**
- Guaranteed price for farmers
- CACP (Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices) recommends MSP
- Government procures at MSP through FCI, NAFED, SFAC
- Kharif 2024: Rice MSP = ₹2,300/quintal; Wheat = ₹2,275/quintal

**PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY):**
- "Har Khet Ko Pani, More Crop Per Drop"
- Irrigation coverage, micro-irrigation (drip, sprinkler)
- AIBP (Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme) for completing old projects

**e-NAM (Electronic National Agriculture Market):**
- Online trading platform for agricultural commodities
- Connects APMCs across states
- Farmers can sell produce from home APMC to buyers anywhere
- 1,260+ mandis integrated (as of 2024)

## Land Reforms in India

| Reform | Period | Significance |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Abolition of Zamindari | 1950–1960s | Eliminated intermediaries; land vested in state |
| Tenancy reforms | 1950s–70s | Security of tenure for tenants, rent regulation |
| Land ceiling laws | 1960s–70s | Maximum land holding limit per family; surplus to landless |
| Consolidation of holdings | Ongoing | Merge fragmented plots |
| Bhoodan-Gramdan | 1950s | Voluntary donation of land — Vinoba Bhave movement |

**Current challenges:** Sub-optimal farm size (average 1.08 ha), fragmentation, land record digitisation (DILRMP scheme), lack of land leasing market.
