# Crop Science — Applied Agronomy & Production

## Kharif vs Rabi vs Zaid Crops

| Season | Sown | Harvested | Examples |
|--------|------|-----------|---------|
| Kharif | June–July (monsoon onset) | Sept–Oct | Rice, Maize, Sorghum, Cotton, Groundnut, Soybean, Tur |
| Rabi | Oct–Nov (post-monsoon) | March–April | Wheat, Mustard, Barley, Chickpea (Gram), Peas, Linseed |
| Zaid | March–June (summer) | June–July | Watermelon, Muskmelon, Cucumber, Vegetables |

## Major Crop Production — India

### Rice (Paddy) — Oryza sativa
- **Area:** 44 million hectares (largest in world)
- **Top producers:** West Bengal, UP, Andhra Pradesh, Punjab, Bihar
- **Water requirement:** 1200–2000 mm (highly water-intensive)
- **Varieties:** IR-36, Jaya (high yielding), Basmati (export quality), Swarna (flood tolerant)
- **Soils:** Clay loam to clay (high water retention), pH 5.5–6.5
- **Nutrients:** High N requirement (split dose), P at basal, K for grain filling
- **Pests:** Brown planthopper (BPH) — most serious; stem borer, leaf folder
- **Diseases:** Blast (Pyricularia oryzae), Sheath blight, Bacterial leaf blight (BLB)

### Wheat — Triticum aestivum
- **Area:** 32 million hectares
- **Top producers:** UP, Punjab, Haryana, MP, Rajasthan
- **Water requirement:** 450–650 mm (5–6 irrigations)
- **Critical irrigation stages:** CRI (21 days), Tillering (40 days), Jointing (60 days), Boot, Heading, Dough stage
- **Varieties:** HD-2967, GW-322, DBW-17, K-307; MACS-6222 (rust resistant)
- **Nutrients:** 120 N, 60 P, 40 K kg/ha (split nitrogen: half basal, half at CRI)
- **Diseases:** Yellow rust (most important), Brown rust, Black rust, Loose smut, Karnal bunt

### Cotton — Gossypium hirsutum (American) / G. arboreum (Desi)
- **Area:** 12 million hectares
- **Top producers:** Gujarat, Maharashtra, Telangana, AP, Rajasthan
- **Soil:** Deep black cotton soil (regur), well-drained
- **Season:** Kharif (sown May–June, picked Oct–Feb)
- **Critical pest:** Bollworm complex (American bollworm, Spotted bollworm, Pink bollworm), Whitefly
- **Bt Cotton:** 95%+ of India's cotton is Bt hybrid — introduced 2002, controls bollworms
- **Irrigation:** 6–8 irrigations; critical at squaring, flowering, boll development

## Soil Nutrients — NPK Functions

| Nutrient | Primary Function | Deficiency Symptom |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| N (Nitrogen) | Chlorophyll, protein synthesis, vegetative growth | Yellowing from older leaves (bottom up), stunted growth |
| P (Phosphorus) | Root development, flowering, seed formation, energy (ATP) | Purple/reddish discolouration of leaves, poor root growth |
| K (Potassium) | Stomatal regulation, disease resistance, enzyme activation | Marginal leaf scorch (browning at leaf edges), starting from older leaves |
| Ca (Calcium) | Cell wall formation, meristem growth | Tip burn, blossom end rot (tomato), apical damage |
| Mg (Magnesium) | Chlorophyll (central atom), enzyme cofactor | Interveinal chlorosis — veins remain green, rest yellows |
| S (Sulphur) | Protein synthesis (cysteine, methionine), oil crops | Yellowing of young leaves (unlike N which starts from old) |
| Fe (Iron) | Chlorophyll synthesis, enzyme cofactor | Interveinal chlorosis on young leaves |
| Zn (Zinc) | Enzyme activation, IAA synthesis | White bud in maize, Khaira disease in rice |

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

### IPM Principles (WHO definition)
Compatible combination of pest control tactics that minimize economic, health, and environmental risks.

### IPM Components (in order of preference)
1. **Cultural control:** Crop rotation, resistant varieties, sowing time adjustment, sanitation
2. **Mechanical/Physical:** Yellow sticky traps, light traps, pheromone traps, hand picking
3. **Biological control:** Natural enemies (predators, parasitoids, pathogens)
   - Chrysoperla (predator): aphids, whitefly, mites
   - Trichogramma (egg parasitoid): lepidopteran pests
   - Beauveria bassiana (entomopathogenic fungus): various insects
   - NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus): bollworm, Spodoptera
4. **Chemical control (last resort):** Use only when pest crosses Economic Threshold Level (ETL)

### Economic Thresholds (ETL) Examples
| Crop-Pest | ETL |
|-----------|-----|
| Rice — BPH | 5–10 insects per hill |
| Cotton — Bollworm | 5–8% damage or 5 eggs/25 plants |
| Wheat — Aphid | 100 aphids per 10 tillers |
| Tomato — Whitefly | 20 adults per plant |
