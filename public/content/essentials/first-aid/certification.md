## Core Exam Topics

```
CPR SEQUENCE (Adult):
  1. Check scene safety
  2. Check responsiveness — tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
  3. Call for help — dial 108 (India) / 911 (US) / 999 (UK)
  4. Open airway — head-tilt chin-lift
  5. Check breathing — look, listen, feel (10 seconds)
  6. Start chest compressions:
     - Position: heel of hand on centre of chest
     - Depth: 5–6 cm (adults), 4 cm (children)
     - Rate: 100–120 compressions per minute
     - Ratio: 30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths
  7. Use AED as soon as available — follow voice prompts
  8. Continue until: victim recovers | EMS arrives | you are exhausted

AED USE:
  Power ON → attach pads (right collarbone, left side under arm)
  → CLEAR everyone → SHOCK if advised → resume CPR immediately

CHOKING (Heimlich Manoeuvre):
  Conscious adult: 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts, alternate
  Unconscious: start CPR, look in mouth before rescue breaths
  Infant: 5 back blows face-down + 5 chest thrusts face-up

BLEEDING CONTROL:
  Apply direct pressure with clean cloth — do not remove
  Elevate limb above heart level
  Tourniquet: only for life-threatening limb bleeding
  Pressure points: brachial (arm), femoral (leg)

BURNS:
  Cool running water 20 minutes (not ice, not butter)
  Cover with clean non-stick dressing
  Seek medical help for: face, hands, genitals, >1% body area, deep burns
  DO NOT: burst blisters, remove stuck clothing, use toothpaste

FRACTURES:
  Immobilise above and below injury — do not straighten deformed limb
  Apply splint with padding | check circulation distal to fracture
  Signs: pain, swelling, deformity, inability to use limb

SHOCK MANAGEMENT:
  Lay flat, raise legs 30 cm (unless head/chest/spine injury)
  Keep warm, reassure, do NOT give food or drink
  Monitor breathing and consciousness
  Call emergency services immediately
```
