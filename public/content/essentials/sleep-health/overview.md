# Sleep & Recovery — Overview

## Why Sleep is the Foundation of Health

Sleep is not downtime — it's active restoration. During sleep:
- **Brain**: Clears metabolic waste (amyloid beta), consolidates memories, processes emotions
- **Body**: Repairs tissue, synthesizes proteins, releases growth hormone
- **Immune system**: Produces cytokines, fights infection
- **Heart**: Blood pressure drops 10-20% (non-dippers have higher cardiovascular risk)

**Impact of sleep deprivation (even 1 week of 6hr sleep):**
- Cognitive performance equivalent to 24 hours without sleep
- Immune response drops by 30%
- Cortisol rises, testosterone falls
- Insulin sensitivity decreases (pre-diabetic glucose response)

## How Much Sleep Do You Need?

| Age Group | Recommended Sleep |
|-----------|------------------|
| Adults (18–64) | 7–9 hours |
| Older adults (65+) | 7–8 hours |
| Teenagers (14–17) | 8–10 hours |
| School age (6–13) | 9–11 hours |

**Quality matters as much as quantity.** 8 hours of interrupted sleep ≠ 8 hours of continuous sleep.

## Sleep Architecture — The 4 Stages

```
Stage 1 (N1): Light sleep, 5–10 min, easy to wake, hypnic jerks common
Stage 2 (N2): True sleep onset, 20 min, body temperature drops, heart slows
Stage 3 (N3): Deep sleep (slow-wave), 20–40 min, hardest to wake, physical restoration
REM:          Dream sleep, 20–25% of total sleep, memory consolidation, emotional processing

One sleep cycle = ~90 minutes. 5 cycles = 7.5 hours.
Deep sleep dominates early cycles; REM dominates later cycles.
```

## Evidence-Based Sleep Hygiene

### Environment (most impactful)
- **Temperature**: 65-68°F (18-20°C) is optimal for sleep onset
- **Darkness**: 0 lux during sleep. Blackout curtains or sleep mask.
- **Noise**: < 30 dB or use consistent white/pink noise
- **Phone**: Outside bedroom, or at minimum face-down, Do Not Disturb on

### Timing (circadian entrainment)
- **Consistent wake time**: The most powerful circadian signal. Even weekends.
- **Morning light**: Get outside within 30 minutes of waking (no sunglasses). Sets cortisol peak.
- **Evening light**: Dim lights after sunset. Blue light from screens delays melatonin by 1.5–3 hours.

### Behaviours
- **Caffeine cutoff**: 12–14 hours before bed (half-life = 5–7 hours; quarter-life = 10–14 hours)
  - If you sleep at 11pm, last coffee should be 10–11am
- **Alcohol**: Disrupts REM sleep and causes fragmented sleep second half of night
- **Exercise**: Improves sleep quality, but intense exercise < 3 hours before bed can delay sleep
- **Wind-down routine**: 30–60 minutes of low stimulation before bed

## Common Sleep Problems

### Can't fall asleep (sleep onset insomnia)
- Racing thoughts → Try 4-7-8 breathing or body scan meditation
- Too warm → Drop room temperature
- Caffeine too late → Track intake timing
- Blue light → Use f.lux or Night Shift after sunset

### Wake up in middle of night (sleep maintenance insomnia)
- Alcohol → Avoid after 7pm
- Bladder → Limit fluids 2 hours before bed
- Stress → Write tomorrow's tasks before bed ("offloading")
- Sleep apnea → Suspect if snoring, morning headaches, unrefreshed after sleep

### Can't wake up (delayed sleep phase)
- Social jet lag (late weekends) → Fix wake time 7 days a week
- Insufficient morning light → Go outside immediately upon waking
- Too much evening light → Strict light discipline after 8pm

## Tracking Sleep

- **Oura Ring**: Most accurate consumer device (HRV, sleep stages, temperature)
- **Apple Watch / Fitbit**: Good for trends, less accurate on stages
- **Free**: Just track bedtime, wake time, and subjective morning freshness (1–10 scale)

**Key metric to track**: HRV (Heart Rate Variability) — higher = better recovered, lower = stressed/ill/overtraining
