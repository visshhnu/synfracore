# Nutrition — Intermediate Guide

## Intermediate Nutrition

### Reading Food Labels and Planning

```
FOOD LABEL READING:
  Serving size: all values based on this — check portion vs full pack
  Calories per serving: multiply by servings eaten
  
  Nutrients to LIMIT:
    Saturated fat: <10% total calories (<20g/day for 2000kcal diet)
    Trans fat: 0g (avoid completely)
    Sodium: <2,300mg/day
    Added sugars: <50g/day (WHO: <25g/day for additional benefit)
  
  Nutrients to GET ENOUGH:
    Dietary fibre: 25–38g/day
    Protein: per your RDA (0.8–2.0g/kg depending on activity)
    Vitamins and minerals: check % DV (daily value)
  
  INGREDIENT LIST:
    Ordered by weight — first ingredient is most abundant
    Watch for hidden sugars: corn syrup, dextrose, maltose, cane juice
    Watch for hidden sodium: monosodium glutamate, sodium benzoate

MEAL PLANNING:
  Step 1: Calculate calorie needs (TDEE = BMR × activity)
  Step 2: Set macro targets (protein first, then carbs + fats)
  Step 3: Plan meals around protein source
  Step 4: Add vegetables and fruits (half the plate)
  Step 5: Choose whole grain carbohydrates
  Step 6: Include healthy fats in small amounts
  Step 7: Prep in advance — Sunday batch cooking

HYDRATION:
  General: 2–3 litres water/day (more in heat or exercise)
  Thirst is a late indicator of dehydration — monitor urine colour
  Urine colour guide: pale yellow = well hydrated | dark yellow = drink more
  Electrolytes needed after 90+ min exercise or excessive sweating
  Avoid: sugary drinks as primary hydration, excessive caffeine

TRADITIONAL INDIAN DIET STRENGTHS:
  Dal + rice/roti: complete protein combination
  Turmeric (curcumin): anti-inflammatory properties
  Curd/buttermilk: probiotics, calcium
  Seasonal vegetables: diverse phytonutrients
  Cooking methods: mostly steamed, boiled, stir-fried (healthier than deep-fried)
```
