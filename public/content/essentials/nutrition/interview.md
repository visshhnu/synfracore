# Nutrition — Key Concepts and Q&A

## Core Concepts

**Q: Macronutrients — what they are and how much you need.**

**Carbohydrates** (4 kcal/g): Primary energy source. Brain runs almost exclusively on glucose.
- Simple: sugars (glucose, fructose, lactose) — fast energy, spikes blood sugar
- Complex: starch, fibre — slow digestion, stable blood sugar
- Recommended: 45-65% of total calories. Emphasis on whole grains, vegetables, legumes.
- Fibre: 25-38g/day. Feeds gut bacteria, regulates blood sugar, bowel health.

**Proteins** (4 kcal/g): Building blocks (amino acids). Muscle, enzymes, hormones, immune function.
- Essential amino acids (9): must come from diet (cannot synthesise)
- Complete proteins: all essential AAs — meat, fish, eggs, dairy, quinoa, soy
- Recommended: 0.8g/kg body weight (sedentary). 1.2-2.2g/kg for athletes.
- Higher intake supports muscle retention, satiety, metabolic rate.

**Fats** (9 kcal/g): Energy storage, hormones, fat-soluble vitamins (A,D,E,K), cell membranes.
- Saturated: animal fats, coconut oil — moderate intake
- Unsaturated: nuts, seeds, avocado, olive oil — cardioprotective
- Omega-3 (EPA, DHA): anti-inflammatory, brain health — fatty fish, flaxseed, walnuts
- Trans fats: industrial (partially hydrogenated oils) — avoid completely
- Recommended: 20-35% of calories. Avoid trans fats; limit saturated.

**Q: Micronutrients — key vitamins and minerals.**

| Nutrient | Key Function | Sources | Deficiency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vitamin D | Bone, immunity, mood | Sunlight, fortified foods, fatty fish | Rickets, immune dysfunction |
| Vitamin B12 | Nerve function, RBC formation | Meat, dairy, eggs (vegans supplement) | Anemia, nerve damage |
| Iron | Haemoglobin (carries oxygen) | Red meat, lentils, spinach + vitamin C | Anemia, fatigue |
| Calcium | Bone, muscle, nerve | Dairy, leafy greens, tofu | Osteoporosis |
| Iodine | Thyroid hormones | Iodised salt, seafood | Goitre, hypothyroidism |
| Zinc | Immunity, wound healing | Meat, shellfish, seeds | Poor wound healing |
| Magnesium | 300+ enzyme reactions | Nuts, seeds, whole grains | Muscle cramps, fatigue |
| Folate (B9) | DNA synthesis, fetal development | Leafy greens, legumes | Neural tube defects (pregnancy) |
| Vitamin C | Collagen, immunity, antioxidant | Citrus, bell peppers, amla | Scurvy |

**Q: Balanced diet frameworks.**

**Harvard Healthy Eating Plate:**
- ½ plate: vegetables and fruits (variety of colours)
- ¼ plate: whole grains (oats, brown rice, whole wheat)
- ¼ plate: protein (fish, poultry, beans, nuts, eggs)
- Side: healthy oils (olive, mustard, groundnut)
- Water/unsweetened drinks. Limit red meat, processed foods, sugary drinks.

**Indian dietary context:**
- Traditional Indian diet can be nutritionally complete: dal-chawal-sabzi-roti combination provides complete nutrition
- Concern: low vitamin D (despite sun), B12 (vegetarian population), iron (women)
- Challenge: ultra-processed foods, maida (refined flour), excessive ghee/oil, high salt

**Q: Reading nutrition labels.**

```
Serving size: everything is per serving (may be misleading — check servings per pack)

Calories: total energy per serving

Nutrients to LIMIT:          Nutrients to GET ENOUGH:
Saturated fat (<10% DV)     Fibre (≥20% DV = good source)
Trans fat (0g ideally)      Vitamins (A, C, D, calcium, iron)
Sodium (<2300mg/day)        Protein
Added sugars (<10% DV)

% Daily Value: based on 2000 kcal diet
5% or less = low | 20% or more = high

Ingredients list: listed by weight (descending)
First ingredient is most. Sugar hidden as: dextrose, fructose, corn syrup, maltose.
```

**Q: Nutrition myths — evidence vs misinformation.**

**Myth: Carbs are bad.**
Fact: Refined carbs (white bread, sugary drinks) are problematic. Whole food carbs (vegetables, legumes, whole grains) are associated with better health outcomes.

**Myth: Fat makes you fat.**
Fact: Excess calories of any macronutrient cause fat gain. Healthy fats are essential. Low-fat foods often add sugar to compensate.

**Myth: You need to detox.**
Fact: Liver and kidneys continuously detoxify. No scientific evidence for "detox" products. Eating whole foods, staying hydrated, and sleeping supports natural detoxification.

**Myth: Protein supplements are necessary.**
Fact: Most people can meet protein needs through food. Supplements convenient but not essential unless food intake is insufficient.
