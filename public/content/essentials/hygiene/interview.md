# Hygiene — Key Concepts and Q&A

## Core Concepts

**Q: Infection prevention — how diseases spread and how to break the chain.**

Diseases spread through the "chain of infection":
1. Infectious agent (pathogen: bacteria, virus, fungus)
2. Reservoir (where it lives: person, animal, environment)
3. Portal of exit (how it leaves: respiratory droplets, faeces, blood, skin)
4. Mode of transmission (how it travels: contact, droplets, airborne, faecal-oral)
5. Portal of entry (how it enters new host: mouth, respiratory tract, skin break)
6. Susceptible host (weakened immunity, unvaccinated)

Break any link → prevent infection. Hand hygiene primarily breaks the contact transmission link.

---

**Q: Hand washing — the single most effective hygiene practice.**

Proper handwashing with soap and water: 20 seconds minimum.

**WHO 6-step method:**
1. Wet hands, apply soap
2. Palm to palm, rub
3. Back of hands
4. Between fingers
5. Backs of fingers (interlocked)
6. Thumbs
7. Tips of fingers, nails
8. Wrists

**When to wash:**
- Before eating or handling food
- After using toilet
- After coughing, sneezing, blowing nose
- After touching animals
- After handling garbage
- Before and after caring for sick person

**Alcohol-based hand sanitiser** (60-95% alcohol): Good when hands not visibly dirty and soap unavailable. Does NOT work against: Clostridioides difficile (C.diff) spores, norovirus — need soap and water.

---

**Q: Food safety — temperature and cross-contamination.**

**Danger zone**: 5°C to 60°C (40°F to 140°F). Bacteria multiply rapidly in this range.
- Keep hot food hot: >60°C
- Keep cold food cold: <5°C
- Never leave perishables at room temperature >2 hours (1 hour if >32°C ambient)

**Temperature rules:**
- Fridge: ≤4°C | Freezer: ≤-18°C
- Cook chicken/poultry: 74°C internal temp
- Ground meat: 71°C | Whole cuts: 63°C | Fish: 63°C
- Leftovers: reheat to 74°C

**Cross-contamination prevention:**
- Separate raw meat from ready-to-eat foods
- Different cutting boards (colour-coded: red for meat, green for vegetables)
- Wash hands between handling raw and cooked
- Store raw meat on bottom shelf of fridge

---

**Q: Personal hygiene for health.**

**Dental hygiene** (often undervalued):
- Brush twice daily (2 minutes), floss once daily
- Poor oral hygiene linked to: cardiovascular disease (bacteria enter bloodstream), diabetes complications, preterm birth
- Replace toothbrush every 3 months or after illness

**Skin hygiene:**
- Wash daily, especially areas prone to bacterial growth (underarms, groin, feet)
- Moisturise to maintain skin barrier (skin is first line of immune defence)
- Clean wounds promptly

**Sleep hygiene:**
- Clean bedding (bacteria accumulate) — wash weekly
- Shower before bed (removes allergens, bacteria)
- Clean environment supports respiratory health

**Respiratory hygiene:**
- Cover mouth and nose when coughing/sneezing
- Use elbow (not hands) — hands spread droplets
- Dispose tissues immediately, wash hands after

---

**Q: Hygiene in healthcare/food service settings.**

**Standard Precautions** (healthcare): Treat all blood/body fluids as potentially infectious regardless of known diagnosis.

**PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)**: Gloves, mask, goggles, gown — selected based on risk.

**Donning (putting on) order**: Gown → Mask → Goggles/face shield → Gloves
**Doffing (removing) order**: Gloves → Goggles → Gown → Mask (most contaminated removed first)

**Food handling licence** (FSSAI in India): Required for food businesses. Covers: personal hygiene, temperature control, pest control, clean facilities.
