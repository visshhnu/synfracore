# Daily Movement and Exercise Overview

## Why Movement is Medicine

Sitting 8+ hours daily is an independent risk factor for early death even with separate exercise. The body evolved for constant low-level movement.

**Key benefits:**
- Reduces all-cause mortality by 30-35%
- Improves insulin sensitivity and reduces type 2 diabetes risk
- Increases BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) for better memory and focus
- Reduces mild-to-moderate depression symptoms as effectively as antidepressants
- Improves sleep quality and deepens slow-wave sleep

## WHO Minimum Guidelines

| Type | Minimum per Week |
|------|-----------------|
| Moderate aerobic (brisk walk, cycling) | 150 minutes |
| Vigorous aerobic (running, HIIT) | 75 minutes |
| Strength training | 2 sessions (all major muscle groups) |

## For Desk Workers

**20-20-20-2 Rule:**
- Every 20 minutes: look 20 feet away for 20 seconds (eye strain)
- Every 2 hours: stand and move for 2 minutes (circulation)

**Daily movement habits:**
- Morning: 5-minute stretch (hip flexors, spinal mobility)
- Lunch: 15-minute walk outside (sunlight + circulation)
- Evening: 30 minutes of any exercise you enjoy

## 20-Minute Home Workout (No Equipment)

```
Warm-up (3 min): Jumping jacks, arm circles
Circuit (14 min, 2 rounds):
  Push-ups: 15 reps
  Bodyweight squats: 20 reps
  Plank: 30 seconds
  Glute bridges: 15 reps
Cooldown (3 min): Static stretches, 30 sec each
```

## Signs You Need to Move More

- Afternoon energy crashes (2-4pm)
- Back or hip pain after sitting
- Difficulty concentrating after 1 hour desk work
- Resting heart rate above 75 bpm
- Waking tired despite 7-8 hours sleep
