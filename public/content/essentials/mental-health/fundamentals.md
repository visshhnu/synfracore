# Mental Health — Fundamentals

## Understanding Mental Health

```
Mental health = emotional, psychological, and social well-being
Affects: how we think, feel, act, handle stress, relate to others

Common conditions:
  Anxiety: excessive worry, physical symptoms (heart racing, sweating)
  Depression: persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue
  Stress: response to perceived threat/demand (acute vs chronic)
  Burnout: work-related exhaustion — emotional depletion + cynicism

NOT weaknesses or character flaws — biological and environmental factors
1 in 4 people experience mental health conditions in their lifetime
```

## Evidence-Based Self-Care

```
Sleep (most underrated):
  7-9 hours for adults
  Sleep deprivation = impaired cognition, mood, immunity
  Consistent sleep/wake times — even weekends
  No screens 30-60 min before bed (blue light disrupts melatonin)

Physical activity:
  150 min/week moderate intensity (brisk walk, cycling)
  Exercise = antidepressant (releases endorphins, BDNF)
  Even 10-min walks improve mood

Social connection:
  Strong relationships #1 predictor of happiness
  Quality > quantity
  Reach out first — others are waiting too

Stress management:
  Deep breathing: 4-7-8 (in 4, hold 7, out 8) — activates parasympathetic
  Progressive muscle relaxation: tense then relax each muscle group
  Journaling: write thoughts to process emotions
  Mindfulness: notice present moment without judgment
```

## When to Seek Help

```
Professional help is warranted when:
  - Symptoms persist > 2 weeks
  - Affecting daily functioning (work, relationships)
  - Thoughts of self-harm
  - Using substances to cope

Types of help:
  Therapist/Psychologist: Talk therapy (CBT, DBT, etc.)
  Psychiatrist: Medication management (MD who specializes in mental health)
  GP/Family doctor: First point of contact, can refer
```
