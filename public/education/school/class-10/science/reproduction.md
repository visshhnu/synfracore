# How Do Organisms Reproduce?

## Why This Chapter Matters
Reproduction is tested every year — 7-9 marks in CBSE boards. Asexual vs sexual reproduction, human reproductive system, and reproductive health are all important. Diagrams of flower parts and human reproductive organs are commonly asked.

## Prerequisites
- Cell division: mitosis (Class 9 — NCERT brief mention)
- Life Processes (Chapter 6)
- Basic genetics concept helpful

## Core Concepts

### 1. Why is Reproduction Important?
Maintains continuity of species. Without reproduction, species becomes extinct.
Reproduction is NOT essential for survival of an individual, but is essential for continuity of the species.

### 2. Types of Reproduction

#### Asexual Reproduction
- Single parent, no gametes (sex cells), offspring genetically identical to parent (clones)
- Faster, simpler, requires fewer resources

**Types in organisms:**

| Type | Example | How |
|---|---|---|
| Binary fission | Amoeba, bacteria | Single cell divides into 2 equal halves |
| Multiple fission | Plasmodium (malaria parasite) | Single cell divides into many daughter cells |
| Budding | Yeast, Hydra | Small bud forms on parent → detaches |
| Fragmentation | Spirogyra (algae) | Organism breaks into pieces → each grows |
| Regeneration | Planaria | Cut pieces regenerate into complete organisms |
| Spore formation | Rhizopus (bread mould), ferns | Spores released → germinate in suitable conditions |
| Vegetative propagation | Potato (eyes), Bryophyllum (leaf buds) | New plant grows from vegetative part |

#### Sexual Reproduction
- Two parents, gametes fuse (fertilisation), genetic variation in offspring
- More complex, produces variation (important for evolution)

### 3. Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

**Flower parts:**
- **Stamen** (male): Filament + Anther (produces pollen grains with male gamete)
- **Pistil/Carpel** (female): Stigma + Style + Ovary (ovule contains female gamete/egg)
- Sepals: protect flower bud
- Petals: attract pollinators

**Pollination:** Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma.
- **Self-pollination:** Same flower or same plant
- **Cross-pollination:** Different plant of same species (by wind, insects, water, birds)

**Fertilisation in plants:**
Pollen grain lands on stigma → germinates → pollen tube grows through style → reaches ovule → pollen tube enters ovule → male gamete fuses with egg → **ZYGOTE**

**Double fertilisation** (unique to angiosperms):
One male gamete + egg → zygote → embryo
Second male gamete + 2 polar nuclei → endosperm (nutrition for embryo)

**After fertilisation:**
- Ovule → Seed
- Ovary → Fruit
- Zygote → Embryo

**Fruit** = ripened ovary | **Seed** = ripened ovule

### 4. Human Reproductive System

#### Male Reproductive System
- **Testes** (pair): Produce sperms (male gametes) and testosterone
  - Located outside body in scrotum — sperm production needs temp slightly lower than body temp (37°C)
- **Epididymis:** Sperm maturation and storage
- **Vas deferens:** Tube carrying sperm from testes
- **Seminal vesicles + Prostate gland + Cowper's gland:** Produce fluids → together = semen
- **Urethra:** Common passage for urine and semen (not simultaneously)
- **Penis:** Transfers semen into female reproductive tract

#### Female Reproductive System
- **Ovaries** (pair): Produce eggs (female gametes/ova) and hormones (oestrogen, progesterone)
- **Fallopian tubes (oviducts):** Carry egg from ovary to uterus. FERTILISATION occurs here.
- **Uterus (womb):** Where foetus develops. Lined by endometrium.
- **Cervix:** Lower part of uterus
- **Vagina:** Birth canal, receives semen during intercourse

**Menstrual cycle (28 days):**
1. Menstruation (days 1-5): Uterus lining sheds (no fertilisation)
2. Follicular phase (days 1-13): Follicle develops, egg matures, oestrogen rises
3. Ovulation (day 14): Egg released from ovary
4. Luteal phase (days 15-28): Progesterone prepares uterus; if no fertilisation → cycle repeats

### 5. Contraception

**Methods:**
- **Barrier methods:** Condoms (also protect against STDs/STIs), diaphragm
- **Chemical methods:** Pills (hormonal — prevent ovulation), spermicides
- **Intrauterine devices (IUDs):** Copper-T placed in uterus
- **Surgical methods:** Vasectomy (males), tubectomy (females) — permanent
- **Emergency contraception:** Pills within 72 hours of unprotected intercourse

### 6. Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs/STDs)

**Bacterial:** Gonorrhoea, syphilis (treatable with antibiotics)
**Viral:** HIV/AIDS, genital herpes, genital warts (no complete cure)

**Prevention:** Condoms provide significant (not 100%) protection. Avoid multiple partners. Regular testing.

## PYQs

### 2023
**Q:** Define fertilisation. What is the site of fertilisation in humans?
Fertilisation = fusion of male gamete (sperm) and female gamete (egg/ovum) to form zygote.
In humans, fertilisation occurs in the **Fallopian tube** (oviduct).

### 2022
**Q:** Why are testes located outside the body cavity in humans?
Sperm production requires temperature slightly LOWER than body temperature (37°C).
Scrotum maintains 2-3°C lower temperature, essential for normal sperm production (spermatogenesis).

### 2021
**Q:** What is double fertilisation? Why is it significant?
In flowering plants, two fertilisations occur:
1. Sperm + Egg → Zygote (forms embryo)
2. Sperm + 2 polar nuclei → Primary endosperm nucleus (forms endosperm — nutrition for embryo)
Significance: Endosperm provides nutrition to developing embryo.

### 2020
**Q:** Explain vegetative propagation with two examples.
Vegetative propagation = reproduction using vegetative parts (root, stem, leaf) of a plant.
1. Potato: Eyes (axillary buds) develop into new plants.
2. Bryophyllum: Leaves have buds at margins that fall and grow into new plants.

## Revision Notes

```
ASEXUAL: 1 parent, no gametes, identical offspring
Types: Binary fission, Budding, Fragmentation, Spore formation, 
       Vegetative propagation, Regeneration, Multiple fission

SEXUAL: 2 parents, gametes fuse, variation in offspring
In plants: Pollination → Fertilisation → Seed + Fruit
  Ovule → Seed | Ovary → Fruit | Zygote → Embryo

HUMAN REPRODUCTION:
  Fertilisation site: Fallopian tube
  Development site: Uterus
  Testes outside body: lower temp needed for sperm

MENSTRUAL CYCLE: 28 days
  Day 14: Ovulation (egg released)
  Days 1-5: Menstruation (if no fertilisation)
```

**Common Mistakes:**
❌ Fertilisation in humans occurs in uterus — WRONG! It occurs in Fallopian tube
❌ Pollen grain is male gamete — WRONG! Pollen grain contains the male gamete
❌ Fruit is formed from ovule — WRONG! Fruit from ovary; seed from ovule
