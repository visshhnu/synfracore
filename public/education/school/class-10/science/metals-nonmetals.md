## Core Concepts

### 1. Physical Properties

Metals: Shiny, hard (except Na/K/Li), solid (except Hg), malleable, ductile, sonorous, good conductors.
Non-metals: Dull, soft, solid/liquid/gas, brittle, non-sonorous, poor conductors (except graphite).

### 2. Chemical Properties of Metals

**With Oxygen:**
2Mg + O2 -> 2MgO (burns bright white)
Al: forms protective Al2O3 layer (does not corrode further)
Gold, Platinum: do NOT react with oxygen

**With Water:**
Na: 2Na + 2H2O -> 2NaOH + H2 (very vigorous, may ignite)
Ca: Ca + 2H2O -> Ca(OH)2 + H2 (moderate)
Fe: 3Fe + 4H2O(steam) -> Fe3O4 + 4H2 (steam only)
Cu, Ag, Au: No reaction with water

**With Acids:**
Zn + 2HCl -> ZnCl2 + H2
Cu + HCl -> NO REACTION (Cu below H in reactivity series)

### 3. Reactivity Series
K > Na > Li > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Ni > Sn > Pb > [H] > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au > Pt

More reactive = displaces less reactive from salt solution.

### 4. Ionic Bond Formation
Metals lose electrons -> cations (+)
Non-metals gain electrons -> anions (-)
Na -> Na+ + e- | Cl + e- -> Cl-
Na+ + Cl- -> NaCl (ionic compound)

### 5. Extraction of Metals

**High reactivity (K, Na, Ca, Mg, Al):** Electrolysis (coke cannot reduce these)
**Medium reactivity (Zn, Fe, Sn, Pb):** Reduction with coke (smelting)
  ZnO + C -> Zn + CO | Fe2O3 + 3CO -> 2Fe + 3CO2 (blast furnace)
**Low reactivity (Cu, Hg, Ag, Au):** Heating alone
  2HgS + 3O2 -> 2HgO + 2SO2; 2HgO -> 2Hg + O2

**Refining:** Electrolytic refining — impure metal (anode) dissolves, pure metal deposits on cathode.

### 6. Corrosion and Alloys

**Rusting:** 4Fe + 3O2 + xH2O -> 2Fe2O3.xH2O (needs BOTH oxygen AND water)
**Prevention:** Painting, oiling, galvanising (zinc coating), tinning, electroplating, alloying.

**Alloys:**
- Brass: Cu + Zn (utensils)
- Bronze: Cu + Sn (statues, medals)
- Solder: Pb + Sn (joining metals, low melting point)
- Steel: Fe + C (construction)
- Stainless steel: Fe + C + Ni + Cr (cutlery)
- Duralumin: Al + Cu + Mg (aircraft)

Why alloys? Higher strength, lower reactivity, modified properties for specific uses.

## PYQs

**2023:** State the principle of electrolytic refining. Which electrode is pure metal?
Impure metal (anode) dissolves in electrolyte, pure metal deposits on cathode.

**2022:** Why is sodium stored in kerosene?
Na reacts with water: 2Na + 2H2O -> 2NaOH + H2 (vigorous, can ignite).
Kerosene prevents contact with moisture and air.

**2021:** Give one example of: (a) liquid metal (b) non-metal that conducts electricity
(a) Mercury (Hg) | (b) Graphite

**2020:** Write equation for reaction of iron with steam. What type of oxide?
3Fe + 4H2O(steam) -> Fe3O4 + 4H2
Fe3O4 is a basic oxide (magnetic iron oxide).

## MCQ Practice

Q1. Metal NOT reacting with cold water:
(A) Na (B) K (C) Mg (D) Ca
Answer: C (Mg reacts only with steam)

Q2. Al2O3 extraction method:
(A) Heating (B) Reduction with C (C) Electrolysis (D) Displacement
Answer: C

Q3 (Hard). Wire of resistance R stretched to double length. New resistance?
(A) R/2 (B) 2R (C) 4R (D) R/4
Answer: C [Length doubles (R=2R) + Area halves (R=2R again) = 4R total]

## Revision Notes

```
METALS: shiny, malleable, ductile, sonorous, conduct electricity
NON-METALS: dull, brittle, poor conductors (except graphite)

REACTIVITY SERIES (top = most reactive):
K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > H > Cu > Ag > Au

REACTIONS WITH WATER:
Na, K -> cold water (vigorous)
Ca -> cold water (moderate)
Mg, Al -> hot water or steam only
Fe -> steam only
Cu, Ag, Au -> no reaction

EXTRACTION:
High reactive -> Electrolysis
Medium -> Reduction with coke
Low -> Heating alone

CORROSION:
Rust = Fe2O3.xH2O (needs O2 AND water)
Prevention: painting, galvanising (Zn coating), alloying

ALLOYS: Brass(Cu+Zn), Bronze(Cu+Sn), Solder(Pb+Sn), Steel(Fe+C)
```
