## Core Concepts

### 1. Basic Proportionality Theorem (BPT) / Thales Theorem

**Statement:** If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle, it divides the other two sides in the **same ratio**.

If DE ‚ą• BC in ‚Ė≥ABC, then:
$$\frac{AD}{DB} = \frac{AE}{EC}$$

**Converse:** If a line divides two sides of a triangle in the same ratio, it is parallel to the third side.

**Memory:** BPT ‚Üí the line parallel to one side creates equal ratios on the other two sides.

---

### 2. Similarity of Triangles

Two triangles are **similar** if:
1. Their corresponding angles are equal, AND
2. Their corresponding sides are in the same ratio (proportional)

Notation: ‚Ė≥ABC ~ ‚Ė≥DEF means:
- ‚ą†A = ‚ą†D, ‚ą†B = ‚ą†E, ‚ą†C = ‚ą†F
- AB/DE = BC/EF = CA/FD

---

### 3. Criteria for Similarity

#### AA (Angle-Angle) Similarity
If two angles of one triangle are equal to two angles of another triangle, the triangles are similar.

*Since angle sum = 180¬į, if two pairs of angles are equal, the third pair is automatically equal.*

#### SSS (Side-Side-Side) Similarity
If the three pairs of corresponding sides are proportional:
AB/DE = BC/EF = CA/FD ‚Üí ‚Ė≥ABC ~ ‚Ė≥DEF

#### SAS (Side-Angle-Side) Similarity
If one pair of corresponding angles is equal AND the sides including those angles are proportional:
AB/DE = AC/DF and ‚ą†A = ‚ą†D ‚Üí ‚Ė≥ABC ~ ‚Ė≥DEF

---

### 4. Ratio of Areas of Similar Triangles

$$\frac{\text{Area of }\triangle ABC}{\text{Area of }\triangle DEF} = \left(\frac{AB}{DE}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{BC}{EF}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{CA}{FD}\right)^2$$

**Key point:** The ratio of areas of similar triangles = **square of the ratio of corresponding sides** (NOT the ratio itself!)

---

### 5. Pythagoras Theorem

**Statement:** In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse = sum of squares of the other two sides.

If ‚ą†B = 90¬į in ‚Ė≥ABC:
$$AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2$$

**Converse (equally important!):** If in a triangle, the square of one side equals the sum of squares of the other two sides, then the angle opposite the first side is 90¬į.

**Proof of Pythagoras (using similarity ‚ÄĒ must know for board):**
- Draw BD ‚ä• AC where ‚ą†B = 90¬į in ‚Ė≥ABC
- ‚Ė≥ADB ~ ‚Ė≥ABC (AA: ‚ą†A common, ‚ą†ADB = ‚ą†ABC = 90¬į) ‚Üí AD/AB = AB/AC ‚Üí AB¬≤ = AD √ó AC
- ‚Ė≥BDC ~ ‚Ė≥ABC (AA: ‚ą†C common, ‚ą†BDC = ‚ą†ABC = 90¬į) ‚Üí DC/BC = BC/AC ‚Üí BC¬≤ = DC √ó AC
- Adding: AB¬≤ + BC¬≤ = AC(AD + DC) = AC √ó AC = AC¬≤ ‚Ė°

## Solved Examples

### Example 1 ‚ÄĒ BPT
**Q:** In ‚Ė≥ABC, DE ‚ą• BC. If AD = 4 cm, DB = 3 cm, AE = 6 cm, find EC.

By BPT: AD/DB = AE/EC
4/3 = 6/EC ‚Üí EC = **4.5 cm**

### Example 2 ‚ÄĒ Similarity (AA)
**Q:** In ‚Ė≥ABC, ‚ą†A = 60¬į, ‚ą†B = 80¬į. In ‚Ė≥PQR, ‚ą†Q = 80¬į, ‚ą†R = 40¬į. Are they similar?

‚Ė≥ABC: ‚ą†A = 60¬į, ‚ą†B = 80¬į, ‚ą†C = 40¬į
‚Ė≥PQR: ‚ą†P = 60¬į, ‚ą†Q = 80¬į, ‚ą†R = 40¬į
All three angles match ‚Üí **‚Ė≥ABC ~ ‚Ė≥PQR** (by AAA/AA)

### Example 3 ‚ÄĒ Areas
**Q:** Areas of two similar triangles are 81 cm¬≤ and 49 cm¬≤. If altitude of first = 4.5 cm, find altitude of second.

(h‚āĀ/h‚āā)¬≤ = 81/49 ‚Üí h‚āĀ/h‚āā = 9/7 ‚Üí 4.5/h‚āā = 9/7 ‚Üí h‚āā = **3.5 cm**

## Revision Notes

```
BPT: DE ‚ą• BC ‚Üí AD/DB = AE/EC

Similarity Criteria:
  AA  ‚Üí Two angles equal
  SSS ‚Üí Three sides proportional
  SAS ‚Üí One angle equal + including sides proportional

Area Ratio = (Side Ratio)¬≤

Pythagoras Theorem:
  ‚ą†B = 90¬į ‚Üí AC¬≤ = AB¬≤ + BC¬≤
  Converse: if AC¬≤ = AB¬≤ + BC¬≤, then ‚ą†B = 90¬į

Common Pythagorean Triplets: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (7,24,25)
```

**Common Mistakes:**
‚ĚĆ Ratio of areas = ratio of sides (wrong! it's the SQUARE of the ratio)
‚ĚĆ Writing SSS similarity condition wrong (must be PROPORTIONAL, not equal)
‚ĚĆ Not using the converse of BPT when proving lines are parallel
