# Data Interpretation

## Why This Chapter Matters
DI is the highest-weightage topic in Banking exams — 15-20 marks in every paper. The key skill is speed and accuracy in reading tables, bar graphs, pie charts, and line graphs. Practice reading and calculating quickly.

## Types of DI

### 1. Tabular DI
Data in rows and columns. Read carefully which row and column the question asks about.
Common questions: percentage, ratio, difference, increase/decrease, average.

### 2. Bar Graph
Compare values across categories. Read the scale carefully.
Common questions: which has highest/lowest, % increase from one year to next, ratio between two bars.

### 3. Pie Chart
Shows parts of a whole as % or degrees.
Total = 100% or 360 degrees.
Value = (percentage/100) x total value.
Common questions: value of a sector, ratio between two sectors, % more than another.

### 4. Line Graph
Shows trends over time. Good for identifying increase/decrease.
Rate of change = change in value / change in time.

### 5. Mixed Graph
Two types of graphs together (bar + line). Read both carefully.

## Key Calculation Techniques

### Percentage Calculation Shortcuts
To find x% of y: if x=10%, divide y by 10. If x=5%, divide by 20. If x=25%, divide by 4.
Build up: 17.5% = 10% + 5% + 2.5% = y/10 + y/20 + y/40.

### Ratio Comparison
To compare a/b and c/d: cross multiply. If ad > bc: a/b > c/d.

### Approximation
In DI, always look if you can approximate. If answer choices are far apart, rough calculation suffices.

## Practice Strategy

**Step 1:** Read the question carefully BEFORE looking at data.
**Step 2:** Identify which part of the data is needed.
**Step 3:** Calculate ONLY what is needed (don't calculate everything).
**Step 4:** Use approximation when answer choices allow it.
**Step 5:** Eliminate wrong options quickly.

## Sample DI Questions

Given table: Sales (units) for 5 companies A,B,C,D,E in 2021 and 2022.
A: 500, 600 | B: 400, 350 | C: 300, 450 | D: 600, 720 | E: 200, 300

**Q1:** Which company had highest % growth from 2021 to 2022?
A: 20% | B: -12.5% | C: 50% | D: 20% | E: 50%
C and E both 50% — check exact: C: 150/300=50%, E: 100/200=50%. Both same. (check question — usually there's a clear winner).

**Q2:** What is the ratio of total 2021 to total 2022 sales?
2021 total = 2000. 2022 total = 2420. Ratio = 2000:2420 = 100:121.

**Q3:** Average sales of all companies in 2022?
2420/5 = 484 units.

## Revision Notes
```
PIE CHART:
Value = (sector%/100) x total
Angle = (sector%/100) x 360

BAR GRAPH: Compare heights for relative values.

% CHANGE = (New-Old)/Old x 100

RATIO comparison: a:b vs c:d -> ad vs bc (cross multiply)

SHORTCUTS:
10% of x = x/10
5% of x = x/20
25% of x = x/4
33.33% of x = x/3
```
